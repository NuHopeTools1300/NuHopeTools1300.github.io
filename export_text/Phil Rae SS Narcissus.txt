for the record.... the main "3 foot" model was a collaberation between bower, me, john packenham, and the late simon deering (i think)...maybe others. i became invloved at the detailing stage of which i did most, and then ALL the painting and weathering.

bower was primarilly responsible for the "design" of the front end to compliment the full size back desighned by the art dept from the single ron cobb painting.

i was given free reign to devise the detailng "look" n weathering. 

the bigger forced perspective front end was entirely my work. i had to reproduce the detailing i did on the main filming model at 2x size. i wish they  (Allder) had told me that in advance cos it was a right royal **** to do that, but it worked. with close scrutiny it is easy to tell the two models apart, but NOT obvious on screen! i was very relieved it worked out. the larger model was done in only about 3 days, and the truth is that, i was working on it right up to the point of shooting the full face on shot, so in fact it never really got finished,which accounts for some of the discrepancies you rivet counters can study and see today. 

the main model disappeared at an exhibit in la we heard....never to be seen again, but it is surely still around.

the interior i made (4 hours maybe) was used, filmed but then discarded and can only be seen in outtakes. i saved that and it is now inside the bower repro at the french museum.

the famous pic of me with a drill workijng on the fp verion is because allder used tv monitors for the live action insert into the windows. of course i knew nothing of this until the model was complete. allder just turned up with one and of course it wouldn.t fit, so there i am in the pic drilling holes  to allow for the coners of the tvs, then i made wiggeted shields to disguse them sticking through. details that is not on the main filming model

i'm sure i can tell more but rite now my head hurts! LOL!
