Its been awaiting my attention for a bit now, so thought id start tinkering away at sub assemblies i need to improve, before i actually assemble her.

Going for the obvious, pretty orange spot variant, maybe not going to the lengths Julien has, but then my OCD has already kicked in, so I'm already tearing into some Renaults, Ferraris and Morser Karls.

Had a problem drilling out the wing hinges, two of them cracked due to a lot of resin in the hole, and having to ream quite a hole, but, these were replaced with acrylic tubing, which actually makes the wing actuation, a lot more true, so no biggie.

Not a great deal to show, as it will be shaving and replacing on all subs, till i get to putting some of it together, but its all good fun, and I'm looking forward to the challenge, its a great kit, that can be built from the box, but then, we don't really do that round these parts do we :lol.

Ok a small update here. Ive scratch built the rear cockpit section a bit closer to the prop. Ive opted not to cut anymore of the shuttle cone, lens distortion in the pics was driving me nuts, so i thought its best left alone.

Here we have a new styrene centre hub, ABS inner spinner, and styrene rear section, the weight saving alone is astonishing when compared to the resin parts, its way, way lighter. The entire cockpit section spins freely on a scratch built bearing hub, containing two 9mm Tamiya bearings, on a 5mm threaded axle. I planned for the whole thing to gyroscope like the Hasbro toy TBH lol, which works flawlessly when just rotating the outer hub, but adding the rear cone, and canopy hampered the action a little, if i get to figure it out to work once all assembled, ill stick a video up.

Still need to fill the outer rib joins, and add all the donors, but its getting there, and I'm a happy bunny thus far!

lee

Main wing in basic form added. now i can start to assemble whats here, finalise any filling, scribing, rivet drilling and clean up, and have some killer fun with OE donor parts! main wing was maybe the hardest part thus far, so much to keep track of as it builds, all parts were lay flat on glass, heated to be perfectly flat, this helps them marry up nicely, but there are still areas to keep an eye on. The centre recess in the main wing centre, this area is tricky, as it sits lower, than the edge of the surrounding wing surface, nothing that can be helped in casting such a complicated chunk of parts, but go easy, and dry fit, dry fit, and dry fit, test everything over and over till it all meets and marries nicely and as flush as can be, take huge care, NOT to sand too much wing join surface.

I think the hardest part of the main wing are the leading edges, pretty sure id lost some wing contour, so my leading edges were a pain to get even close, I've done my best here, and its not too bad, bit i still have a slight wave after a LOT of blending and opening surface bubbles, by the time its painted, or, i pass on into the afterlife, no one will ever know LOL.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Engines now primed. The boosters sit on a scratch built base, this is easier to light, and just my preference over the kit supplied part. Each booster has been cored out so they weigh very little and are now easy to run the lighting through, the entire unit is removable so i can fit all the electrics as and when in a modular fashion.

A tip on the boosters, don't flat sand them, its better to razor saw them away from the platen, and core out, this way, when you do flat sand the out end, you can get a good clean flat edge to join the exhaust. Also a good tip, once you have achieved a flat edge at the exhaust end, you may find your engines still sit pretty wonky, i sat mine each on my Proxxon mini drill press, and ran a grind disc across the tops (the end that joins to your baseplate) this ensures a much more aligned solution!
