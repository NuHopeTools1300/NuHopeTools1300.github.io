Hi all from the UK!, My name is Simon. I work at UCL now but for thirty years I was a prop modeller in films and tv. From captain america the first avenger, to senior propmaker on Episode VII , to 'running weapons and hand props' on the Acolyte. check out my IMDB here:

			https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0331517/
		

It was great fun and I made a lot of cool stuff but just after we wrapped on 'F1' the writers strike hit and everything closed  so I found work at a university in London and liked it so much I never went back. Nobody leaves propmaking at that level, they carry on until they drop dead. I always thought i` would keep going until I died but life is full of surprises! Less cool, less money but... I get to play with 3D printers and laser cutters all day, no 3 hrs in the car every day, take my son to school , play in a proper rock band and get home at 6pm instead of 7.45! There is more to life than money.

I have always loved this design and there are no decent kits of it that I can find. So the past few weeks I've been drawing it up, bit of a lunchtime project, and now I've printed it so here are some pictures. A good friend of mine has one of the copies made of the original model and I did photograph that in 2011 but god knows where those pictures are. It was around 23 inches long i remember that so so is the larger one of these. It was also rough as f%^ but then the original was probably carved out of Jelotong!! It is proportionally correct but the detais I have added myself because I think they look cool and I don't really care if they are screen perfect. To me, if it looks right and captures the essence of the ship, thats good enough. I did some figures too. Of course the interior is completely made up . Everything is made of booleaned shapes in Rhino7 so its all a bit basic. I do have Zbrush but its so horrible to use so blocky Bulgey figures it is!

It is so elegant and very much of its time. Its almost like two saturn V's on a P38 Lightning. McQuarrie was the GOAT.
