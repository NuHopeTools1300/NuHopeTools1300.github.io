Hi All!

Unfortunately, the problem that we run into is that even though you update your information in your profile, for some reason, it does not always update the information in the database for previous orders. . . . So if there is anyone who would like to inquire about their status, please email me at Bryan@efxcllectibles.com

If you are not in the first allocation, the second shipment is scheduled to be ready to ship from Asia to the US around January 23

Thanks!

Yes, I got it on Monday.

During assembly I could not get a wing on the right wing arm of the body. I tried for over an hour and even swapped the wings trying both sides and using each bolt. Still couldn't get the bolt to hand thread onto the right side.

It just wouldn't go in straight.

So I e-mailed Bryan and he got back to me the same day with some instructions on how to get the wing on.

Basically, the factory spray painted the metal mounting points that the wings bolt onto and they were not supposed to. Bryan instructed me to sand the paint off the metal mounting point which i did with fine grit sand paper. I then wiped it with a paper towel and methyl hydrate to remove any dust and left over residue. I then mounted a wing onto the left side, which went on very easily. Then i laid the Tie flat on it's left wing on the carpet and slid the right wing on the right mounting point but not all the way down, just enough to get the hex bolt on by hand. Once I had it hand threaded a couple revolutions I made sure the notch in the wing and the mounting point were lined up. I then proceeding to tighten it with the Hex wrench, and it finally went on. It still didn't go on as easily as the other side but at least I got it on...whew!

I also sanded the outer edges of the caps that cover the bolts slightly with fine grit sandpaper. They seemed very tight to put on and I thought that they were too tight and may be too difficult to get off again later.

Other than the little wing hiccup everything else was perfect and Brayn was very helpful in resolving the problem. Funny how a little extra paint threw the whole mounting point out of whack.

Anyway, I am very happy with this model, and like you mentioned Allan it does have the feel of a real studio scale filming model. I actually found it more impressive in the flesh than in pictures. Definitely a very well put together model and worth the 2 plus year wait.

I'll try and post up some pics later.

Thanks Bryan and all those who worked on this project and for taking their time to get it right!

You're also comparing what is essentially a master pattern in the eFX (albeit from scanned kit parts) to a casting of the TIE - resin shrinkage was much worse back in the '70s, assembly was far from uniform, models were roughly handled in the act of filming, parts popped off and were glued back on in different locations, etc.  

Look at the Snowspeeder.  Most of the real world kit parts bear little resemblance to what is decorating the surviving models.  

It would be extremely difficult to "gener-age" (generation age via molding and casting) a modern model to look exactly like the casting used for an ILM filming prop.
