Well, this kit is deceptively challenging, but in a good way - and I have a few words of advice to help every one else build theirs.

Rear engine ports - I think if I had mastered this kit, I would have made a "cap" that would have finished off the ends of the engine ports. I had to do a lot of time consuming puttying and sanding, and I actually shaved off some of the detail and replaced with strips of styrene to make it sharper. I recommend doing this to save time. Things line up pretty well - this is a great kit.

[IMAGE: IMGP7046.jpg]

Be careful here too - a few air bubbles popped up when I wnet digging - no biggie, and they are filled now.

[IMAGE: IMGP7047.jpg]

The ends of the wings are also a little tricksy. One of the two has the correct angle to fit at the right curve. The other would point too far downward, so I used an angle attachment on my small belt sander to make the part match. took 5 seconds - but be careful that you get these ends lining up correctly. They meet along the top of the top curve, but by the time you get to the trailing edge of the part, it's sitting below the main body's bottom hull. Photos of the real deal support this though, so it's correct - the model is from a real one, after all.

[IMAGE: IMGP7049.jpg]

The bottom is easy.

[IMAGE: IMGP7051.jpg]

Lights look rad.

[IMAGE: IMGP7062.jpg]

SO the only "real" problem on the kit is the canopy. All pics I've seen of this KIT hide the fact that the canopy has a gap in the area where it meets the body. I've talked to Jesse, and he's aware of it. All you have to do is add some mass to the back louver, mainly at the bottom. Here's a before shot, an after shot, and a comparison between the part as you get it out-of-the-box, and how much more I added to it.

[IMAGE: IMGP7055.jpg]

[IMAGE: IMGP7063.jpg]

[IMAGE: IMGP7057.jpg]

Great kit - just took a few days longer with the putty than I thought it would. Still highly recommended. Next up? Paint.

Hey all....sorry I haven't been around much...work keeps getting in the way of fun.

As for the Raider...The canopy is from an original, and is a little warped..it's fixable, but not perfect.  I think, similar to the Viper, the Raider as a whole wasn't perfect.  If you look at pictures of the studio models they had various inaccuracies including glaring symetry errors.  Mycroft and Jim fixed a lot of those, and didn't fix others.  As always, its an issue of whether you make it perfect (and thus perfectly WRONG) or make it right, and make it look imperfect.  (Did I get that right?.?.)

I was also under the impression that the wingtips were original...I thought I read that (regarding the ships recreation), and I thought the seller told me that...but I'm not positive.  My apologies if I was wrong there.

Mycroft did generously send us lots of information to make a 2nd generation Raider, or add on parts to the current one.  Right now my partner and I are deciding what to do.  Interest in the kit has not been high, and it's expensive to do.  We have considered making a new version, which would be a lot more time consuming, but would allow Mycrofts work to be finished as it would have been (had he been able to...long story there) or simply providing some additional add on parts.  Some of these add ons are already included in the kit...just little pieces that were not there in the first version.  I suspect, we will end up doing both...making a new version and offering upgrade parts to people who already got a kit.  All that said, I have not commented on this till now because nothing is set in stone.  Both my partner and I are swamped right now, and we have discussed discontinuing the Raider indefinitly because of the time and costs involved.  I don't want to get to high on the radar either.  I know a lot of people will say "Ill wait until the next version" and I want to be clear that may never happen.  I just don't know.  I have the best of intentions, but like many of you have a million other projects lined up that I also want to do someday.  I'd hate to promise somethign and never do it, so it was better to simply offer what we had to people who didn't get it.  If we ever do another version Ill make sure we do our best to make it available as an upgrade or cheap update to people who already bought the first version.  In some ways, I wonder if we will ever be "done" with the Raider and other projects...there is so little information out there that it seems whenever I think it's perfect I find out about another little difference Apogee did on one, or another, or at one time.

So...to conclude...(sorry for the long note. )

Jason...looks awesome...can't wait to see more.

Current version - available now but will be limited and run may end soon. (PM or email me if interested to jestefarean@comcast.net)

New version - no guarentees here.

Mycroft - thanks for all the help and information.  Your the best....
