Hey all! About 10 years ago, I started to ID the Endor Moon Bunker Generators as seen in ROTJ. Many of the parts were obvious, but some were complete mysteries to me. Fast forward to 2020 and I finally found all the components and donor kits! None of this is 3D printed, all scratch-built. From then until now, I've (very) slowly made progress on molding each assembly.

Well, it's now official, I will be releasing an accurate studio scale kit of the generators very soon.

[IMAGE: 323425879_539846084872444_7685605032028485862_n.jpg]

						Estreluna Models & Miniatures
					

Estreluna Models & Miniatures. 132 likes. Scratch-built models and kits.

[IMAGE: ay1hV6OlegS.ico]

					www.facebook.com
				

While accurate, please keep in mind that I had to take artistic liberty with the large cup assembly. To my knowledge, the original cups from that era no longer exist. And believe me, I now have a plastic cup collection! 
		
		
	

	

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/1f603-png.1655390/]

The kit will come with two complete towers (20 parts in total), it will be hollow cast, and it will include all the base material as seen in the Archives today (acrylic, hardwood, and tubing stock).

I even built a tower in Rhino so that I could calculate the needed casting material and to draw my instructions. I hope you enjoy the update!

P.S. The two towers in the last pic are made from rejected castings, so you will see bubbles and blemishes on these.

- Joe

The hunting and discovery have definitely been satisfying, but a chore as well, haha! Concerning just the ID's, all (not including the Saturn V) were found without kit scans, unfortunately. 

Many times, I'd get a hunch and would have to dig through eBay, or, search the internet until I found pics that would lead to other clues. There were hundreds of after work hours just surfing away and looking at the same 21 reference photos (many thanks to whoever posted those way back in the day). But yeah, looking back, it was fun in it's own weird way.

Hi all! I've got a long overdue update. It took me a while to get the base coats down, largely due to chasing vintage Floquil paints to try and get something as close to what ILM used as possible. Big thanks to Guy Cowen who offered some really good advice for the orange color and green rail color (not pictured). I ended up deviating a bit, but his guidance was much appreciated. I quickly learned that color matching this particular colors was harder than it looked. Regardless, I found something that makes me feel good about the build. Weathering and final assembly are up next.

Joe
