sigh - this is pretty much like asking which anh darth vader helmet is the most accurate... it's a really difficult question to answer 

As a fellow collector - my take on it is the v3 is very very accurate. From what I've seen from the eFX x-wing it's also very accurate.

I"m sure you can read the hundreds of posts of how the eFX and v3 have studied tons of references to make theirs as accurate as possible. 

Both are just slightly different, and if you can't get both and need to make a choice I suggest you consider the following that I think I know about the models. 

the eFX has metal wings - that may not be good or bad but personally I think that's cool. It's not accurate in terms of the original studio model, but the wings wont sag like the old icons ss model. The v3 wings are made really nicely too - so no worries about sagging. I personally just like metal. 

the eFX has an armature allowing it to move its wings - another plus unless you bought the metal armature built for the v3 here on the RPF> 

the eFX has glass (plastic) built into the canopy - the studio models apparently had glass built in as well - many people here don't like glass in the canopy it's really your preference. 

the eFX has lights too - put together with the armature - the electronics - the paint job and it's worth the price. 

The eFX will have a factory paint job - as good as these paint jobs can be there will most likely be some overspray and some inaccuracies in terms of little splatters here and there. It again depends on just how accurate you want your x-wing to be. There are some amazing paint jobs that have been shown here of with the v2 and v3 salzo and the paint job really makes or breaks a model. From a day to day basis, when looking at a x-wing, I personally am not looking to see if every panel line is scribed correctly, or every blast mark is the correct size.... I want to be able to look over and see a model and not a toy. Of course, if you search around you'll find that really really good reference pics of the x-wing are also something that most people on this board (and the world) lack. Over time, they may surface making a whole new run of even more accurate x-wing paint jobs. 

As time goes on - there will be more and more accurate models available, the eFX is not the end of the x-wing line, nor is the v3. Back in the day some people thought the icons x-wing was the most accurate. I wouldn't be surprised if in a few years they came up with an even more "cool" x-wing that could open and close its wings automatically with a touch of a button. 

Without anyone actually having the eFX x-wing, and no one having the ability to study the model up close it's hard to say if they got every little greeblie right. 

Again, it all comes down to personal preference. At this point, I don't think you can go wrong with either. You always have the opportunity to repaint the eFX if you so desire. You could always add the things that are missing from the v3 (like canopy glass) onto your v3. 

Ultimate and "most accurate" are unfortunately phrases that are thrown around too much (I throw it around a lot too). But I have come to realize that what we're really saying is "ultimate for now" or "most accurate for now" 

The Ultimate x-wing would have been the exact one they used for filming - but they blew that one up so there's no hope of actually getting that one back. 

just be happy with your purchase knowing that both are rare - both have very limited runs - and you're lucky to have one at all - most people outside this board have no clue that such things even exist.

The V3 is a "generic" kit in that the body is based more on Red 3 than anything else. The wings are based on the larger thicker pyro wing, not the hero wings, which also likely vary on every studio model as well. Every thing else is kit parts or are scaled to the formentioned parts. Also it has non specific detailing in other parts as it is a kit that would need to be individualy detailed by builders to fit their desired build. Some people may happy with the generic details and a paint job that is close to the Red # they are building, others may use it as a starting point and add the level of detailing they want.

It is shorter than the EFX (by how long i do not know at this time) but could still be considered "correct" depending on which body you are comparing it to, I am sure that the EFX is also correct using the same reasoning, and is superior for people looking for Red 5 concering the detailing.  Again the V3 is generic...and no longer available, so IMO if you get a chance at an EFX, pick that up....I have my deposit down....

In the Red 5 stakes, the V3 does have one advantage in that the cockpit is ready cast into the hull, as on all ILM 1/24 X-Wings. This definitely gives more of an ILM feel to the mating of canopy base to fuselage.

But then the EFX hits back with lovely very narrow gauge rings circling the fan things on the laser guns, which are a bit thick on the V3... but anyway it's all academic since the kit is no longer available.

Also, only the most superficial paintjob is possible with these factory replicas; a convincing coordination of tonal and hue values is always somewhat lacking. As is, the EFX carries only a bare approximation to a studio paintjob. For an authentic look, a repaint by a master hand is required (as with the MR Falcon).
