The policy of the museum is not to allow photography.  If you look at the posts on http://www2.ezbbs.net/36/starwars_model/ (you'll have to use a translation service like http://babelfish.altavista.com/ if you don't speak Japanese), you'll see that people are managing to get photographs, but they're having to hide their cameras in their coats and so on. There are two x-wing displays, to my recollection--I remember a small one, and there was certainly a big one--though I could be confusing the small one with another model in my memory.  I was there last weekend.  The big Falcon and one smaller Falcon are there.  

Miniaturizer Ray is correct in that Kyoto National Museum does not allow pictures.  I sneaked a photo or two for a board member when I  went, and I may be able to do so if I go again, which I may in the coming week.  No guarantees, of course.

The big Falcon is a real challenge to photo since it's the big display (after the Star Destroyer) right at the entrance, swarming with staff members.  I consider it a challenge.
