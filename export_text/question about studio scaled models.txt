My understanding is the model should be the same size as the miniature which was used for filming.  

...For CG vehicles, that can get interesting.  I've seen (on the Stargate DVD collections) behind-the-scenes materials showing foam-board mock-ups of the Goa'uld pyramid ships.  Specifically, Peter DeLuise was zooming one around describing a scene, and it was shedding sections as he kept talking.  But that was a mock-up, not a filming model.

Studio Scale is the same size as the size of the shooting minature.  It also implies built with the same detailing and parts (this gets iffy when sectiosn are scratchbuilt due to the lack of original parts)

I don't think that the commnity has come to a concenus when the term is applied to a CG model.  However - in many cases a physical model is built and scanned to get the textures and shapes for the CGI model used on film.  I've seem some people consider that physical model the "studio Scale" and others argue that since it never saw real screen time it doesn't count... 

So in short your question on a CGI model is not able to be answerd definitively.  Particularly if it ONLY existed as bits and bytes.  in some ways its a freeing exercise...  as you can build it in whatever scale suites your fancy - but you may not be able to claim "studio scale" just "screen accurate"...  I hope that makes sense.

I hope that helps a little bit...
