I've got a question that has been bugging me for a bit. I am curious as to if anyone has come up with a definitive list of the different sizes of shuttle studio models used for the James Bond film Moonraker?

I know they had some big ones for the closeups of the orbiters, as well as the launch configuration shuttles. I know that Airfix shuttles were docked to the Drax orbiting complex, but I don't quite know the size and or scale of the salt shuttle (the one that dropped salt out the back to simulate rocket exhaust contrails) or the one on the rail track that popped the SRBs off.

Reason I ask is I've started work on a Monogram 1/72 Shuttle with ET and SRBs that I am building as Moonraker 6. I recall PHArchivist a few years back built a 1/72 orbiter as a Moonraker for the studio scale forum as the details of the sizes of studio models used were just sketchy enough to justify it. But I figure there might be better data out there today and I was wondering if I could justify it as studio scale or should I just keep it in General Modeling? Its no biggie eitherway, but my intent is not to just slap Moonraker decals on an OOB Monogram stack and call it even as I am modifying my model with unique Moonraker features, such as the smooth ET intertank (looks like an oversized Airfix tank), no foam texture on the tank and modified SRBs to take away some of their realistic appearance. The orbiter itself will likely have some details that more closely match the studio model as well in spots (I haven't quite decided there).

Thanks in advance.
