Hi guys,  got this MR Y wing from our own S. Neisen and he had already removed the piping on the dorsal of the ship before he decided to sell it.    I wanted to model the Y wing with the "T" in the piping that is primarily copper tubing.    So I set to work repriming and repainting the base colors first,  added the new pipe work and then assembled and did the final color scheme and weathering this morning.  I just finished her and snapped a few pics to share.  Peace,

Dave

Yeah,  the weathering job MR did on the Y is what has put me off of buying one all this time........until now.  I finally decided to bite the bullet,  get an airbrush and fix it.  So I got the darkly weathered MR Y and tried my hand at learning to airbrush.  With a dual action brush in your hands,  anyone could learn to do a fine job.  I'm not calling what I did a fine job,  but it's better than what MR's folks did.  They always lay down on the black weathering apparently.  A very little, goes a long long way.  

Dave
