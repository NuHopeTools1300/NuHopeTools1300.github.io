Here she is all glued up.

[IMAGE: _DSC1477_resize.jpg]

I'm pouring in some Smooth Cast and kinda rotocasting the inside of the hull hoping this might strenghten up the whole thing.

[IMAGE: _DSC1479_resize.jpg]

NOTE: after the Smooth Cast had hardened I noticed it was very easy to peel it off the hull. So if you are gonna do this make sure you use some kind of primer so the Smooth Cast (or something else) really sticks to the hull.

But in the end the smooth cast did the job.

Next up: trimming, filling, sanding and filling and sanding and filling.............:wacko

Post #51  (forum post #1864670)
Author    : almar bouwman
Date      : Sep 4, 2011 at 8:21 PM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

I first used Tamiya chrome silver X-11. Then brushed on some latex rubber here and there. Once dry I spray painted the entire ship matt black (XF-1). I then used Tamiya sky grey (XF-19) for the main color. After that I washed the entire ship and used some fine steel wool to remove some of the wash (and latex rubber) and exposing the chrome silver layer. But it's hard to see in these pics because I used the wrong ISO settings on my camera, too much grain now :wacko.
