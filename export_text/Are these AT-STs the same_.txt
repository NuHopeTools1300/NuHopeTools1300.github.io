Yes you are right, sorry for the confusion they are not the same in the pics.

The euro AT-ST was used for front shot while the Asian was used for back shots.

The back of the euro ATST is a raw casting while the Asian has more details. The euro ATST has the rear "232 fender" part (which is was not 232 parts on the ilm models but scratchbuilt, the bandai is wrong here I think they took the nice-n as ref which uses 232 parts, this is wrong) cut to allow clearance for the mount. The Asian was mounted from the side and the front. 

The euro ATST has extra kitparts on the underside of the head while the Asian has a raw castings.

Casting details are mounted differently on the legs from a version to another as well as the flakvierling 38 parts. 

There are also some other small differences in the way they were assembled and detailed (side guns, piping under the head etc...)

Well the euro ATST has been displayed in Europe with the black acrylic stand while the Asian one was displayed on the wooden stand. However the euro ATST has been seen at least once on the wooden stand in the archives 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
)

But it's easy to figure which one is which, the Asian ATST has a full "232" butt while the euro at-st has it cut in half with the center piece missing. That's why there are almost no pictures of the back of the euro AT-ST. The back of the head of the asian one also have details than the euro ATST doesnt have like some extra piping and Gepard small parts. 

The euro ATST also has a small engraved circle in the top center of the front armor plate of the head. The Asian does not.
