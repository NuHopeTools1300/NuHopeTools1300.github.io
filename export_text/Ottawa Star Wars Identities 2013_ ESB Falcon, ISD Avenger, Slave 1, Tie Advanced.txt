This past summer I went to see the Star Wars Identities in Ottawa and took a few pictures of the models there. I was able to light the models at the museum with a LED day for 1 ½ days before the staff told me to turn off the LED. However, by then I had taken the majority of my pictures. I used a Nikon D7100 camera with a Nikon 18-300mm f/3.5-5.6G AF-S DX Nikkor lens. The camera automatically adjusted for lens distortion. I used RAW mode which gives an image that is 6000x4000 pixels and is approximately 28 Mb. I used Nikon’s Capture NX 2 to do a basic conversion of the RAW file to a jpeg and applied the Auto level setting to each file, which produced a manageable file size under 4 Mb.

  I uploaded the files to my Microsoft Onedrive account (formally skydrive). It’s free to access the pictures. However, if you don’t have an account I believe you’re limited to downloading a maximum of 300 Mb at one time. It’s free to set up an account with them.

  Use the following link to access the files: https://onedrive.live.com/#cid=B9FE0AEBDC25FDBC&id=B9FE0AEBDC25FDBC!109

  You can find the following:

  Millennium Falcon ESB 3 foot (1127 pictures)

Millennium Falcon ESB 3 foot orthographic views (96 pictures)

  Slave 1 (224 pictures)

  ISD Avenger (7000 pictures)

  Tie Advanced (131 pictures)

  The Y-wing was not present; it was damaged after being at the Montreal exhibit.

  Feel free to share these pictures, but no selling for profit and please don't post them to other websites without my permission.

  Steven
