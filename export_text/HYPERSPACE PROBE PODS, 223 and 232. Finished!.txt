Click to expand...

Thank you, especially for your offer to help with parts! Still needs weathering but here's how I painted them so far.

Since years back I'm only using waterbed acrylics for health reasons. I Prime pretty much everything these days with "Ultimate primer". I have white and black which I then mix to a base tone close to the finale based coat. Awesome product! I guess MIG One shot primer is kind of the same thing if you can't find "Ultimates" products.

The Pods where base coated with my own mix for "Imperial ESB blue/grey". I've been using the same mix for most of my imperial ESB stuff, don't know if it's right or not but looks good to my eye. In the past I used Tamiyas TS-32 Haze Grey for ANH TIE's but I hate the smell. These days I just add 20 drops of Tamiya XF-8 Flat Blue (from a Tamiya mixing spoon/stick) to full jar of Tamiyas XF-82 Ocean Grey 2 (RAF). I would love to try Archive X's Imperial grey but it's not readily available in Sweden and the added import fees these days are just crazy. Does anyone know if Archives Imperial grey mimics the ANH tone or ESB? To me the ESB/ROTJ Tie Color always seemed different?

The dark grey areas where just the same mix with some added Tamiya Flat Black. On the 223 I also picked out the small Plastruct lamps / Flackvierling details with really light grey. Haven't seen this replicated before but that's really what I see under all that soot.

You're right about the markings, I just masked them with cut stripes of Tamiyas masking tape. Dark paint, tape then base coat on top, remove tape.... Scaling it right on the 232 turned out a hassle, got the strips way to big since I had my ref-material zoomed in to close. Stripes that measured 2.5 mm seemed to work good for the 232. Same stripe just cut into different lengths. Finally som pre-shading was done towards the bottoms with Tamiyas XF-86 Rubber Black. One of my favourite weathering colors. Darker then grey, lighter then black.. Just right. As I said, though still more weathering before I call them done.

Hope this helps.

Yes, I really tried to capture all the cool features on the 223 that's been revealed by the Japanese Chronicle book. The buckets clearly sits on some round wheel looking parts. I just kept trying out tank wheels until I found one that fitted and looked right. Not claiming to have found the original part but I think I ended up using the guiding wheels from Tamiyas 1:35 Chieftain. They fitted snug with only some light sanding at the bottoms where they meet the 8-Rad brackets. 

If you look carefully you can also see that I used Plastruct T-track for the front trims, also visible in the Chronicle pictures as well as the U-Shaped brackets under the Leichter hoods. Wasn't able to ID them so I ended up designing some that my friend printed out. If anybody is interested in getting a set just let me know. I also have the designs for the Panter threads, Morser Karl bars (the pesky ones you need 10 of) as well as the Panzer III covers if somebody needs them.
