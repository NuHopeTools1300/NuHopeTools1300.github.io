As if I didn't have enough projects going on in me head...

I was recently made aware of a trove of new photos of the U.S.S. Jupp kitbash from DS9, made available by the model builder Adam Buckner himself, that showed a lot of previously unseen detail on the filming model, and people have been able to identify more of the pieces from various other model kits that were used in the kitbash. So, I got the bug to once again try to build a Jupp. However, unlike the filming model, which sported the famous "war paint" paint job of the Dominion War kitbashes, I'm going to build a "factory-fresh" Jupp. I may at some point in the future build another Jupp in proper war paint, but for the time being, I don't feel like spending the time, effort and money trying to match all the colors on the original, especially seeing as how a lot of the colors may not be available anymore, as the Model Master line of paints has been discontinued for some years now. The original model also had TNG-era Starfleet arrowheads on it, which I will be replacing with VOY-era arrowheads. I will also be giving the ship a proper NCC number, appropriate for a ship built in the DS9/VOY era. The number I settled on was NCC-71964, 7 denoting a very modern ship, and 1964, for Adam Buckner's birth year. The premise I will be working with is below:

U.S.S. JUPP - NCC-71964

Following the end of the Dominion War, Starfleet was in no position to scrap any salvageable ships, as the war had inflicted devastating amounts of damage to the fleet and fleet infrastructure throughout Federation space. Upon the war's end, the Jupp, as well as many other vessels were sent to a dockyard where they were stripped, gutted and completely restored and updated with the latest equipment and software. The ships were rechristened and given proper active duty status and missions. The Jupp is among these saved ships.

SHIP'S EXTERIOR

The exterior of the "new" U.S.S. Jupp will look just like a "new" ship of its era - the same hull detailing as the 1701-A on the saucer and nacelles, with modern Starfleet hull graphics.

SHIP'S INTERIOR

The interior of the "new" U.S.S. Jupp will reflect the quick, yet "proper" restoration/refitting of the ship, meaning all the interior spaces will share a consistent look. For this, and to convey the relative quickness of the restoration/refitting, I have decided that the interior spaces will follow the VOY design and color aesthetic. The corridors will be the VOY corridors, the turbolifts will be the VOY turbolifts, the Mess Hall will be the VOY Mess Hall from the pilot, with the replicator wall, as there would be no Neelix's kitchen. All other interior spaces would conform to the refit Constitution-Class saucer interiors, but adhere to the VOY look and color scheme.

For the Bridge, I'm thinking of the floor plan, ceiling and the CONN/OPS console of the U.S.S. Righteous, from the Star Trek: Borg game, but with VOY-style wall stations, chairs, and completely VOY color scheme, carpet color and materials.

I'm also awaiting a set of custom decals as well as a painting guide and a set of aztec masks for the saucer and nacelles. As with my other projects, this will be somewhat long-term, so updates wont be too regular, but they will come. Stay tuned.

So, to get things started, and to answer Clerval's question, below is a list of model kits you will need to build the Jupp. More kits went into this bash than I thought, as you can see in the attached photos, which were originally posted by Adam Buckner.

For the Enterprise donor kits, you will need the AMT 1/537 kit - either the Star Trek: V or the Star Trek: VI version, because as you can see, you will need the rear hatch piece from the enclosed shuttlecraft, which will be placed on the top of the bridge module, directly aft of the aft windows.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/v-jpg.1978121/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/vi-jpg.1978122/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/juppshuttle-jpg.1978123/]

The next donor kit is obvious - you will need the original Monogram U.S.S. Voyager in 1/677 scale, so you can have the pylons connecting the neck to the warp nacelles.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/voyager-jpg.1978124/]

The last two kits required contribute only one piece each to the build - the first being the Monogram Maquis Ship kit in 1/323 scale, as one of the little greeble doodads is seen on the bottom aft of the pylons/support structure.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/maquis-jpg.1978125/]

Finally, you will need a Revell AH-64 "Apache" Attack Helicopter kit in 1/32 scale, as half of a Hellfire missile is used on the bottom aft of the pylons/support structure, directly in the middle between the Voyager impulse engines, where both pylons join the bottom of the neck.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/2024-02-03-13-43-05-jpg.1978126/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/apache-jpg.1978127/]

Thankfully, if you're planning on building other DS9 Dominion War kitbash ships, both the Maquis ship and the Apache helicopter kits will still come in handy, as parts from the Maquis ship are used on other kitbashes, and parts from the Apache are used on the U.S.S. Bradford kitbash.

I'm sorry to learn of your U.S.S. Yeager splitting already. Yikes!

What I have done so far concerning the Apache part and the VOY pylons is super-glued them (didn't hold), made a styrene plate that fit down into the pylons (didn't hold), a combination of Tamiya Extra Thin cement and super glue (holds until bumped). I'm going to try the Tamiya putty and the extra thin cement next. There just isn't a lot of surface to glue to hold the parts together. Probably use 5 minute epoxy and the putty. The stress from the long length of the nacelles, and the small area of surface on the VOY pylons, and where they connect to the neck, is the main reason for having some kind of armature to keep this model together, so the parts can hang from that.
