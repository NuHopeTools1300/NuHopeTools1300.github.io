I recently started this commissioned Galactica. The idea with this build is to replicate the studio models exterior detailing part for part while revamping the interior to make the model as light weight, durable and user friendly as possible. The body is skinned entirely in .60 styrene over a bass wood skeleton affixed to an aluminum armature.  

Jim C

Re: Battlestar Galactica build - studio scale

Here's some pics from the beginning.

The armature is 1/16" thin walled aluminum pipe tacked together with a 'pulse on pulse' welder that doesn't warp thin aluminum. The lighting will be all LED.  I'm using an incandescent tinted LED fixture for the fiber optics and much whiter LED spotlights for the engines. These LED fixtures are extremely bright and are tinted to give off the same color temperature as halogen bulbs but without putting out any heat, negating the need for any cooling apparatus.

Jim C

Just so it's out there... in case it's been missed:

At no time in any "canonical" information (argue over the definition of that, but I take it to mean the original show, the show scripts, the director's notes, and the novelizations) has it ever been stated that there were only 12 Battlestars. That is a popular concept that started in fan circles and somehow has continued to be perpetuated.

Glen Larson himself stated in an interview that he had never laid that out as part of the mythos of Galactica. Fans have just thought it was a neat idea that every planet would have one Battlestar.

In the original telemovie, a total of five battlestars are seen at the "Peace Conference" (also called the Battle Of Cimtar, or alternately The Destruction).  Background dialogue indicates that those were The Galactica, The Atlantia, The Pacifica, The Triton and The Acropolis.

The Pegasus appeared later in "Living Legend".

The Solaria, The Cerberus, and the Prometheus are all potential Battlestars that appear in the costumer's notes, but were never written into the show.

But there was never a cap on the number of Battlestars.  

I know... I'm a geek with a capital "G". Spreadin' the good word.

I finished up the starboard bay interior & lighting. 

I had initially built a forced perspective interior that looked cool when viewed in person but scrapped that because it didn't really resemble what you see in the studio model.

The new bay insert looks much better and was way easier to build. 

A short film of the landing lights. http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mPqU0-kMkvM

Simplified bay theater

[IMAGE: IMG_0330.jpg]

Fiber optics running lights lit from the flasher circuit board located in the ships main body. The fibers and power leads for the LED's run up inside the rear arm.

I know it took Sean Sides about 3+ years to build his? Mike Oz and Phil Curl have been building theirs in Australia for years as well. At Custom Replicas it took our crew of 3-4 people working 5 days a week, approx 9 months to build our Galactica, and that was because 99% of the information on the parts was available.
