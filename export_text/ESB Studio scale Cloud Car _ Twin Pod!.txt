Kuhn Global, I think this is a very good question.. And I'm really glad You brought it up...becouse this is an important question for me to.

The thing is, I work as an tooth tech, and I cast and mold every day, and I'm kind of anal about it

Re: ESB Studio scale Cloud Car / Twin Pod!

Here is the lowdown on getting the Hulls together, tips to make it easier for everyone and so you have an expectcation of what it needs.  I would say i only spent about 20-30 minutes joining the top and bottom hulls together. This is how i got the hulls together for the pic above.

I am starting to accept orders for this so PM or email me if you are interested.

1. While I was able to get the masters to fit well, of course once cast and pulled on and a bit of resin shrinkage, it needs a small amount of tweaking. The good news is that any small gaps are covered by the styrene strip. I flat sanded the mating surface of each hull, glued a few spots together, i did need to heat an push on a couple of spots to mate up better, likely a result of "pulling" the hull from the mold. Once it was glued up I ran an exacto along the seam to tighten up any small sideways "slop". The good news is because it is pressure cast any small tweaking you need to do will not expose swiss cheese. 

2.Joining the assembled hulls to the midsection was pretty straightforward, i would use solid brass (i think i will include it with the kit) and make sure to sink it deep into the midsection, i would do 2 things different than what i did not mine. 1. I would get the alignment right using the bottom hulls only before putting assembling each top/bottom hull, it's just easier than fumbling around with an assembled hull. 2. on assembly i would put a thin layer of apoxy sculpt between the mid section and the hull, the fit is pretty good but if you put apoxy sculpt on the joint and "squish them" together, and gaps wil be filled and it may be stronger than superglue.... 

thanks

mike
