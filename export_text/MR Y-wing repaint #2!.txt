Brian yes the speckling, its most prevailent of the Red Jammer and thanks to Jamie (darkside72) for putting me in the light on this, for this i used various paint brushes, and indeed toothbrushes to fleck the paint at the model.

A few colors were used to achieve a weathered dirty look, burnt umber and vandyke brown being two of the colors used, i also used water based artist acrylics as i could easliy control the viscosity.

As Andrew said, set the airbrush on very low pressure, and try that too, it works great, this method is a favorite of mine and just looks right.

LOL Brian, get off that games console and get to the workbench lol.

Be nice to see your progress buddy!

I think you should try some of the flecking Andrew, maybe in the neck areas here and there with rusts etc.

I did try out some MIG pigments too with the same approach, i used a tooth brush just a tad wet, and added some MIG rust, then slowly dabbed off the excess on some kitchen towel.

Run your finger over the brush and itll spray onto the model, let it sit for a while, then dab with a soft cloth or pastel sponge, it creates a fine rust spatter that looks pretty cool, i used this method on the Red Jammer as well as Red Leader.

Obviously, youll need to seal the paint if using pigments and pastels though.
