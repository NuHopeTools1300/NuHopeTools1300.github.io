Some Final pics on Post 43 added.

Moving along on the beautiful "Imperious" replica kit of the original BaseStar. I'm at about the half point with this build. Still a long way to go, but thought I'd start sharing as it continues to progress.  This model is so incredibly exacting in EVERY detail.  The engineering and fit is incredibly precise, parts fit better than many styrene kits I've worked on. 

One of the coolest features of this kit is all the 'walls' are pre drilled out not just the acrylic, but already has all the fiber holes pre drilled in!!  You just install the acrylic, then place the resin wall over it, and pefect alignment and holes for the fibers! No drilling or labor required there. Templates are provided for all the fiber locations in the 'inner pies', and those are easily drilled out.

The over 3 years of development in this kit show, and a lot of what you see below are just kit parts placed on for the picture. They don't represent the final positioning, just at a point to show how cool this thing looks as it is coming along.  

I was stunned when putting the black and white wall comparisons together. As you'll see, the details match up precisely.  I've not seen any other attempts at building this kind of detail succeed at that level. Just amazing.

Well, I'll try to get regular updates on this as she moves along. Hoping to finish it up in a couple months.

First two pics show the 'pre-cut' styrene edging that goes around the perimeter.  It's a very efficient, and accurate looking way to recreate that effect as the original had.  First shot is the raw styrene, second is painted.

Finally getting back to this.  Finished up the inner pies, specifically the upper inner pies.

Hey THANKS guys!! Really appreciate the comments!

Jay, right back at ya!!  It was good to see you, never enough time at those shows.

mb1k, the various paint shades wound up being custom to get the hues correct, but the base colors included:

Light Ghost Gray

Flat Gull Gray (mixed with LGG)

AMC Gray (mixed with LGG)

The blue shade was custom mixed with the base LGG and a few different dark blues to get to the right tone. 

The trick was to keep the contrasts very mellow. It was a balancing act to get these right without making it look like a zebra, or so subtle you couldn't see them.  This model looks extraordinarily different under day light or different inside lighting conditions. But all in all, I think it achieved the subtleness I was looking for.  All the pictures are still off than how it actually looks in person.

Thanks,

-Sean
