Well, the problem with the later ST ships is that they relied on a entirely different set of donor kits.  To me, those look like some sort of Japanese robot/spaceship type kit as opposed to the usual F-1 car, German tank, etc. kits that were used in the past.

And though Greg Jein didn't build the Defiant, he did relay a little bit of trivia that might come in handy down the line.  Because they were so overused, he never populated his models with parts from the Leopold/Anzio Annie or the Karl Morser kits.  He tried to find different detail parts to use.  Come to think of it, I never saw any of those kits during my (relatively brief) tenure there......

Not sure if Tony Meininger had the same view.

With the matrix technique you can control the exact thickness of the rubber, rather than brushing the rubber on where you might get thick and thin areas, so it uses a lot less rubber. Also, if you need to make a new mold, you don't have to go through the whole clay up and mother mold process again, you can use the same jacket over and over again.
