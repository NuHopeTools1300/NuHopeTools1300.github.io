Problem is, there is precious little ref material for this 'Legacy' ship and all the members on here that have tackled thisare not returning my calls, for help.

I have the wondrous Marcel Leuthold from www.scifi-studio-scale.com assisting me with his amazing kit bash brain and kit collection so we're off to a flying start but there are big holes in the data base. Any help with decent/rare reference would be extremely helpful

Confession; I always saw this ship and assumed that this was the head/front with the 'headlights' and the back end was the hull stack...maybe because the 'fins' are swept 'backwards' made it look like it pointed in that direction. Now of course I see that the Saturn V 'eyes' are in fact the rear engines/thrusters and it all clicked into place...stoopid

Looking good! Not sure if you were planning to make this available, but if so would you consider leaving all the greeblies/plant-ons as separate objects? So-called "plunging geometry" works perfectly fine for printing while also giving max flexibility to the person doing the printing. 

While a lot of people merge everything into one structure I find that's often a big pain when it comes to assembly and clean-up. I prefer to build my printed projects like a model kit by keeping as many of the details as separate as I can. Sometimes I'll use FDM printing (or even laser-cutting) for some of the structure and resin printing for all the plant-ons.

The added Jedi detail seems pretty odd in an ESB context, but many are common to other models from that film. I was not very familiar with Jedi subjects when I ID'd it, so spent some time re-inventing the wheel. As always for a model this complex there are quite a few unique surprises.
