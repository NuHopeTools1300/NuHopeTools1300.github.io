Hi,

Several years ago I bought a resin kit from Sergio Gutierrez of the ESB probe Droid. The time has finally come for me to put it together. I'll document this in this thread mainly as pictures and with some occasional sporadic comments.

The kit did not come with any assembly instructions but with an vast amount of reference pictures and they're fine to use to figure the assembly out.

After thoroughly cleaning the parts I started the build by figuring out how to deal with a overmold issue on the 'Head'-bulb.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0445-jpeg.2015515/]

I carefully worked on the affected area with a thin needle file. I took quite a while but in the end I got it to look pretty acceptable.

Then I organized the different parts to figure out what goes where. the first thing I assembled was this odd greeblythingy sitting underneath the body. It took some hunting amongst the reference images to get correct but in the end I got there.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0547-jpeg.2015516/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0549-jpeg.2015517/]

I then needed a suitable base for it to sit on. I found a steel rod in a good diameter and length which I attached to a wooden cutting board. On the top of that I designed a part that is screwed onto the 'Body' of the droid. The idea here is that I want the completed build to be able to disassemble in parts to make in possible to move when needed.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0560-jpeg.2015518/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0563-jpeg.2015519/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0561-jpeg.2015520/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0618-jpeg.2015521/]

I then drew up and printed a part for the 'head' to attach to the 'body'. It's just an round disc that is screwed onto the head and keeps in place by friction.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0622-jpeg.2015522/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0623-jpeg.2015523/]

The bottommost part showing above will be attached to the rod fix part and thus hiding it by Neodym magnets at a later stage.

Next up was the arms,

This was a bit of challenge to figure out and took a while.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0552-jpeg.2015524/]

Of course the arms needs their actuators so I made these out of brass rods which I soldered together to match what I could see on the reference pictures.  I might add that they're moving parts, what's soldered are end pieces and sleeves. This was also a good excuse to use my tiny lathe, which is always a joy 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0626-jpeg.2015527/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0629-jpeg.2015528/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0631-jpeg.2015529/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0635-jpeg.2015530/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0637-jpeg.2015531/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0638-jpeg.2015532/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0645-jpeg.2015533/]

When that was finished it was time to attach everything together to the main body. I attached brass rods at the ends of the arms which are then fixed on the 'Body'.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0652-jpeg.2015534/]

Lastly I soldered together a couple of brass antenna(e)s and attached to the head.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0657-jpeg.2015535/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0655-jpeg.2015536/]

This is as far as I've gotten at the time of writing this. Next up I'll be spraying primer of the parts so that I can get onto the paint-job for which I'll be using Archive-X enamels and acrylics.
