All-resin, that is, except for the styrene rings&strips, of course.

The rest has been cast from real kit parts.

My choice is based on optimizing some rare to find kits, also I had some silicon left from another project.

I'm halfway through (still some sanding to do) and I'm unsure about the thickness of said rings.

I cut them out of a vac-formed 1 mm thick styrene sheet hoping that the process would reduce it to roughly .75mm, but it still looks too thick to my eye.

Any thoughts/pieces of advice would be thoroughly appreciated!

I've been slightly distracted from the remote, but yesterday I decided to sand down all the styrene parts, including both rectangles and rings.

I'm glad I did it. I'm much happier now with how it all fits together and the fact that using this method I've ended up with an uneven thickness doesn't bother me too much.

Just some more putty&sanding to do at the equator and it'll be ready for the last greeblies.

And then the magic moment: priming time!
