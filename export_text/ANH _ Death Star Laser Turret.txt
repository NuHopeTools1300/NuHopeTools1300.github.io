Hey guys,

Time to introduce this project to the world.

Back in early 2008 i visited the Star Wars exhibit in Brussels, with the sole purpose of taking pics of the ANH Death Star Laser Turret, Turbo Laser, Gun Turret or whatever you want to call it. I was lucky and the miniature was placed directly next to the edge of the display case, and took nearly 200 pics. God knows how i made 200+ pics of something so (relatively) simple.

I bought kits, made molds, and did 2d and 3d cad work in AutoCAD and Rhino.

Last sumer i rekindled that project while working on the APC's. So as not to interfere with the latter, i spent very very little time on it, but was somewhat shocked that it progressed much faster than the APC's. Sure, the geomotery lends itself to it, but it was obvious i had much to learn.

Now here's the thing. I will produce and offer these as partial kits, but NOT the donor kitparts. Nearly all parts will be machined from styrene or abs, just like the original. I'll break up the model, and offer each section seperately when it's ready, so you can buy whatever you like, and not just the entire model. This also shortens the 'time-to-market' if i may call it that. 

When the APC's start hitting the airmailwaves, i'll slowly start up producing the parts for this model. The APC's are simply first in line. 

A pre-order list will be opened before that (probably this week), and when your parts are ready to ship, you'll receive a PM or email to cough up the dough. Very different from all earlier offerings, but this is simply the way it should be done. And shame on me for not doing so earlier.

Without much further ado, here's the pics.

Earlier 3d version. The inner rings covered in the T-strip are not present, neither are the kit donor parts, except for the Mig 21 engine barrels.

[IMAGE: Wasili-DSLaserTurretwip001.jpg]

Some of the kitparts that i got. Man, don't you just love that 1/48 Panther G. It's everywhere. I did buy the wrong 1/24 Panther for the rear deck, though.. aaarghh. It should have been the Jagdpanther. Something i know now due to the talented Cwkwankoo. 

Bottom of the pic shows the plastruct T-strip (#T6, 4.8mm x 4.8mm x 1.5mm thick ). 

The bog rolls on the left are actually papercovered, roughcut plastruct tubing for the barrels. Slots still have to be machined on those.

[IMAGE: Wasili-DSLaserTurretwip002.jpg]

The base of the turret. Made up of 5 sides of 4mm styrene. Slots on the front, each rear has machined chamfered edges so they fit together perfectly. The base of the real miniature was so close to the glass, that i could take some dims, so this is probably as close to the real deal as you will get it.

Sure, the engraved lines are machined, so straight, as compared to hand made and wobbly on the original. 

This is the first kit part to go up for offer, and will be made identical.

[IMAGE: Wasili-DSLaserTurretwip003.jpg]

Each side, and the top, is covered in 1mm rectangular pieces. All pieces are included, and numbered, with a guide included.

[IMAGE: Wasili-DSLaserTurretwip005.jpg]

The 'blocks' that sit at the rear of the barrels. Not finished yet. At the bottom of the pic is that slanted block that's underneath the Sherman parts.

[IMAGE: Wasili-DSLaserTurretwip004.jpg]

Before i got the plastruct T-strip, i modeled and machined them from styrene. What a waste of time. Alas...

Cheers,

Wasili

Okidoki. By popular demand (you know who you are 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
 ), here's some more pics of the turret.

Overall view. And yes, the forward droop of the top turret is supposed to be there. Look at all the pics and screencaps. It was obviously nose-heavy, with them big-uns at the front.\/

[IMAGE: Wasili-DSLaserTurretwip024.jpg]

The droop is a tad under 1 degrees (0,94... in my book). "what, only one, then who cares ?". Well, we all do, as that 1 meager degree makes the rear go up for 4mm, and more importantly, it changes the whole look of the tower. Oh, and it sits a good 3,75mm to the rear as well. Not dead center either. Xist....

Anyhow, more closer. The hole in the panel on the left of the pic houses the Hummel vent.\/

[IMAGE: Wasili-DSLaserTurretwip025.jpg]

The top. \/

[IMAGE: Wasili-DSLaserTurretwip026.jpg]

On the top rear deck, the M8 Munitions carrier scoop kitparts are actually embedded, in between the rest of the panels, hence the 1mm chambered contour. Same with the droidstrip, which is 1mill lower than the rest of the turret. Detail of M8 contour machined. \/

[IMAGE: Wasili-DSLaserTurretwip027.jpg]

And with M8 part in place. When placed, it leaves a 0,5mm 'ledge' on the front. \/

[IMAGE: Wasili-DSLaserTurretwip028.jpg]

Another one of the top deck. \/

[IMAGE: Wasili-DSLaserTurretwip029.jpg]

Now the rear panel is an interesting one. I struggled with it, as it's wonkyness-extreme on the real deal. Although my panellines are idealised to some degree, i added some wonkiness to this panel to make everything work. More interesting is the fact that this panel was removable on the studio model (note 4 screws in corners), so i machined it leaving 1 of the 4 chamfers that each panel has. It sits perfectly within the surrounding panels, but can be made removable by the builder. Just like the real deal. \/

[IMAGE: Wasili-DSLaserTurretwip030.jpg]

I added the holes and (how do you call that in english, the taper of the hole ?) the 'taper'. The holes are offset from the side edges by exactly 7,5mm, but are positioned in wonki-mode, like the ILM one. They were obviously made by hand, in a hurry, on the studio model. Close-up. And yes, the panel sticks out by 2mm. That edge is for the 2mm dia brass rod that goes up the sides, and into that mystery kitpart that Jason scratchbuild for his kitcastingset. \/

[IMAGE: Wasili-DSLaserTurretwip031.jpg]

The screws used on the model had a head diameter of about 5.7mm, calculating back to about a 3mm or 1/8" woodscrew. The hole i machined is 3,0mm. I mention woodscrew, as the frame of the turret of the ILM model was made from wood, clad with thin styrene. I took pics of the bottom of the turret (as best as i could, given the incredibly poor lighting sit at the Brussels expo in 2008), and the wood is clearly visible, even when looking along the gun barrels in flashpics.

Rear panel. Hole is for the Hummel vent. The Hummel vent is weird in that it's half out, half in. And the hole is bigger, you can see all the way through, but that's the way it is. 

[IMAGE: Wasili-DSLaserTurretwip032.jpg]

And here's the last panel. The Bandai Panther kitpart (under the walkway) sits on the turret, while the Jagdpanther kitpart (above the walkway) is embedded in that chambered rectangle. No holes here, them ILM-ers simply stuck it on, and build the panels up around it. \/

[IMAGE: Wasili-DSLaserTurretwip033.jpg]

 

Missing from the pics are the cast blocks under the barrels, the small styrene rectangles, and the ring that fits inside the top turret (you'll know what i mean when i show it). Tomorrow will see more pics with those parts, but also a breakdown and more close ups of the insides of the parts.

Btw, my take on the ILM turret is this:

- The base is way nicer build than the top. The lines are straighter and line up all the way round. Either it was build by a different person that worked on the top, OR, it was build first until time ran out, leaving ample time to finish the top.

- The top turret is made from a simple wooden frame, with a big gaping hole in the bottom (will show pics). That bottom ring is 5-6mm thick wood. A frame instead of sheets, probably to lower the weight. 

- That wooden frame was first clad in 1mm styrene (yes, only 1) to create the basic shape. Subsequently 1mm handcut panels were glued on that basic shape. Yes, they are really 1mm. Compare them with all the kitparts near the edges.

- The rearpanel is so thin (the aforementioned 2mm), and only attached by 4 screws, that it warps all over the place. The sidepanels are not straight either.

- Due to the weight of the barrels, and the fact that the top is positioned on the base by a single thin central axis, the top hangs forward.

- Early pics of the ILM turret, in the trench, show no ladder rungs.

- It's cool as hell.

Yes, thank you to Wasili for the insane work in making those for everyone that paid and received kits. My heart really does go out to those who were on a list but did not get the opportunity to buy one. 

Also thanks to Eric and Craig and Ed and all those who IDed the parts, and massively again to Craig for sending those bits I didn't have (and it was a LOT) to Mike to mold and cast. It ended up being such a nice way to make a model that the Blockade Runner can now happen this way, and the under-structure is handled by Craig/me/etc so there will be an opportunity for every resin part buyer to buy the acrylic. But back to the turret:

NO ONE had ANY intention of selling something that was incomplete. Wasili completed his transactions, and unfortunately the interest outstripped his ability/time to make more. His kit was CRAZY labor intensive. 

I caught up with Steve Neisen today on the phone, and we talked about food and models and whatnot, and the Turret came up (I read this earlier and it was fresh in my head). He does indeed have Wasili's pattern, I have seen the components in person (from last May) and it will be a great release when it's ready. It will be FAR less tedious a build than what you would have had with cutting all those T-strips, too. I suspect those that bought Mike's resin parts won't have to spend more than they would have, just in getting Wasili's pieces. Just a feeling. And those parts from Mike are all over the studio scale universe, so you didn't necessarily waste your money - if you want to build a tower extension, use them and make more details (I'm extending my tower), or on the other models that used these parts. There are dozens of critical Bandai 1/48 Panther G and 1/15th Bandai bits in those bags that save you hundreds upon hundreds of dollars. It's classic ANH resin mirepoix.

And if you are STILL mad, I get that too. So tell you what - if you want to scratch build that tower NOW, hit me up and I will help you check your measurements. Please understand I am crazy busy on a good day, and get a lot of emails every day, so if I don't get back to you asap do not take it personally. But I absolutely understand being frustrated, excited to build something, and not wanting to mess up. 

I'm NOT going to give you laser files since I didn't make any, and I'm absolutely NOT going to just send a bunch of measurements that Wasili would consider ripping off his work.

If you want someone to verify that the four sided box you are building is accurate, I am happy to look at your work, and check it against what I have built here. I hope that is cool with everyone and in the spirit of the hobby? If you have spent the money and have the Salzo parts set in hand, you have TONS of "landmarks" to lay out and measure, and build a box. 

And here, for free use however you see fit, is a simple .ai file of the "tower extension" I sketched out based off of the Joe Johnston drawing. IT IS NOT CANON. Go nuts!

 tower-extension-final.ai

The thing is set up like this: red is cut lines, black is etched for panel lines. It's mostly useless for you guys except the TOP etch line should mate up with the bottom dims of the ILM tower, as it's designed to perch on top of that first tall section and "blend in".... at least it fit the one I have built! 

So there you go, a hard data point for the very bottom of the tower box. Everything else I just made up to me "in the spirit" of the prop. YMMV. I hope I can help you guys that are building and don't want to wait for Neisen's kit. I suspect there aren't a lot of you, because if it were me, I would wait and build something else in the mean time.
