Hi Lee,

Yes, those are the correct parts!  They're used on lots of the UFO models - the Lunar Tank, the B-142 probe, as well as Interceptor and Sky 1 cockpit window POV sets for instance.

The base plate disc is 37.7mm in diameter, and the pier sections are 18.9mm diameter around the bottom, with the overall height including 'pip' on top 16.4mm.

I have a couple of 'spares' if you'd like them

I'd planned to do a parts map of what I know to post here anyway, just haven't had time yet! I'll try and get it sorted soon.

With the Tamiya Chieftain all you need is ONE track link..... that's all (down by the swing-arm for the rear antenna dish). An expensive kit for one small piece I'm afraid......

It's strange, in all the years I've been researching the UFO miniatures all I've ever found from the Chieftain are the track links.... nineteen on the small Skydiver and one on SID... that's it. Not a single other part from the kit anywhere else, which is very odd.

I do have a theory though. When I built  my first Chieftain up as a kid I remember having some track links spare afterwards - six I think - maybe it varied from kit to kit. Perhaps one of the studio modelmakers had built one up at home themselves, then decided to take the spare links into work to use on UFO? Pure speculation of course, but the thought does appeal to me
