Regarding the lighting on the Falcon.

The cockpit/headlights/engines are lit. They mounting pole enters the Falcon's body in the same place as on the studio scale minitaure (it is a replica afterall 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
). It enters the body though the lower gun turret, but instead of removing the detail as happens on the model, MR left the detail on, the only thing missing is the gun itself. The mount ends in a jack plug that connects the model to the power.

The replica will come with an international transformer that will work, like most computers do, in any country. They're common enough. All you'll need to do is buy a holiday adapter for the American plug that will be on the power cable and voila, Bob's your uncle and Fanny's your aunt.

Date      : May 30, 2006 at 9:58 PM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A paint master is used by the factory as a teaching guide to "match" their production paint job to.

It may take several passes for the factory artists to get it to a point that is a match or as close as one possibly can get. After this hey paint several "control copies" and each artist uses these to guide every hand painted replica.

The MR snow speeder, mastered by Jason Eaton, featured a difficult weathered paint finish. We though it would be a challenge to replicate. The facory pulled it off.

Factory paint jobs on the Boba Fett Helmets I've seen come as near as dammit to the amazing job Lee Malone did on his paint master.

Dean Dymerski was briefed to match the model as near as humanly possible. Dean is painting three Falcons. Each one differs slightly, after all he isn't a computer, but each hand crafted piece is similar. Since the production pieces will also be hand painted, naturally each will differ slightly. What you're paying for at the end of the day is a hand made, hand painted replica that will be as close to the "paint master" as it's possible for the factory to make them, within tolerances.

Every company who mass produces anything is faced with problems of repeatability. There are a variety of techniques factories can use to help facilitate the production process.

Dean set the bar high with the Falcon and that's the way the factory likes it.

When I get pics of the signed off on factory paint master I'll share them with you.

Barry
