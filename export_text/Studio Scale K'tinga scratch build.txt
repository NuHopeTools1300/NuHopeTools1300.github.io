I hear what you're saying, Galaxyman, but the distinction is rapidly blurring. Rapid prototyping machines (3d printers) mean that a model inside a computer is potentially one of the tools of a real, physical modeller. The technique is currently either crude or expensive, but the prices are falling as the quality is improving. 

And I believe a number of modellers already use laser-etched parts and CNC-milled components which are derived from computer models.

Post #70  (forum post #2327664)
Author    : REL
Date      : Jun 25, 2012 at 7:31 PM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Thanks everyone.

I'm working on the neck now.

First make cross sections out of plexiglass.

[IMAGE: ss_ktinga_60.jpg]

Then fill them in and sculpt them to shape, in this case a combo of 15lb rigid foam and bondo.

[IMAGE: ss_ktinga_61.jpg]

Then vac form styrene over them so I have something to detail. I did this twice because of the layered details.

Post #224  (forum post #2770196)
Author    : REL
Date      : Feb 26, 2013 at 10:17 AM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Since just about all the pics posted are different hues of green it's hard to nail it down. So I used photoshop to take a color sampling of all the different colors in the photos and screencaps, plus some pics that aren't available online that I can't post, made a color chart, then bought all of those different shades of green in enamel paint and mixed them together, then used lighter and darker versions of that color to do the subtle panel textures. 

So depending on the lighting in the room, it will take on each of the green colors, sometimes looking more brassy, sometimes more muted. 

But I think these pics here look the best to me so that's ultimately what I'm trying to replicate.
