Sorry if this has been gone over, but I've never seen any comment on this on the boards ... thought I'd post it up for anyone who hasn't got the Rinzler book. 

How about this alternate Vader's TIE pic in the Rinzler 'Making of SW', a built- up model where the lateral hull is extended by two huge forward-sweeping wedges, which are fixed to wings thrown much further forward. The look anticipates the later interceptor design. What's odd is that Johnston comments that Vader's ship was a 'quickie' design for which Lucas picked a sketch showing the wings and hull exactly as they appear in the film. Presumably, in building, they tried out this other design then switched back to the sketch...

First pic: the sketch Lucas picked, according to Johnston, accordng to Rinzler.

I'd say it's a foam-core mockup, but using the existing parts from the standard TIE which made it into the final model.

One interesting thing about Vader's TIE is that it gives the impression onscreen of being a smaller ship than a standard TIE. Just speculating that this mockup might show a previous attempt to make it a bigger ship, suiting Vader's character, which was then reduced and kept in better proportion to the existing elements.
