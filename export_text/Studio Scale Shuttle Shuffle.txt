Well what can I say, work began on my first Vos/Rel Shuttle today. This is going to be one testing build for sure, I have had to think way out of the box on certain area's. I really want to fit Koolshade to the correct place but the resin is so thick there that it makes it impossible to fit it from the inside. A few options I thought of. Remove the hole area & replace with a scratch built front end, dremel away all the bulk of the resin (this could damage the casting though or make the walls too thin, so I decided to try something that will let me still do both other methods if it was to go wrong. I cut out the resin where the KS will go then rough sanded it to shape, then using a dremel cutting wheel, cut a slot for the KS to slide into. It's hard to explain but I'll throw a pic up asap to show what I mean. I think its by far the simplest way to get the cool shade in place without major work. 

This is going to be a tough build in deed. Its not really avoidable as to fit the armature for the wings I had to bore through  an inch of resin to create the 3/4 holes for the alu rod. 

I'll take some pics right now to show what I mean.

Although a tough build I am really enjoying it as it makes it all the better when you have to think outside the box.

Looks like a chore Guy, but I know you'll make it work.

If you have to trim the Koolshade to fit in there, I've found the only way to keep the louvres from warping a bit is to make sure your cut travels in the direction the slats are facing (away from you):

cut --> / / / / / / / 

This way will cause a lot of warpage:

cut --> \ \ \ \ \ \ \ 

Found that out early on

Quite a lot done today, but still a million miles from the finish line. I will add some updates asap. I've been using resin dust & CA glue as filler on this build for the first time & it work a treat for fast repairs on tool slips, I used to use the same tech on guitars for dented fingerboards, but with wood not resin. Be careful doing this though as it gets hot & releases fumes.

Ok, I got of my arse & took some pics.

Thanks to Matt I have some vintage Koolshade & thanks to me I have to make this a test piece, oops. I have re opened the slot for it & its made a big diference. Then next piece wont need as much trimming so should hold its shape better? I still have to do some filling around the edge where the tool has clipped the resin but I should be able to get this quite neat.

[IMAGE: IMG_0005-6.jpg]

[IMAGE: IMG_0004-5.jpg]

The wing fixing are in as is the alu armature but this was an absolute bugger to do. I have had to slowly whittle away at the resin inside the hull as it was over 1/4 inch thick in places, the result is the hinge holes are slightly over sized. I may narrow the slots with styrene yet, not decided!!! The smaller front hinge is has 2 prongs that sit either side of the wing, these have snapped on all the ones I have so I weil add these later to the wing as a none functional detail (If the replacements dont arrive in time). There is a very slight twist to the top body section that needs to be addressed but its only off on one side by 2-3 mm, this may not be a problem but I wont know till I fit the cockpit?

[IMAGE: IMG_0006-4.jpg]

Next is the top wing scratch front section, when I goit the kit this bit gave me the fear but it goes together really well with very little cleanup. I even made a quick scratch of the internal detail that doesn't come with the kit, its a bit rough as it was a test but me being me I just glued it in anyway. I've still not decided on how to light this yet.
