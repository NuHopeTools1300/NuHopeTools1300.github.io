To echo Scott, for many kits, you dont need to source the vintage. Many kits today are of the same tooling, and its only the builders quirk, that may lead him to find its original release.

Some vintage kits are actually awful or delicate, take a few of the Hasegawa Leopolds i own, they are vintage, the same tooling as the current release, but, frighteningly brittle when cutting.

Tamiya are starting to retool a lot of stuff.....luckily, so far, they are leaving the F1 stuff alone (ive not seen the latest 312b Ferrari) so thats good news, as OOP 1/12's are really spendy, especially when multiples are needed, and you dont want to cast duplicates.

One of the really funny sides to the hobby, is when you snag an Ebay auction, for a super rare, very old vintage kit, only to receive it, and its a real bummer of a kit, soft moldings, brittle plastics, maybe only one part used from it.....thats horrible lol.....i tend to get that from a a lot of the AMT stuff from way back......but, needs must.

Regards the same vehicle, same scale, same parts, hmm no, that rarely works either.

Different manufacturers, use different tooling, and in that tooling, some parts can be amalgamated into an assembly, rather than an additional part, if that makes any sense lol.
