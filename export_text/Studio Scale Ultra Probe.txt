Well, it took me a bit to dust her off and re start a project I shelved over two years ago to work on Battlestar Galactica and many other projects. 

This is the Ultra Probe from the Space 1999 episode Dragon's Domain.  This replica is of the 5' shooting miniature. 

I am in the progress of building all the tube frames as you can see in some of the picts. I completed the build thus far and primed it yesterday ( Tuesday  Jan 24th). There are a couple little pieces still not IDed but I can add those at any time. They are not in critical areas. 

I have a casting of the Command Module that Chris Trice, a well known Space 1999 replica builder and some one who has restored several of the shooting miniatures, that he allowed me to mold and cast.

His help is greatly appreciated and with out the ability to purchase the needed photos he had and the use of the CM to make a casting from, I would not have been able to reach this level or accuracy.

So with that, here is the intro:

June 6th, 1997. After being shuttled to the Interplanetary Space Station Centuri where the Ultra Probe ship was docked, The crew prepared for their long voyage into space. Embarkation and countdown continued without a hitch. Launch took place at 1200 hours on schedule. 

So the longest ever manned space flight began.

Author    : Chris Martin
Date      : Feb 4, 2007 at 1:39 PM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Carbon fibre or glass fibre rod can be used. The latter is cheaper. You only need to use it for the longer lengths as 1/8th EMA pipe (as used on the original) tends to distort too easily. It's OK for the shorter lengths though. The carbon or G/F rods can also be sourced from kite shops. I found a dremel cutting disk, like you might use on piano wire, was the best thing to cut the lengths. Wear a dust mask and watch for splinters. The carbon fibre is the worst for this.

Hi Mel,

         Not to hijack my own thread but the Base Star has been in patterning for several years. Only when I was able to survey the real Base Star was I able to clear up some issues that I just did not want to take the best guess at. It is a fantastic miniature that really was under presented. The painting is a beauty of its own. 

I have recently secured a source for the 3' disks and the tube that makes the core model. This was a search for the best price so as not to make the kit too expensive. One thing that needs to be known, there are 10 pulls of each of the two panels. That's twenty panel per kit. the outside trenches have the same wall detail as the inside trench walls only they are cut down to a reduced pitch. That alone is saving me patterning time. I don't have to make 4 separate patterns 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
  Just the main 2. The floors are like the outer panels. One pattern for the outside trenches and one patterns for the inside. As well as the bridges and launch platforms that extend out of the trench areas. The hang up now is there are certain areas that were made of kit parts only, no castings. Molding and casting would cost more then the kit parts needed so I'm researching this problem. As with the SSD they cut a layer of plastic and notched it and it borders both the top and bottoms of BOTH disks a long with kit parts on it. It is a labor of love. I have been working hard on it. I took a break for some personal reasons but thanks to a couple friends, George and James, I am getting back to it and going to get it kitted ASAP. I will not take any pre orders. This is a practice that breads trouble. 

I hope that helps :love 

You can PM me if you like. But for obvious reasons; time invested, kits and parts purchased and money spent, I will not be posting any images till the kit is in production. I'm not trying to be an elitist. Just trying to protect my exhaustive investment in this project.

Mark
