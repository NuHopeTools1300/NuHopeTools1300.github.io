Build update.

Virtually completed all the building. Some bits gave me pause for thought as the reference I have includes photos from the smaller and larger studio scale models. Definitely decided to keep the cabin so that meant adding other bits and pieces. I've altered things on the feet, broadening the "ankles" as they seemed a little slim.Also the top part running over the foot needed a little remodelling to make it closer to the ideal. And the toes will extend in and out to vary the length in the photos. Had a lot of bad luck with this build , I seemed to chip and snap bits off it like nobodys business.

[IMAGE: 2016_0328waxwings0003.jpg]

Legs are done. These seem pretty strong and apart from a little twitching here and there they are finished. The only part that I would query is the disk behind immediately behind the "shield" on the leg. It has a flowery pattern in the centre whilst the reference on the smaller models shows concentric raised circles like a wheel. Any one know the part that goes in there?

[IMAGE: 2016_0328waxwings0001.jpg]

The underside of the main cabin is full of leads, I counted six at the front and three at the back. For four of them I used 3mm cooper wire wrapped around thicker copper rods. Bend the rods into shape before you slip the wire on, which is best done on a straight piece before hand. The black wire was single core bell wire, its stiffer and held the shape quite firmly. It was a pain trying to work out exactly where they all went ,this is my best guess from the pictures I had. Also added the Skf 222 parts , they were a lot cleaner and sharper and needed a little modification anyway. Guess I will be building those two probe droid pods at some point in the future anyway as a first scratch build using all model parts!!!! 

[IMAGE: 2016_0328waxwings0008.jpg]

[IMAGE: 2016_0330waxwings0006.jpg]

You can see the three at the back of the cabin in the next photo. Use a brass rod slightly smaller than the one you first wrap the copper around other wise it bunches around the corners.

[IMAGE: 2016_0328waxwings0005.jpg]

Because I decided to keep the cabin I had to cut the hatch out through the top, which wasn't hard to do. Kudos to Mike the parts matched up very neatly so It didn't take much remodelling ,apart from smoothing out the walls. However the top hatch needed to be altered. As it opens and closes thanks to the metal thread, I've based the underside of the hatch on the full scale model in ROTJ, which is best seen when Chewie opens it buy the bunker (except of course it opens at a completely different angle from the SS model).

[IMAGE: 2016_0330waxwings0002.jpg]

[IMAGE: 2016_0330waxwings0001.jpg]

[IMAGE: 2016_0330waxwings0005.jpg]

[IMAGE: 2016_0330waxwings0003.jpg]

 I guess the next step is to paint it up after a little bit of a clean up. I might add internal lighting as I'm thinking about doing it as part of a diorama with the At St in for "Repairs" with a catwalk built around it. Hence the hatch being able to open. That way I can thread some brass rods in around the body so as to support the weight of it. Need to get a base sorted first  !

Just a small update:

The base of the neck "gromit" ,once you've cut the rubber from its plug its still a little inaccurate to the SS part. You can sand it and that's fine but I decided to tidy it up and extend it a little by adding a plastic collar then smoothing it into the rest of it with apoxy sculpt so its a closer match to the model.

[IMAGE: 2016_0411waxwings0006_2.jpg]

Because the neck part is quite soft and easily compressed I added a plastic inner that keeps it more stable and stop the paint from cracking in the future. I tried it and unless you give it a good squeeze it won't crazy pave:

[IMAGE: 2016_0411waxwings0007.jpg]

To be honest it really could do with being a couple of more millimetres thinner at the base , but unfortunately I'd already trimmed the rubber too closely to fit onto the base, so I had to make the collar slightly wider so I could attach it. Food for thought to anyone tackling another of these in the future.

[IMAGE: 2016_0411waxwings0019.jpg]

 

A bit of sanding and then more of the ever reliable primer for a smoother finish. I've also put on a few coats to help stop it cracking. All in all its a much tidier fit and look for it but I've found as you start to finish a model of you get pickier with the things that don't quite cut it for you.

Cheers guys, I enjoyed doing this, been a while and I need to get a few other models finished off and new ones built this summer, since last year was spent doing a big conversion to add a few rooms to the house. The weathering used a couple of techniques I've never used before and alot of dry brushing  to try and get it more "ILM"ish  and they mostly seemed to  work. 

One thing that is really clear is you need to establish the basic colour tone early, based on what conditions the original reference studio scale  model was photographed. If the pictures were taken with flash it washes out a lot of colour and flattens its. Here's an example of mine taken under different conditions:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/2016_0606waxwings0031-jpg.1384622/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/2016_0606waxwings0032-jpg.1384623/]

So its worth considering getting hold of as much ref as you can before being satisfied with what you set out to replicate. Nice cast kit as always from Mike S , fancy doing an X wing next.
