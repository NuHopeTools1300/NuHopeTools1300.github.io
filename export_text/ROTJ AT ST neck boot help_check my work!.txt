Hi Mike

I bought this kit a few years ago, although work has been a bit stop and start with it because of various other on-going projects. I started again more or less from the begining last last year though, on a mostly scratch-built walker but using the kit's head and feet. Nearly there with drawing for a machinist (I think)..

..Anyway that's all for another thread. By coincidence I've been working on my walker's neck recently as well. I'm having a little trouble deciding on the shape of the top half. I have enough ref of the bottom part but because I can't decide on the top I thought I'd do both bits separately, trying out a couple of variations then when I reach a combo of top and bottom that I like I'll bond them together, fill the middle join a little and mold it. 

Here's what I've done so far. These were made by tracing from photos, scaling those tracings, using that to make a half silhouette in plastic that I could rotate around a basic shape sculted from putty. As I rotated the plastic silhouette I added car filler so that outline of the shape is formed smoothly all round (bit like making a clay pot)... bit hard to put in words but I'm sure you get the idea!
