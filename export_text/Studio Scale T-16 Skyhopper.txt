So I also decided to recreate the T-16 Skyhopper that Luke Skywalker plays with in the homestead. Although the design has never been my favourite it is growing on me. I built it because it seemed fairly easy to do and because I like the model. Not as a concept but rather for its prop value.

I made a quick model in Sketchup. Jason checked my measurements for me (thanks again for that) and I was really close on most of it. On some dimensions down to only 0.2mm off. Exported the shapes to illustrator and had everything lasercut in clear and black acrylic. Here's the results after about a week playing with the lasercut parts.

Well I'm not good at turning metal. Actually ruined one of my chisels with just this thing. 

To be honest it's not that difficult. Just get a set of parts from www.grandbrass.com (1/4 I.P.S.), find the correct size nut somewhere and get some K&S brass rod. Go to a local machinist have them cut and thread the rods and cut down the nut a bit and drill and thread three holes in the nut's sides. Bob's your uncle.

Is anyone building this by the way?

Here are the parts that I used to make the stand:

GRAND BRASS    BB0-1/2X6/32 1/2in. SOLID BRASS BALL WITH 6/32 FEMALE TAPPED BLIND HOLE

GRAND BRASS    NI4-0X1/8BR - BRASS 4in. X 1/8ips. (3/8in. O.D.) THREADED HOLLOW BRASS NIPLE

GRAND BRASS    NU434 1/8-27 I.P.S. Female X 3/4in. BRASS FLAT ROUND KNURLED NUT

GRAND BRASS    RE8FX4X3/4 1/8ips FEMALE X 1/4ips MALE THREADED 3/4in. REDUCER WO SHOULDER

K&S METALS        KS8166 3/16 Brass Rod 12in (4.76mm)

K&S METALS        KS8137 7/16 Brass Tube 12 Inches

Think I used a 1/4-27 I.P.S. Female X 1in. X 1/4in. Thick BRASS NUT for the centre piece. I had to put in a spacer to make it all fit.
