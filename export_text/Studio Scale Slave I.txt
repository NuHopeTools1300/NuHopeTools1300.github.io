Post #74  (forum post #865014)
Author    : REL
Date      : May 6, 2009 at 8:19 AM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

There's more than one reason this won't be offered as a mass produced kit. 

For starters, the complexity of it. To break even on it, the kit would have to be well over $2,000, anything less and I'm giving it away. 

I initially figured the cost around $1,000, but after all the work, molds, parts etc. that figure was way underestimated. 

Secondly, a licensed company has announced they're planning on making one, and it's not a good idea for a garage kit to be stepping on their toes. They paid for a license, at considerable cost, so it's unfair for me to take away any sales they may eventually have. If they read that someone would rather buy mine than theirs, they're not going to like that much, and they shouldn't, as like I said they paid for a license. 

So, the people that I have said will get one, will get one as soon as it's finished, and that should be later this summer.

After reading some of the prop threads, I saw some of the guys had luck with using resin as a gelcoat, backed with fiberglass for reinforcement. 

So today I did a resin brushcoat, with epoxy fiberglass (10 oz. fabric) reinforced casting. 

This process took me all about 20 minutes to do.

[IMAGE: slave_r_body01.jpg]

It would normally take me about 2 weeks to completely cast up a Slave body, using this technique, it takes me 2 hours. That was the one major reason for not offering a kit of this ship, the time and money involved in casting the body. 

With this technique, the body is the easiest part.
