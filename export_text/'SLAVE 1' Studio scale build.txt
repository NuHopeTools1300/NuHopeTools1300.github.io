SLAVE 1

Hi, I'm building  a studio scale model of my favourite Star Wars ship, Slave 1. The files were sourced from Gambody and 3D printed in ABS on my Ultimaker over many weeks. The 3D prints only provide a basic framework as there are quite a few inaccuracies and the detail is a bit wonky. It's getting some major surgery and a lot of scratch built detail including an all new cockpit.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/51165029961_22b67a704e_o-jpg.1456033/]

SLAVE 1 ROTATING COCKPIT

​
I've been making progress on my 3D printed 'studio scale' 1/34 Slave 1 from files downloaded at Gambody. This model isn't 100% accurate and I am trying to correct that. Rather than totally screen accurate I want to give it engineering credibility and have scratch-built the cockpit to my own design.

You can see my design for Slave 1's rotating cockpit here on my YouTube channel.

This is the landing / take off position for the flight deck.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/51737098787_3595814a0e_o-jpg.1519970/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/51738810870_9c50012f79_o-jpg.1519971/]

The unit then tilts back 90 degrees for flight

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/51738565724_f82a238197_o-jpg.1519972/]

Progress is going well on my studio scale 3D printed 'Slave 1'. The original stl files serve just as a starting point. All the detail has been removed and a fair bit of re-engineering has taken place. All the main units are superglued together now and I have begun adding detail. The upper hull has been clad in styrene and the process of scribing panel lines has begun. I've also added styrene detail beneath the tail. Still lots to do. The Epax are old printers but still can do some prints for the smaller parts.
