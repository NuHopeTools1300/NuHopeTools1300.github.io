Just a rider - EPOXY resin, EPOXY!!! Not regular fibreglass resin, that **** is polyester; that'll eat right through styrene foam. Epoxy resins are two-part mixes where (usually) you add about equal amounts of each part. It's much less smelly and reactive, and stronger. Think Araldite only runny.

To add to Al's remark, wet it with epoxy, apply mat (strips or squares of a lightweight weave), stipple it in being sure there are no dry spots. Going over the top of it with *a little* more resin is fine if you do get some. Use a disposable brush.

First  for sanding the foam i like to use a card file.  The wire brushes used to clean the metal off a file.

For the vac buck. What i like to do to save money and have a strong buck. Make your pattern.  Wax it. Do a waste mold in concrete an then fill the mold with concrete again to get your vac pattern. Dont forget to wax the mold.  And your waste mold should be one layer and very thin.    I did a write up on it in the betty thread.
