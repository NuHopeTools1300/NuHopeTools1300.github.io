REL's model of the design can't be faulted. The design can, though, to me at least. While it looks great as posed by McQ in his painting, it does not look so from all angles, especially the back. Wings for me give off a whiff  of cardboard toilet roll tubes glued to cardboard (not REL's prob, it's the design. It'd still look that way if made out of steel). The irony is though that if you gave the X-wing to Apple's Jon Ive to design, it'd certainly look more like this than the Johnston/McCune final version. But for me such a dreamy lightness of touch is utterly wrong for the character and role of the ship in the story. It's too simple, too simple for the rugged yet noble-looking steed upon which our hero rides. Lacks the real-world weight that Lucas rightly required from every element in the film. The final design has that in spades.
