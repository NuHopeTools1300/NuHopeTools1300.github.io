Hey yeah not studio scale but here goes. 

I’d start with a 1:25 1950’s car kit for the seats, dashboard, headlights etc. The dome you can get. Other details on the bottom and back j ges your best bet is jet and space kits either real life or scifi. Only hard part is the body so either find some product containers that fit or do an at home vac form. The latter is how I would do it.

I suggest going to a fancy Lighting store and looking at their replica mid-century modern lighting fixtures, because that circular design looks very reminiscent of certain styles of lamps from that era.  Ebay searches of the same may also be productive.  Do a search on "mid-century modern" and also "Eames" lighting fixtures and you're sure to find something that's usable -- if a bit pricey, alas.  You might also try the Ikea catalog, which sometimes does retro stuff like this for cheap.  I would also scour antique stores and vintage shops.

You may want to try a second hand store or a dollar store.  A small Frisbee would work for the top, but you need to get one that isn't made of polyethylene which doesn't take paint, cutting, and filler very well.  Scouting through these stores, you may find several cheap objects that will do, or even objects that might be used for bucks for vacuforming.

If you don't have a lathe, or don't know anyone with one, you can try to make a turntable and use a cut out shape to screed the shape you want out of clay. I believe Lasse Henning (a member here) had that on his old webpage off of www.starwarz.com The model Shop which has turned into a facebook page.
