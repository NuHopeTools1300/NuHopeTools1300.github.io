Hi Guy,

I think with your plaques on the base it will look even more classy.

The base is granite it is very heavy and was incredibly hard to drill. It cost just under a tenner from Tesco http://direct.tesco.com/q/R.100-9445.aspx so it is a really cheap but effective base (I did grab an extra one for my TIE in the future). It is a work surface protector for the kitchen. It also cost me 4 masonary drill bits 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
 

I countersunk the Panavise to try to get the bolts flush on top. I then drilled three holes 4.5mm through the granite which requires patience as I didn't want to push to hard and risk breaking the granite. I also countersunk the back of the base with an 8mm masonary drill to have the nuts recessed into the granite and cut the bolts to length accordingly so the base sits down ok. The base has rubber feet anyway so you have some protection on the surface you put the base on. I then painted the bolts black on top. 

It doesn't show to well in the pictures but the finish on the base looks really nice in the flesh. If you do use this idea check the finish on the ones in Tesco some have light coloured marks in the granite surface.

Lee I moved the upper and lower right guns as per your advice and it worked a treat so I am even happier with this model if that is possible 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]

Cheers Chris
