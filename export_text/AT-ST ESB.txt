Ok, I've been looking at the pics. This isn't my final opinion but it is what I am seeing at the moment.

First, I think the Karl part sits flat against the armature. 

I also see what Julien means. You mention the point at the 5 o'clock position of the Gepard. Using that, the opposite 9 o'clock(ish) point should be really visible in pic #437. 

Dave, do you have copies of the ILM AT-ST blue-prints? I don't know the exact timeline but I am pretty sure these blueprints were used in the same manner the Speederbike blueprints were....to make the full sized props for ROTJ. And hopefully you remember how we pulled id's from the Speederbike blueprints. I don't have a high quality copy but you can see they are really detailed to the ESB model and is more than likely why the model was torn apart. That being said, I cannot make out the 9 o'clock point in the blueprints which tells me that that area of the Gepard part was modified to fit the Karl and other parts. 

Here's my thing. In pic #437 you can see a ring at the bottom of the "knee" joint. This has to be primer. The "white" color of the lower leg is the natural metal of the armature. So that shows a good picture of the contour of the "knee" joint. Following that, yeah the Karl part overlaps that circle line into the Gepard space. BUT....if you look at the thickness of parts.....combined, the Croc part sitting on top of the tiny protruding oval detail at the Gepard 10 o'clock position are the same exact thickness as the Karl part. 

The only way I see the Karl "sitting" over the Gepard is if you notch the Karl part. I mean, the side facing the armature is "hollow", right, so it would be very simple to notch. Still, though, pics #441 and 453 do not show a notched Karl. My drawing isn't entirely accurate but it shows what I mean. Still, if it was notched, the Croc part would sit higher than in pic #470 UNLESS you shave off the oval detail. It sucks because I cannot get a good angle to see if the oval detail is there. So it can still go both ways, I think.

[IMAGE: legpartstudy.jpg]

On a side note, I still don't understand why the Gepard wasn't centered to the knee joint. The top contour of the part matches the contour of the joint. You end up with armature showing either way but at least if centered, you would hide the armature screw. Hmmm...

Whew...lmk what you think.

And Q, I'm studying the 2nd Karl part. It appears to  me so far that the 312B part hangs over the edge of whatever sits underneath per pic #447.
