If you can set the angle to 90 degrees, you should be able to draw a circle with the length of the scan as the diameter and choose a point on the appropriate circle. 

 However, there was no guarantee that the scan was taken vertically based on the base of the model, so there is something I overlooked here.

 If it's tilted, that distortion adjustment will be needed. When I took this scan, I assumed that the model was mounted vertically, and I made a vertical standard for the camera in three cases. 

I'm waiting for help from anyone with behind-the-scenes knowledge or reasonable guesses about this photo. 

ps: Does distortion occur even if it's taken flat? It seems like you use the lens distortion value when calculating this, but does anyone know how to find out the lens value used in the shooting or how to use it to get the original length?

I don't know if this helps AT ALL, but it was always my surmise that form followed function, and that the Star Destroyer plates were 90 degrees at the rear corner simply by virtue of the fact that if you take a 4'X8' sheet of plexiglass, and give it the appropriate cuts, you end up with a.) the least amount of cuts and b.) the maximum use of material to make the wedge-shaped ship.  So the rule of design "elegance" (maximal structural integrity using minimal material means) suggests a 90-degree angle, as well as the Zvezda Star Destroyer and the Bandai 1/5000 Star Destroyer, which have 90-degree angles at the back corners.

For this particular inquiry, I would definitely download any free 3D model and comparing it to imagery. I'm using the hobbiest Fusion 360, but it has a huge learning curve. I've also used blender, mostly for model conversions which I'm not suggesting here, but Blender is flexible enough for a quick & dirty load and viewing. Something I do a lot, is manipulate the viewing angle of the 3D model until it matches a 2d reference image. Can't necessarily calculate exact angles, but you can determine if the shapes are correct and angles between panels are appropriate.
