Thanks fellas.   The build was a dream.  Very little sanding etc.  Not a single bubble on this kit.  Yes I believe it is 1/24.    The paint in person is much more blue than the pics show.  It looks electric blue when I take a pic with the flash.  But it washes out all the details so I left those pics off.  This color is the correct Tamiya color.  I'm actually more partial to the regular TIE,  just because I'm an ANH nut!!!  Thank you so much for the kind words everyone.  Hearing it from you fellas is a true compliment indeed.  
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]

It is not currently lighted,  but all it would take is drilling a tiny pilot hole thru the engine exhausts and wire it up inside to a couple red LEDs.  I may very well do this at some point.   
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]

Dave

PS,  the top hatch,  front canopy, wing center covers  and bottom button are all removeable.  I added some 1/4-20 connectors into the main armature for all 6 axes and I can simply screw into a standard panavise mount for photography purposes.  Just like the original except for the 1/4" connectors.   There is a sweet sliding panel behind the pilot's seat that reveals the connector thru the front canopy.   I painted up both the TIE pilot and the military pilot which both came with the kit.  As you know ILM used the military pilot in the cockpit of the TIEs.  I can interchange them for fun.

The models were painted the same colour from ANH to ROTJ. The white/gray on-screen look in ANH was due to the blue-screen proscessing, which removed enough of the blue spectrum from the image to render the TIEs white.

It may have been a case of fixing something 'wrong' as far as the productions were concerned, but I remember in 1980 it took a little getting used to! All we knew was what we'd seen on-screen in Star Wars: TIEs were white! :lol

From this far away (New Zealand), I'm not 100% sure of anything! :lol

I'm just going on what's available. There's a passage in Famous Spaceships of Fact and Fantasy which goes something like 'The Ties were painted a light blue, almost powder blue, but because gray values were lost in printing the Ties appeared much whiter on movie-house screens'. It may even say a 'light gray-blue' (that's from memory, as I can't find my copy right now).

And that was published in 1979, before TESB, when there was only one movie and it was called Star Wars!

Then there's the bit in SAG, where Lorne Peterson says they weren't allowed any blue on the models, but that "the TIE fighter's Stormy Sea squeaked through thanks to the amount of gray in the mix". I think if you see both gray and blue TIEs on screen in Jedi it has more to do with slight variations in colour timing during compositing.
