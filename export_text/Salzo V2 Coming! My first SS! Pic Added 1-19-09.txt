I found soaking the fuselage in hot water and putting heavy weights on them overnight on a flat surface flattened them out, mine were slightly warped. They went together nice and flush after that. 

Also the back end cap for the fuselage, was a little too large, I had to again, soak the back of the fuselage in hot water to spread the fuselage a bit to meet up with the end piece properly.

I guess I am saying, hot water can be your friend on this one if you have some fit issues. Don't try to sand the problem away first.

Well the kit was delivered by UPS yesterday! Yay for me! I was surprised by how small the box was. That is until he handed it to me and I felt the weight! With great antici...............................................pation I opened the box and unpacked the contents. I was impressed to say the least. I marveled at the detail. Something I have never seen in a plastic kit before. After inspecting all the parts for any possible shipping damage, I layed them all out. There was some very minor damage to one of the canons, the two tabs had broken off. That can be easily fixed though.

Next, I grabbed my dremel and started trimming the hull parts. I had to see them together. They were staright as a board, or so I thought. Holding them together I held the end cap up. It overhung the hull by a good quarter inch. I figured the hulls over the wing roots were warped. After a few minutes in a sink of hot water I tried to straighten them. Nope, the resin is too thick. I will have to resort to some other method of heating the rear sections to bend them upwards.

Over all I am very impressed with my first SS kit. I need to do some serious research as there are a few pieces that I'm not sure where they go. Hey, I'm a newbie to SS and not an X-Wing expert. I'm going to need to pick some brains here!

Stay tuned.
