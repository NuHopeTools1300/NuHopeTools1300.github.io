Hey guys,

When I built my V2 (awesome Salzo's kit) I was planning to motorized wings. Unfortunatly I didnt have the skills nor the ref at this time.

With the release of the V3, I thought it was worth to give it a try ! 

My goal with this X-Wing is to build mine like the Hero (meaning materials, design of the armature etc) but I will not focus on the details (you will notice the chips on the wings are the chips of the pyro wings 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
). I will use only 2 Airfix kits so it means I will not have the right cans (I should got 4 kits but I cant 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
, if someone has spare cans, I would be happy to buy them 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
).

The 2 first cans :

[IMAGE: r4-8.jpg]

My first wing (I cheated and I added a rib at the root of the wing, it is hollow on the hero wings but it more esthetic IMHO). I used mostly acrylic, except for the inner plate which is styrene. The U-channels are styrene on the prop, but I used brass U-channels to add strenght to the whole thing !

[IMAGE: r4-10.jpg]

I sanded the plexi to remove the lips of the scribbing job and to get a better grip for the paint :

[IMAGE: r4-11.jpg]

I glued the wrong 251 part on the trailing edge of the wing. It has been corrected now :

[IMAGE: r4-12.jpg]

Here you can the rib (styrene) I added at the root of the wing :

[IMAGE: r4-16.jpg]

I will not be able to work on it before a long time, so dont expect updates soon !

Today I worked more on the cannons, they are now finished. 

That was a ***** to get the Hummel parts to shape because Ive got a repop (80's) of the Hummel which has those wheels molded in a low friction plastic (that's the R/C version). It means those parts just cant be sanded ! I ruined 2 wheels before a success. If you put those wheels on your dremel to use it as a lathe and use sandpaper and that the speed is too low, you sand nothing ! If you use a highter speed, you got melted plastic. 

So I used the tip of a file, not to sand the part, but to remove plastic while the dremel was on. Took me 25 minutes / wheel. :angry

As for the ring, I used a ring of my V2, I added at the back styrene to get a taller ring and to transform the ring into a cap 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
 Then I molded it.

The C parts and the tip tubing were also bashed from my V2. I have to find something else when I will rebuild my V2 ! :lol The C-parts will be replaced by metal ones when I get the stuff.

The M8 wheels and Sherman parts were added.

Thanks ILMwannabe,

Yeah I should take better pic of it. It's not finished so as I plan to repaint the canopy and nose cone and maybe tweak a bit the weathering !

Yeah it was a pyro, that's 100% sure :

- fuselage with same chips pattern as the other pyro

- wing mounting

- engines cast ine one piece (turkey feathers included). Same mold lines on the 4 engines, and it's not straight running from the turkey feather to the point where they meet the Saturn V cans. Same mold line can be seen on the other pyro engines.

- solid wings with the same chips patterns. Upper right wing and lower left wing are identical, upper left and lower right wings are identical. We can notice some airbubbles on the lower left wing !

- the nose cone cant be removed unlike the hero noses.

- R2 strip socket different from the hero birds but the same as the pyros.

- Sealab parts on the wings are typically pyro assembly (as they're cast with the wings). Glued a different way on the hero models.

Some more styrene chips were added to the fuselage and wings to make it a bit more unique !

It's the only one X-Wing with an R4 unit (maybe why the R4 unit is called R4 since it comes from Red 4 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
)

The decals come from the Harrier or Phantom, I cant remember, but I wasnt able to find 3 of the decals. All the other ones are the right ones !

Ive never seen pics of Red 4 under construction, Ive only identified Red 12 while being painted (the other yellow-canopy X-Wing).
