────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Studio models were made for exterior shots, so cockpits are going to be very basic as there was no point in making it super detailed because they would make a full sized cockpit/interior for the actors to sit in for those shots. 

So it all depends on what your tastes are, if you want it to be an exact replica of the filming model then accuracy would be best, if you want it to be a display that people can look at that don't know the history behind the models then a detailed interior would be best. It's art, so your own desires should dominate your thinking.

Thanks guys for all the input. Everybody is right. Build what makes you happy. I must admit I am still at a crossroads with regards to this issue. Though I said I don't particularly like what ILM did with the cockpit, it is not bad. REL's point about "composites" is right. For the most part builders are compositing elements of the full scale set into the miniatures but through building as opposed to photographic editing. I am going to take it one step further in regards to models being for exterior photography only. I don't necessarily believe this to be case under all circumstances, for the bomber, sure but I believe the ILM builders built what they wanted and what they felt looked decent. They for the most part had no clue what the sets looked like or were going to look like since they were nowhere near the live filming locations. I'm probably leaning towards full accuracy. I have to say I'm anal about things, not so much about build quality as I am trying to work in the same vein as the ILM guys were, so dings, scratches, rough casts don't bother me very much but dimensions are another story. I've torn things apart that are off by as little as 1/16 of an inch and sometimes less. I've invested in getting all the exact and correct parts so I should probably have a duplicate of the cockpit and would more than likely not be happy with it any other way.

If anyone has any questions with regards to the snowspeeder as far as IDs go I would be happy to help. I've ID'd 99% of it and have studied the models extensively. Oh one last thing. Just wanted to let anyone building these or planning to build these to pay close attention to the model you are building. Rogue Leader and Rogue 2 are very different from each other as far as dimensions, design and even donors.

Thanks again for the help guys. Keep the discussion going.
