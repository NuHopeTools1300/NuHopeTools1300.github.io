Hello!

I am trying to (with my 8yo) write and plan a tokusatsu short - my son has become enamored with Ultraman (my fault!) and now is really itching to make his own Ultra hero. He has a number of designs, we're writing a script together and I've been showing him as much as possible (that exists online) about the making of the original Ultraman series and the current series (Ultraman Z). There's a lot of available resources about costume design but, that I can find, very little about the methods of constructing the models of building used during shots where a monster is destroying a city. From outside appearances the technology used in toku shows to show a building being destroyed hasn't changed much - I know that to add more interest to a building being crushed that flash bulbs laced with gunpowder would be lit and explode within the models;  I know that the interior "floors" of the models were coated in sand and dust and small bits of "debris" but I'm really interested in the basic structure of the models themselves that allows them to be destroyed in structurally interesting ways. What are the building materials used? I'm appreciative of any info anyone would have on the subject, and for all your expertise in general!

In movies I worked on, we would make a type of foamed plaster that simulated miniature concrete which could be easily destroyed.

It was made by mixing Alka seltzer into plaster, and pouring it into forms to set. You'll want to experiment with how much you use to get the density that you want. 

Be very careful removing it from the forms, as it can be very fragile.
