I'm sorry to hear that.

The studio scale hobby certainly has its frustrations. Cost and availability among them.

These days I spend more time in painting groups on Facebook, where the focus is on technique and weathering, of which you have great skill.

I don't have a V5. I recently compared a V3 and V4, and once I'd considered the points of accuracy difference, I found I preferred the crisper lines and panel scribing of the original V3 master pattern. It smacks more of Red 3 with those inner window frames and sharp edges. They are all beautiful models.

Personally, I find the authenticity vs accuracy viewpoint draws me away from Studio Scale. I have always wanted a model that looks ILM, even if it doesn't have that Flakpanzer-waffle-sproket. There can be hyper accurate models that just don't feel "ILM", and there are sloppily painted Bandai kits that look like they came from the archive.

I hope you keep building and painting.
