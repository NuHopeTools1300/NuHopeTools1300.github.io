Posted this on the RI forum also. My friend is making a feature film based on his comic book and short film character Matt Mercury. Originally, it was supposed to be a fun adventure film loosely based on Science Fiction films of the 50’s. Matt Mercury originally had a rocket like those space rangers did in the old days. Once he got into the production, he realized a lot of other people were doing rocket-ship type movies, so they decided to inject some creative juice into their effort. That and the fact that his wife shamed him by reminding him that he always tell her how creative he is, yet he couldn't come up with an original ship design for Matt Mercury. The film is more in the style of 60’s Sci-Fi television anyway, so the 50’s stuff just didn't fit.

I asked Bill to write up something as he sends me pics.

			Ok, well this was the fastest and easiest way I could think to get it done. I’m a cartoonist and film guy and not that much of a model builder. The shapes are rough and I tend to eye-ball everything at first. Just wanted a shape to get fiberglass on it. I plan on doing all the leveling out and clean up in the fiberglass bondo stage. I will probably re-design it a little s I go along. As I see how shooting angles look on the actual part I could get a better idea of the finer points of the design.

The main deal with this is it’s all done as fast as I can. I’m directing the film, designing the sets and pretty much everything as well as doing the editing and the modeling is really the most fun, but I never get as much time as I’d like to do it.

-Bill

The fiberglass was nasty. I used the shapeable mat and asked some guys on youtube if I needed a mask to use it... they said no. I didn't trust them and they were wrong. They didn't use masks and their lungs are gonna PAY for it someday!!!

Coated fiberglass with fiberglass reinforced bondo. That stuff sets up like stone!

Oh one other neat thing, this foam shapes real well by just using scrap as sanding tools. They end up making nice soft curved complex curves!

-Bill

The fiberglass form was hollowed out pretty easily just by scraping out the foam. Then I started to build a wood frame and add on pre-cut acrylic structures for the base of the sodium mixers (engine outriggers). I’m not an electrician, but with the help of Tenacontrols navigation boards and some strip LEDs I was able to make a lighting scheme just as I had imagined it.

-Bill

			The outriggers are designed to look like they don’t exactly match the rest of the ship. In the story, they are an untested technology that was added to the ship as an emergency measure. The ship originally had sleek turbine engines. Oddly enough, what I had in mind as inspiration is the mobile space station from the old Gold Key Space Family Robinson comics. I always loved how that thing could land on the twin uprights and The Electra does that as well.

-Bill
