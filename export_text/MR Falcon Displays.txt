Well, the first caution is that the Falcon is rather heavy. So anything you get will have to by sturdy/heavy, especially if you want to get it closer to eye-level.

The mounting pole for the thing is something like 1.5" - 2". I don't think there is a tripod available that you could easily get to use as a base. Most consumer grade cameras are small and lightweight. Even pro camera tripods aren't designed to hold such a bulky item.

I have several old style motion picture camera tripods. Those might work as they are made of metal and weigh in at around 25 pounds alone. But they would need some sort of modified head in order to mount a model of that size.

The other route you could go is closer to what they actually used - which is to assemble a stand made out of pipe fittings (commonly called "speed rail"). Do a google search and you will find all sorts of neat fittings that can be attached to aluminum pipes that are cut to specific lengths. Look at a lot of the behind the scenes photos out there and you will see how indispensible the stuff is on a movie set. The downside is that the stuff is utilitarian and might just give the impression of a hastily constructed support (which is what the stuff actually was) instead of a classier museum-like appearance.

Gene
