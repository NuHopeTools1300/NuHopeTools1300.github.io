I just scanned in a quick 'surface block' test I did on a piece of cardboard. 

The cardboard is roughly 7 inches by 4 inches and the surface detail is approximately the size it will be on the actual model.

The colors aren't final and the details were done rather hastily. First I painted the base coat, then masked off for the darker blocks. Then I removed the masks.

Using a steel ruler and a dental scribe, I scribed off the darker gray to reveal the base color underneath. I then added pencil lines using the same ruler and then used weathering pastels to give tone variation.

The final product is will be much more carefully done, but I'm very happy with these preliminary results. I KNOW now without a doubt that I can not only do this...but I can DO IT WELL and have it look as it should.

[IMAGE: KuhnStarsurfaceblocktestsm.jpg]

Link to full resolution scan.

I know this works because when I view it from a distance or zoom out on this scan, I've achieved the required scale effect.

Again, this is ONLY the first surface test.

One last test before vacation:

[IMAGE: blocktest2.jpg]

This is a test to look at options for construction breakup and detail. The materials  used here overall are less substantial than what will be used on the real article, but useful for me to test technique.

I used toothpics, slivers of aluminum foil and fine guage wire to depict superstructure girders, supports and stringers and a little creative paint and pastel powder work to break up the edges more.

The toothpics are a little large to my eye and will probably not be used on the real deal. Split flat toothpics in places and lots of metal will be used.

I composited in light sources in Photoshop, as well as changed the lighting to simulate the filming miniature under effects lighting. I also added just a touch of grain.

Overall, I'm pleased, but I need to refine some methods. The real article will have more detail than this test.

In 1992 and 93.

Here's the funny part. Just before I joined the Navy in '88, I built my younger brother a model of the Nimitz and told him that's the ship I'll be on. Of course anyone who's been in the Military or who is a dependent knows this ain't how things work. You go where you get orders to go...

Before I did a WestPAC on Nimitz, I did two MED ( Mediterranean Sea ) Deployments on Nimitz' sister, the USS Dwight D. Eisenhower ( CVN 69 )... so I missed Nimitz by just one boat and hull number.... then I had a friend who was on Nimitz leave the Navy, so I put in a transfer request to take his spot... and it was approved... and that's how I got the Nimitz.

What was also funny is that even while I was on IKE, I had another friend on the Nimitz buy me one of the Nimitz ballcaps from the ship's store there... a cap I still wear proudly today 17 years later and from which I made my current avatar... So when I got transfered to CVN 68, I already had the correct attire. That's my story.... 

And why did I want assigned to Nimitz? Because of The Final Countdown, of course.

Oh... my signature? It's an inside joke we male Sailors have. WAVES are female Sailors...  :love
