I got into a very short mildly above room-temperature exchange with a fellow on one of the threads in this section yesterday and that got me to thinking... After I stopped thinking about the cost of that much boiling  oil for my somewhat complicated and overly-fussing revenge scheme I changed my trail of thought and got to the core of the issue:

To wit - What are we looking for in a "Studio Scale" model? Are we looking for the finest representation of the vehicle or prop or whatever that can be made? Are we looking for the closest possible re-creation of the original filming model, warts and all? Or are we just looking to make these ships in the same size as the filming model?

I have been reading with some enthusiasm form various sites on here and other segments of the forum, notably "General Modeling" and find that the goals are usually very divergent. In one case one person wants to build a model that will wrap in all the filmed elements of the show and essentially turn it into a museum-styled piece that far exceeds the detail of any filmed model exterior, by having a complete interior that actually works to scale inside. 

In another case, the same model is being re-created with a goal toward following as close as possible the original filmed model, even though in that particular case the builder's model almost certainly doesn't allow for some of the intricate mechanics of the model in question needed for filming. 

This is in no way a complaint. I am not in a position to tell anyone how he or she must do things. I'm really following the advice of one of the many awesome artists here and not hijacking someone else's thread to go off in this direction. 

I'd love to see what everyone has in mind? 

Are you trying to go for Real or are you looking to go for Memorex?

Honestly to go back to the original premise of what is studio scale...

The answer is making the model as close to the original as possible in terms of size, and proportion... If you only consider the terms "Studio Scale" 

But This Place (one of the first to get into this area) has LONG defined studio scale to be as close to the original in size, proportion AND detailing... Implying that where possible use the original kits. 

Now in all the fantastic "Studio Scale" builds this place has seen there are very VERY few that have identified 100% and acquired 100% of the origianal parts. So to say its not studio scale unless all the parts are found and used is a bit extreme IMHO. BUT - to be considered "Studio Scale" here - reasonable attempts to use the original parts should be made. A kitbash that "looks the part" is not what it generally means to be Studio scale -here- as good and difficult as that kitbash may be. Its been understood that this type of model belongs in the general modeling forum.

Now please understand as I say this - I do not hold one forum above the other - I hit both regularly... usually in succession when I hit this site. But in each I am looking for different things. One I'm looking for models, and the other I'm looking for studio replicas - or as close as we can get.

Its not even a question of skill and quality... Heck I think it can be reasonable stated that most of RELs (picked you because you fit my premise and you post in both places regularly) models are replicas of their subject - but not all of them are studio scale, which is why some of his best work is over in the general modeling forum
