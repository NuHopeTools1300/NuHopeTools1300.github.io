That top picture from the Bandai book is the Mr. Fasano maquette under bright flash lighting.Apparently, that was also the piece that was broken when shipped overseas to an exhibition.The one dimue mentioned was insured for $250,000.

I found mention of it on the statue forum from the guy who worked at ADI and maintained it and others.He also mentions about the Micheal Burnette piece and the SS version.

Well, to clear everything up about the SS piece, the Burnett piece, etc...

The Burnett piece and the SS piece are actually the EXACT same thing, a re-posed version of the maquette/rod-puppet sculpt that ADI did for Alien 3. Fact is, you can't get any more movie accurate than this sculpt because it IS the movie sculpt (the rod-puppet). It has been re-posed into what you are looking at in the pics. The original stood on two legs with arms fairly straight up. Sort of a upright/vertical "superman" flying pose.

As for the color, it's almost exact to movie accurate. Having worked at ADI for years, and having done touch ups on one of these pieces that was given to John Fasano (the A3 screenwriter) and got broken, I can say with 100% certainty... color... near dead on. That said, there's very little chance in the world that the factory paint job will ever live up to what you're seeing here. You wouldn't know it by looking at the pics, but this is a Gino Acevedo designed paint scheme that would actually make the Predator paint scheme look somewhat simple by comparison.
