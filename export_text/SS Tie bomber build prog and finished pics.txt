Hi all,

Prolly shudda added this to the other thread but here goes anyway..

Just a few pics of my buildup, found about 90-95% of the kits after reading through the original thread and after some advise from a couple of members here, and over at SS builder.

Saturn 5 parts for rear of ship

[IMAGE: Tieasmall.jpg]

[IMAGE: Tiebsmall.jpg]

This ring was for the engraving of the tubes, a tip I found on this forum, can't remember who posted that , but thanks a lot whoever that was.

[IMAGE: Tiecsmall.jpg]

[IMAGE: Tiedsmall.jpg]

Wings with added styrene edges, and resin copied parts, these were a pain to do especially the twenty odd parts from the Morser Karl,(there's only four in the kit) I had difficulty getting castings with no bubbles in the sticking out bits....must have casted about fifty to get enough usable ones Aarrghhh !!. worth it in the end though.

[IMAGE: Tieesmall.jpg]

[IMAGE: Tiefsmall.jpg]

[IMAGE: Tiegsmall.jpg]

I built a styrene center box and added the kit bits to that, I found the chronicals book very useful as the front view in that book is fairly close the finished size.

[IMAGE: Tiehsmall.jpg]

[IMAGE: Tieismall.jpg]

[IMAGE: Tiejsmall.jpg]

this is a scratched part for under the center box, to save buying another kit, I took measurements from the original part.

[IMAGE: Tieksmall.jpg]

Almost there..

[IMAGE: TieLsmall.jpg]

Didn't have a clear pic of this area, I only knew that the airfix harrier part needed to go there, it looked like there were some other bits and pieces as well so I just stuck on what looked about right. I also had to scratch a few more parts using kit parts for reference. Saves buying two more kits..

[IMAGE: Tiemsmall.jpg]

The rear..

[IMAGE: Tiepsmall.jpg]

[IMAGE: Tieosmall.jpg]

Finished !

[IMAGE: Tiensmall.jpg]

Looks a bit more blue in real life and matches close to the pics in the chronicals book...well to me anyway.

I was surprised how much went into such a small model, I thought it would be a fairly quick build when I started, but it took a while.

Let me know what you think, thanks for looking.

Must make a start on the falcon next..

Cheers

Phil
