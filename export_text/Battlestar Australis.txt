Thanks guys. I originally was going to use  as many castings as possible but fell into the collecting actual kits web. The only castings on this so far are the Roco 215's on the bottom slopes & the B52 parts that sit inside the open Roco 215's on the upper slopes. Everything else is a kit part. Luckilly I had some really lucky scores on the Roco's & some have been re-released by HERPA. the only Roco not re-released is the Z211 & they aren't going to reproduce that item unfortunately. I still need 6. I can cast the 4 Ive got but...will probably just keep searching until they come up. Its a very expensive build unfortunately. The armature is made of Stainless Steel. Heres a few more pics & some pics of the first castings I did before I went nuts :wacko  I have a good buddy (Wombat) building one aswell so its sort of a group build. We're bouncing off each other between the two builds & theres already been plenty of reworking:cry. We've also built a pretty good little Battlestar builders group at the site which has helped a huge amount with alot of sharing. Thanks for the feedback. Its fun inbetween the stress.:eek

Have built the neck & arms after some time off the project on other things. Using the new skills of phot scaling I have learnt am now working primarilly from scaled photos & kit parts. Build is preogressing nicely now with the confidence of using these new techniques I have learnt. Am about to start the head & Landing Bays very soon. I will not detail models before base structures are finished any more as flipping the models to work can be very hazardous. Just a learning curb as I am still a beginner. I discovered that the neck(and most of this studio model) are asymetrical meaning not even on port & starboard side in many places. I have incorporated these inacuracies into my build following the scaled photos as closely as possible. Thanks for looking
