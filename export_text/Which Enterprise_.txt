Banking on a mass produced collectible as being an investment is like playing the stockmarket.  You may win, you may not.  No guarantees.  Kind of like Star Wars figures.  In 1977....when EVERY kid was ripping them out of the package...finding one mint on card 20 years later was a rarity.  When the new SW figures came out...everyone was buying them as investments....and...well look what happened there.

Like posted above...if the A is your ship...then get the A.  I buy pieces for my prop collection because I like them. Not for investments.  I have a financial advisor when I need advice on that stuff. 

If you love the E-A (and it is a beautiful ship) you will be very happy you did.  And if it comes out 1/2 as nice as you are describing it...you will never regret it.
