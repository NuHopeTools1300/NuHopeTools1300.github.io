I think I'm going nuts? I have only 2 more days before I go back to work do I just had to paint something...its been driving me mad not painting for this long. Thankfully My E wing arrived last week so I thought that this must be the easiest/quickest build, due to talented duo that made the kit. I was going to go all weird & paint it like a guitar, then an X wing, then a Mad Max style interceptor, then I thought I wonder If this would look good Like Slave 1........ Lets find out I thought. I started kit bashing & doing a few custom mods here & there just to get the feel of it & to give it my own unique look. All the paint work was started this afternoon so I am motoring on this to get as much done as I can. Its a base coat of a vauxhall off white rattle, then a weird green Ford color in a rattle & all the rest is mixed from my Tamiya stock. I have done all this so far with out looking at Slave ref, I wanted to know how well I knew it in my head, the markings obviously dont follow the real Slave 1, but I am trying to capture the feel of the ship in this E wing. Its also extremely good practice incase I ever do manage to get one from our resident casting guru ?????? 

This is ment to be fun & a good way of learning new stuff, I always try & do new things when I paint & whilst my Y is on hold for a short while I shall try & finnish this up. Hope it doesn't look too weird????

The colors dont come through in the light at home so I will take some daylight shots when I get a chance.

Re: Firespray E wing hybrid

Well I'm glad to say I finally got a chance to get this wrapped up today. Its mounted on a pana 6 inch extension with no adjustable head, as it's quite a small prop I though it looked better with less fuss underneath, I'd already driled the mount in the ship at a very slight angle so it display's sort of in flight???? I guess???? The base is a nice solid piece of American oak just painted black. Quite simple but it does look nice.

I've not added the pilot as I just don't to seem to like pilots in ships, or maybe I'm crap at painting them!!!!!!

I've added some decals from some Tamiya kits, & fitted & painted the gun so it will swivel 360% should it feel the need to blow up something behind it, like a cat or house plant!!!

Overall I'm pretty chuffed with the way this came out as I think it was a bit of a Random idea, but like I said it was more of a test for me to see how to paint a Slave 1.

Some of the weathering I would re do if I had the time but as I'm selling this I wont need to see it to wind me up
