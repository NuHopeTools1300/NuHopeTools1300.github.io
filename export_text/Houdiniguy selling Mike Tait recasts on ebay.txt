Firstly, this is not an attempt to discredit or destroy anyone's reputation on the RPF, it is simply information on a situation I have found myself in and wish to warn others about.

2 months ago, I won an ebay auction for a Studio Scale X-wing which was being sold by someone with the username tomg981. A little bit of internet investigation and it turns out that this is Tom Gallo AKA Houdiniguy.

The SS X-wing kit is one which I have been after for a while, however there was no indication as to whether it was a Mike Salzo kit or a Captain Cardboard version or a recast of either. Also, shipping overseas was not specified - so I asked the question and the response (as seen in the auction) was yes, it is a Salzo and yes he ships overseas.

I paid straight away and heard no more from the seller until two weeks later when the kit still wasn't marked as shipped. 

Tom assured me it had been shipped and that he would find me the tracking number. 

Nearly two weeks later... no kit and no tracking number.

After several more emails Tom replied;

Hello,

I have been unable to locate the tracking numbers for this. I  can

offer two options here for you. (Please check my feedback, I am an  hoenst

collector and seller all at 100%) I am highly concerned this has  not

arrived yet. I originally purchased 3 of these*, I will be more than  happy

to send you the final kit I have, get tracking numbers and expidite  the

shipping right to you. I of course will fully pay for the shipping  etc.

Last resort... I can refund the balance to you. Let me know aqnd again  my

sincere appologies on all of this.. Tom

* Bear that comment in mind for later...

I responded that I would cover the shipping costs if and when the replacement kit showed up.

Two weeks and several more unanswered emails later, I received a UPS tracking number. The kit was being shipped from Katy, Texas...

The seller resides in Tacoma, WA.

Another week later the kit arrives and it is obvious as soon as I opened the box that I have a recast on my hands, something which Mike Salzo himself confirmed. 

I started back-tracking through posts on the RPF and the name Mike Tait (fairly synonymous with recasting) kept coming up.

On a whim, I Googled 'Mike Tait, Katy, Texas' and the senders address matched that of the Google listing for Mike Tait on the shipping label on the recast which I received.

Except that the senders name on the label says Tom Gallo.

[IMAGE: IMG.jpg]

By now, it was far too late to open an ebayclaim and Tom did not respond to my final email letting him know how disappointed I was.

I think that now, that disappointment has progressed to a case of being severely pissed off, not just because I received a recast, but because I was lied to by Tom throughout the whole process.

If the latest feedback on his ebay profile is to be believed, that is now something that may be becoming a little more commonplace.

So, I guess that this ends a sorry little chapter in my ebay experience.

Tom, if you are reading this, I am still disappointed and if there is anything that would possibly placate me, it would be a full refund and maybe an apology.

I won't hold my breath for either.

Ok , firstly i am not having a go at you or your molds. There was a lot of problems with the rifles that were pulled from these molds ie air bubbles etc. A lot of people were very disapointed with what they recieved. Now that sounds like Mike Tait all over. Tait is not a very good mold maker and anyone who thinks he is needs to look at some of the other fine molds that have produced very nice casts on this forum.

I agree that it is not always the mold and has a lot to do with the person casting from it but some of the problems that arose from this run could have been avoided had a few simple steps been taken in preparing the molds to begin with.

I have molded everything from ww2 weapons to full body lifecasts and the smallest rivets to large machinery and one thing that is nearly always needed in this type of mold are channels for the air to escape. Especially in weapons like this. A few simple household drinking straws from the edge of the weapon to the edge of the mold placed every two inches would have forced the air from the mold as the resin is poured , thus eleviating air bubbles that would otherwise get traped in the detail parts which is what happened to some on the run that Tom/Tait did.

there is no reason why you can't get a perfect pull from these molds if you cut a small v channel in a few spots where the air is likely to get trapped. Had this been done by Tait there would not have been this problem in the beginning. Its one thing to have a mold sitting there looking pretty and all we can go by are the ones that were pulled from this already. Good luck with pouring yours and i hope you get some great advice from other members here to get the best results.

simon
