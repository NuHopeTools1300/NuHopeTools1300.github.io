Hi all! 

As mentioned in Al aka BigTurc's thread yesterday, I'm just starting out on my own build of a Salzo v3 kit. 

You all know what it looks like, but here's a pic of some of the parts, cause pictures are always nice, right? 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]

[IMAGE: xwing.jpg]

The thing is, this is the third X Wing studio scale kit I ordered. The other two I simply sold again without even touching them, as the whole endeavour just seemed too intimidating to me. 

I don't know what it is about those birds, seeing I'm not a total noob to model building (did my fair share in my teens, tanks and miniatures and whatnot), I know how to work with resin kits (built blaster kits, helmet greeblies, R2 parts, even cast a thing or two myself) and am generally not too bad at finishing things either (people who have seen my lifesize R2 will surely attest to that 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
)

So why am I so freaking scared of these birds? I don't know, but I finally decided it's time to man up and actually get this thing built, instead of hiding it away in the cupboard just to sell it on a few months later... again!

Mind you, I am not an expert on these models, not by a stretch, so I am hoping you guys will be able to help me get this done properly. I am trying to read up on everything here already, but on some things I might need some guidance, so I hope someone out there will be gracious enough to help me out whenever I feel lost 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
 

It won't be a quick build either as I have tons of other stuff going on (commission builds on some Stormtrooper suits, real life commitments, etc), but I will try and post whenever there is progress on it.

And one last thing: Since I realised some things are kinda idealised on the kit itself already, and I'm not even remotely familiar enough with these birds to do a proper uber-screen-accurate job, this ship will be an idealised model. It did come with a Hyperdyne lighting kit, so I'm planning on adding that to it and I'm pretty sure I'll go with the Luke pilot instead of the generic jet pilot guy.

Anyway, enough of my banter, let's get to the first progress pics!

I first started with cleaning up random parts, just to get my feet wet, not really knowing where to start... then it dawned on me. I am an R2 builder after all, so start with what you know, right? So I took the tiny droid and went to town with it! 

I knew I'd want lights in there, so a solid droid wouldn't work... so I first hollowed him out by drilling a pilot hole in there and then used my hand countersink to cut out all the rest: 

[IMAGE: r2x0.jpg]

Next I drilled holes through the front and back PSI, easy peasy!

[IMAGE: r2x.jpg]

Then I felt I didn't like that R2 would be constantly staring slightly to the left, so I took out my knife and spent a few minutes cutting through the lower dome line, then I added a piece of 0.5mm styrene to the body to build a lip for the dome to pop back on, a small extra piece added to the lip so it would be a nice and snug pressure fit and tadaa - now R2's dome is adjustable! 

[IMAGE: r2x2.jpg]

As you can also see, I also drilled a hole through the logic display panel, shaved the rest of the panel off and resculpted it back on with two tiny holes in it - not pictures is the slot I cut into the back logic display panel as well... 

But the dome needs all the lights it can get, right? Right? Or am I taking this too far? :unsure

That's it for now, I hope I'll have more to share soon, guess I will try and paint this little fella next. See how I'm still trying to leave the bird alone? I need encouragement folks 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
 

Oh yeah, I ordered a metal armature to go with this, which should be done and shipped to me very soon as well - I will nee some pointers on how to mount this properly so it will actually support the model 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]

Cheers guys, and sorry for this wall of text... I promise there will be more eye candy in the next updates!
