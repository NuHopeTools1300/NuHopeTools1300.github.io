That was Mark Dickson's casting that John Billings persuaded him to give over for restoration, because he just had to have it for his collection. It's approx 1.250" too short. Roger Sides did 2 "toaster castings" and the combo bond and fiberglass layup was pretty bad. Surprising it could be fixed at all because it had other major issues. 

If anyone could do it Ed could but I would hate to be Ed on that project. Looks really nice now. 

Mark has loads of pictures on that thing. And rubbings, and countours and everything needed to make an accurate new master 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]

Rapid prototyping a new master will ensure symmetry.
