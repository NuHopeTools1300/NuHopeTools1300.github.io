Hi All.  I'm the "infamous" MJ.   
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
 

I bought the patterns for this kit from their previous owner.  They originate from Icons and were originally cast from a prop by Icons for their Viper.  Since Icons 3 other artists have worked on the patterns to clean them up, and fix many of the Icons flaws, but not fix things to the point that it wouldn't be studio accurate.  I have been the most recent person to work on it and replaced a lot of the details on the kit that had become soft over the years in the side and engine section with original kit parts.  The Viper is 1/24th scale (or therabout) and close to 15" long.

Please feel free to ask me more questions and Ill do my best to answer.  Also, please feel free to email or PM me.  I'm a  big BSG fan and just having a blast being involved.

First, I'd like to say, Jestefarean, what a nice job you did reworking this, looks great.  Those of you that buy it, you will love it.

I got the 'same' kit from "Monsters in Motion" a while back. The panel lines were not that crisp, and the middle area of the engines where all those greebly details are were pretty worn down,  so this one looks much nicer. I put mine into a launch tube diorama, which made the effect even nicer.

The only thing I did like about the version I got, was the new engine cowls. They were very clean and straight. This one still has the distorted cowls, but as with so many of our replica builds, one has to decide if you want an exact studio replica (which generally results in a model with many 'flaws' from a traditional sense), or one that is an interpretation of an actual ship or a prop model.

Regardless, this is a very nice model, worthy of anyones time and collection.

My pictures aren't quite as sharp as MJ's, but if you'd like to compare, please visit here: http://squaremodels.netfirms.com/viper.htm

Again, nice work MJ..

Okay...number of things..

1: - The Icons Viper patterns were not created from a hero.  I don't have a hero model (I wish)  From what I know or have gathered, Icons used molds from a non-hero Viper and then got 4 very talanted modelers to fix it...it was a mess.  They used correct detail parts to bring her back to life.

2. The Icons patterns, and the cast of a Viper I have do not have the details on the nose listed below.  You will also find if you check lots of screen caps (as I have) that many Vipers don't carry that badge...and many other details.

That being said, Im not sure what "hump" your talking about.  There are two details you have circled.  One is a raised section on the side of the Viper.  This has puzzled me for a while as I have only caught it on screen caps from 1 Viper.  I believe this hero Viper is a bit different, but I don't know why, or what the detail looks like exactly.  The second more obvious point is the raised section in front of the canopy on the top.  This (if I have my history correct) is actually not a cover for the mount plug (which I believe to be flush) but is actually a cover for the original canopy frame detail.  Originally the canopy would have slid forward to open (like the new Viper) the cap covers detail they originall did and the most obvious raised section on your profile view is the track leading from the canopy.  I don't know why they changed the way the canopy mounted, but in the end they covered the detail.  Some of the Vipers seem to have the cap and rails more prounounced then others.  This could be wear, age, or perhaps the ones with the rails were the first ones, and the later ones don't bear that badge.

Check the 3 heros in the post from PHarchivist and youll notice they dont all have the same size bulge, or other details on the side...again...I can't explain it...and if someone could they would save me a lot of mental gyrations...Ive been trying to piece this mystery together for a while...but suffice to say its part of the "proof" that the heros were not identical...and for whatever reason they carry ideosyncracies that don't match up.
