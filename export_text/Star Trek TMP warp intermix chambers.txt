My recollection is that the TMP warp core was a fairly complex mechanical device that used Mylar, lamps and lasers in order to achieve the effect. Do keep in mind that it was created in 1978, long before CGI became available. The warp cores for TNG, Voyager, and Enterprise were, as far as I know, variations on the TMP mechanical effect - CGI in those days was prohibitively expensive and any solution that got something cool on screen without burning the VFX budget was welcomed. I'm pretty sure there's a detailed description of the TMP mechanism in the 1979 CineFX issue that dug into the ST:TMP production.  That said, I very much doubt that the original TMP effect can be replicated in miniature - it was built full size and physics pretty much dictate that it's impossible to replicate at a smaller size. If I were attempting to replicate the sets at a lesser scale then I'd be focused on experimentation - you're going to have to work out a suitable effect on your own.  Yeah, that sucks, but building reproductions isn't easy. Hoping you can cope with the inherent difficulties.                             Nick
