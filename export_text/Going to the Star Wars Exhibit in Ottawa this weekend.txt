A few months back I got some tips here about taking pictures of models behind glass when I went to the Science Meets Imagination tour.  I did not have a high quality camera, just a cheapie point and shoot Nikon and Sony.

The tour did allow flash.  The best piece of advice I got was to press the camera lens straight up against the glass and it will cut down on reflections.  It worked like a charm. I got some great pics, with and without flash.  I also took a small flashlight that didn't work out too good for me, but I have seen others use the technique and get good results.

The other thing I found out on my own was that you don't need to zoom in for most things.  You are taking a picture from a foot or two away.  Even a cheap 12MP camera can get a good pic.

Here is a link to the pics I took.  My strategy was quality through quantity.  I got lots of good shots and I kept the poor ones because sometimes that is all you got of a certain angle.  

https://skydrive.live.com/redir?resid=B36510BA2591A1A4!169

Here is a link to the thread where I got the advice.  There are other good things there.

http://www.therpf.com/f10/help-getting-good-photos-props-museum-158725/

I hope it helps and I would love to see what you end up with.

Well I have to say that it was exciting and disappointing at the same time. There wasn't that many models there. I will post some pics tomorrow when I get to work for a better internet connection. The exhibit was called Star Wars Identities. Slave 1, TIE X1, TIE Bomber, TIE Interceptor, Red 3, B-Wing, 8 ft SD, Mon Cal Cruiser, 32" Falcon, AT-AT (not sure which one, it wasn't very big), Snowspeeder (big), Shuttle, and Standard TIE were all there. The catalog said Red Jammer was there but sadly it seems to have been damaged during set-up and was not out. Pity because I still have a few pipes to do and was really hoping to get a full view of her. I didn't get nearly as many pictures as I wanted because the crowds were fairly heavy and people were just rude. I only knocked 3 kids out of the way to get to the glass. There were people there pushing WAY more children than I did! The Falcon and Slave 1 were in their own case but the rest were all in one large case with strobing/flashing lights! I had to try to time the flashes to get a decent picture. And the model arrangement made it difficult to get certain angles or close ups. Just a big money grab unfortunately. I did see lots of cools stuff for props and costumes though and all in all I did see something that I will likely never get a chance to experience again. I'll put them on my Photobucket account tomorrow for all to see. Sorry about the Probe Droid and Landspeeder. They must be somewhere else at the moment...
