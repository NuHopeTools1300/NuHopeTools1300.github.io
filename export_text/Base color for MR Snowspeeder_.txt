Tamiya XF-19 with flat white - off the top of my head, maybe 15% white? It's a very close match. The MR ones were painted and the red was pad printed. I have some of the dry transfers left over from when I did the paint masters - you need some? They printed them in a bunch of different red hues - I think all I have left, spare-wise, are the darker maroon-ish ones, which you could rub down, and then paint back over with a brighter red.

Hey Gerardo

Yeah, after looking through my jars I would agree with Moffeaton that XF19 is close if lightened a bit with white. I wonder if it's a bit colder in colour though than the MR speeder shade, and maybe a bit of XF55 deck tan would warm it up a little bit to be a closer match. Maybe a drop or two of Buff in the mix might work instead, but only a drop 'cos it's darker than the Deck Tan, and may send it too warm.

I had a look through tins that I haven't seen for ages though, and found an old tin of humbrol 129 satin us gull grey and after holding the tin lid next to my speeder it looks pretty much exact. I suppose when it's sprayed it may not be exact to the lid, but it looks really close.

I'm not sure how easy all the Humbrol colours are to find now, as they've had problems, and I haven't bought any for a while. But there must be some stores that still have it. It's worth a look if you haven't started yet. 

As usual in this hobby of matching someone else's model the fun is in the trial and error....Lol

Anyway there you go
