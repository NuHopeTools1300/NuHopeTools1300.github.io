The models size was determined thru the usual method of matching kit parts to enlargements. I also made a full size rendering from the side-profile pic of the studio model thats found in the center foldout of the Chronicles book. 

Happily, the numbers I came up with match those stated by ILM - 97.25 inches

[IMAGE: O2100853copy.jpg]

Monogram I-I9 hull makes for a good size reference 

[IMAGE: O2100842copy2.jpg]

I'm doing the forward body the same as the studio model. Vac-formed the upper half and the lower is resin over foam. The sub and Yamato hulls are taped on just to see how things are measuring out so far.

[IMAGE: O2100848copy.jpg]

 

[IMAGE: O2100849copy.jpg]

Armature is 1/8 aluminum pipe. Heavy, but guaranteed not to flex. 

[IMAGE: O2100852copy.jpg]

L brackets welded on for attaching the rear body. The same for up front.

[IMAGE: O2100850copy.jpg]

Two Hasegawa 1/450 Yamato hulls serve as platforms for all the structure and parts that make up the rear of the main body. To determine the hulls placement and the necessary alterations for the lower hull, I first had to ID some parts. 

I had a hunch this "bump" was a boat hull. 

[IMAGE: KG_MED-FRIGATE-%20011%20copy%203.jpg]

After an exhaustive months long hunt, it turnes out to be the old Lightship kit by Frog.

[IMAGE: UPC%205012-400%20Goodwinfr%20copy.JPG]

[IMAGE: O2101584%20copy.JPG]

[IMAGE: O2101583%20copy.JPG]

[IMAGE: O2101591%20copy.JPG]

The lower Yamato hull is heavily modified, to figure it all out, I needed these two grill looking things to start with.
