  Gene Kozicki recently posted this on FB about that TIE with the sticker: Early on in the VFX planning, it was thought that the star fields would be added to the space shots using a technique called Front Projection (FP). Though an older technique, it was popularized on "2001" and Dykstra had used it in a similar manner on "Silent Running". Though it allows for in-camera compositing, it is cumbersome to set up and requires a LOT of pre-planning. And the type of movements that could be done with an FP rig were probably limited. FP tests were done (this is a clip from one), but eventually they decided to go "all in" with the blue screen comp/MOCO technique.  Also note that this early TIE fighter has a pentagon emblem on the lower left hull. While the TIE would eventually have no such markings, the Cylon Raider from "Battlestar Galactica" would have large ones on its wings.
