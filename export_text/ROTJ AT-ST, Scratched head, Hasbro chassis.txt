The head width is allright, but its to short, making it have proportions more like the ESB version.

So I started to plan for completing the SS head first, and use the Hasbro legs, while I build the armature, wich is something I think will take a while to sort out.

Re: ROTJ AT-ST, Hasbro body

Ok, so started on the legs and chassis. Gonna go through what you need to do to make it better. Its pretty straightforward work. Take it all apart, there are a few parts, so keep track on them. Fill the lowest leg part with putty. Cut the area above the joint (where the lower legs meet the middle.

[IMAGE: atst4.JPG]

Next make an inside plate to the top leg out of thin styrene and glue it in place. Cut out the plastic inside the small details below the front joint.

[IMAGE: atst5.JPG]

Cut the plastic where the toes goes in the feet. 

[IMAGE: atst6.JPG]

I also started to cut at the front of the chassis. Ill post more about that later.

Funny note. One of the AT-STs thats on tour, has broken leg details. The Hasbro has these broken details sculpted, lol.

I am feeling rather silly for continuing posting, given how poor the feedback is.:confused But when I started this project I planned to make step by step instructions if anyone felt like having a go, I know I learned tons just by reading other ppls thread, so Im sticking to the plan.

Ok, here is the detail from the bottom of the Vader TIE.

[IMAGE: atstfot1.jpg]

And here it is together with the mounted claw. I used an aluminum rod, to make the holding part longer. Styrene details, and some details from the original Hasbro foot.

[IMAGE: atstfot2.jpg]

The original studio model have really dirty feet. I still remember the surprice I felt when seeing the AT-ST when the exhibition was in Sweden some years back. It looks like the did it this way. Mix white glue with water, sand, fake grass from the model shop and mix it so its pretty thin, more like dirty water, than thick glue. Then paint it on with a brush. If mixed right, it will work like a paint and will dry pretty quickly.

[IMAGE: atstfot3.jpg]

On the studiomodel they painted it afterwards. This will seal the glue and stuff into place, and will be painted to look like dirt again later on. Here is a before and after pic of it next to one on my sons AT-ST.

[IMAGE: atstfot4.jpg]

Jp, I wouldnt call it a toymod, as the head is based on the original go-motion model and I will replace the chassies when I manage to build a new one. :love The hasbro chassies is in good scale so I think of it as practice and something to put the head on. I agree about what can be done with the Hasbro stuff though. Very fun and relaxing to work with. :thumbsup Im also a little curious how it will look.

Ive been busy with the ST the last week. When I started this I wanted the wonky eyes and the dishes to look right, as I think they are the two major things that sells the ROTJ look. 

I vac-formed the dishes in a round shape, but I had to redo them alot untill I felt they were ok. A pretty weird shape to get right. 

There was some fiddelilg with different thickness of the spacers between the head and the side armor. I tried to get them wonky like the one Im basing it on. The armor at the front/side is really weird on the original. It looks like it may be removable, maybe for making the go-motion filming easyer. I think Ive got the basic shape, but its by no means 100%.

Ok, so here is some stuff for those planning to mod their Hasbro ST. Its really fun to work with this.

I filled the feet and made mounting holes. I will fixate the position, so it will be steady for the head. The head will be easily removable, and easy to rework for another chassis when I manage to build one.

[IMAGE: atst%20feet1.jpg]

Then I removed the small plates in the first leg joint. It was kind of tricky so go slow with this part as its easy to screw up. If you do, you will have to buy a new one. 

[IMAGE: atst%20ben1.jpg]

I got the plates loose, cleaned up the joint, and strenghten the inside with CA-glue and baking soda. After that I glued some plastic tubes in the right diameter. Finally I added some wheels from a tank kit that looks pretty close to the original ST. I the started to add styrene and metal details to try to make it more like the original model. I am really impressed with the detailing of the legs made by Hasbro. Someone must have alot of love for SW there. Alot is not bad at all. There are alot of gaps from the construction that needs to be filled.

[IMAGE: atst%20ben2.jpg]

[IMAGE: atst%20ben3.jpg]

[IMAGE: atst%20ben4.jpg]

[IMAGE: atst%20ben5.jpg]

Now I have a lot of gaps to fill between the old and new parts to do. Cheers

Cheers mate! Its a he btw. 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]

It sounds like you are using styrene model glue and it will only work with styrene. The hasbro toys are made of ABS (anyone correct me if Im wrong) and styrene glue doesnt melt it (as it does with styrene pieces).

I use CA, often in a combination with baking powder. It has a good grip and when mixed with baking powder it dries instantly. You need to sand it pretty quick after applied, it gets as hard as a rock when fully cured. Its also great for filling seams and gaps. 

It takes a while to get used to, but its the way that Ive found best. 

I only used epoxy glue for the neck/head connection.

Something to consider when working on the Hasbro stuff is how to get a good surface. I do the mods in steps, I do some work, then add putty ehre needed and sand it to a surface, then primer, then sand again, and the do more work and repeat the proccess... to get a smooth surface with all the details on. 

The backside is that the parts are glued on the primer coat and the primer can fall off. It takes alot of stress before doing so, so its not really a problem.
