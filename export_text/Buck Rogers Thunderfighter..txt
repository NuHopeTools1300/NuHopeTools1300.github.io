Not highjacking, just posting what mine came out looking like, based on the instructions above.

Both Erin Grey and Gil Gerard saw it when they signed my bases, at Starfest god how many years ago?  Both said it was pretty spot on color wise.  Both had their personal ones given to them after the wrap.  Gil made a big deal about his having the pilot name on the canopy.  Never seen proof of that, but I ain't doubting.

 Gil's, sadly, is rotting in a storage unit near Malibu when these pics were taken, and though I was promised that he would "dust it off" and get some pics no amount of bugging his publicist will remind him of that.

Erins is even worse off, she has it, but made the mistake of putting it directly over a heater radiator . . . can you say pretzel?  No pics of that one either, and I tried so I could see the underbelly and what the paint looks like today.  No joy.

Mind you, its still in gloss, in the basement, waiting weathering and adding the cannon tips in. And the "new"(er?) engines from George that need to be fitted and painted. 

To be honest, this ******* is part of what broke me out of SS kitting, literally.  Its awesome to see one getting built, but it still hurts in a lot of ways. I've said it before, and I'm repeating it now.   I loved doing it, but after losing my ass on the SS Thunderfighter, which I  was in with Steve N on, I said enough is enough. We paid a lot for a crappy pull of a original in nasty black resin, and spent a lot more money trying to get it up to snuff to mold, and then a crap-ton on rubber to mold it in a more forgiving resin than the black crap, fix THAT, and then REMOLD.  I tell anyone who has dreams of becoming a GK'er this  little tidbit I learned the hard way. We had a "list" of 75 People who  "committed" to buying the SS Thunderfighter for 400-450. We went forward with that in mind, and I invested in half on that project. We were able to  sell it for $400, the bottom end of the scale, (Remember this is the  kit, above and in this thread, the MAIN HULL on it was over a GALLON of resin!) and we were  able to sell in the first 2 YEARS after making it available,  I think about 18. I pulled 2 additonals for myself and a friend, and  I do not know how many Steve's sold since then, but  I'm guessing its a not much higher number than 20. So, out of 75, call  it, generously, 40-45, so less than half PUT THERE MONEY WHERE THERE MOUTH  IS. And a more than a few of those sales were NOT from people on the LIST!

I'm glad George is giving this one a reboot.  I'd love to get one of his when it comes around, and I probably will as I'm such a sucker for Universal Heartland/MCA77 goodness. I can always have Buck and Wilma's side by side, ya know?

Keep up the good work on yours!  Awesome to see an older "topic" that I though was dead get so many comments and so much interest.  And for those looking for more reference . . . forum search Thunder fighter, and starfighter, you'll find some goodness.

Thanks for that background info. I am sorry to hear you had such a hard time with this project. Unfortunately, the flake factor with people is pretty high. I even posted a thread about it once...something like: "WHY do people tell you they will buy something and then disappear??"

They will post: "I AM SOOO IN!!! TELL ME WHEN YOU WANT THE MONEY!!!"

Then you make the model, e-mail or PM them, and the person can't even bother to reply to repeated correspondence after that. Huh? Wha?

ONE guy finally did reply to me once on a specific project and I think he summed it up nicely: (paraphrasing):  

"I look over the JY and pick out all of the stuff that I really want and post to each thread that I am interested. LATER...I go back and pay for the couple that I can actually afford. I ignore the rest..."

Welcome to the modern world! :lol  

Anyway...

I got my kit directly from Jim Key about 12 years ago. It is supposedly a first generation, direct pull off one of the original models. Looking over the reference, it appeared to be about 98% correct, but there were 4 or 5 teeny discrepencies (missing details), which I had to scratch-build/ fix. (Not sure why they would be missing on a direct pull, unless the models varied).  

And the panel lines were pretty wonky. I had to rescribe EVERY line on the model. Some were so bad that I had to fill them in and do them over again. The engines were slightly "ovalled" on my casting, so I had to rebuild the fronts and I hollowed them out to add the LEDs. Other that THAT, the casting was extremely clean and straight with no warping or distortion.

The nav lights are bulbs. I routed wiring through the model to get to the wingtips. They have little access panels so they can be replaced if they burn out.  

It weighs 8 1/2 pounds! So your point about a GALLON of resin is spot-on! It is exactly 22 1/4 inches long.    

Do you know if Jim pulled his kit off of the same model you guys used?

So I am looking at the OP model. Is it REALLY that tan? Did I paint mine WRONG??!! :facepalm

In the pics I posted, mine really does look white, but the ivory color is much more evident in-person. But the OP pics have me concerned. They are clearly WAY more tan. I thought I did this right. :lol

Is there any correct original color reference? 

AND on the quad-fighter, my impression is that they just built a shell and painted the window areas black to cover over the single canopy. So that is what I did on my conversion. I painted the whole thing black, masked off the windows and built up many coats of filler-primer for the framework, so it is actually raised above the window areas.

I hope I got that right... Is there any more info on that detail on the original?

Those signed bases are FANTASTIC!!!! If you ever want to sell one, let me know!!! 

LOVE this ship!!! Thanks for the great info and pics guys!
