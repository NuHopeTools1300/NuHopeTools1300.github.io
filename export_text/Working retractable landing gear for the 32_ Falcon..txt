Part of the problem as I see it is that based on the photos I've seen, there's really no way for landing gear to work on a hypothetical real ship.  Pictures of landing gear down make it look like the "foot" is bigger than the hole, and that doesn't even account for any kind of hinge system for the door.  In fact, it seems to me that the pics from the movie don't really show gear doors, either.  I guess they disappear into the ship, which compounds the whole issue.  From an engineering standpoint, on a real full size ship, they way it's been presented in the movies, it's just not possible.  Then, trying to put that in the context of a 32-inch model, makes it even more of a problematic.

But, then, oh, I've seen some of those videos of RC jets with working everything, and I don't know.

Thanx newmagrathea,

yes this is quite an exceptional build,  his belly gun control in 1/72 scale is amazing.  He provided me the inspiration to scrap the poorly designed lift system on the deago 32" and rebuild the lift unit so it allows clear access to the interior and works similar to the screen models with synchronized lifting of the center two rams and spring tension on open/closure.  Also the belly gun is going to be so much easier at 1/43 scale.  I have a simple programmable 12 servo landing gear/door controller for the project,  just need to figure how to compress the landing gear without changing the look of the landing gear and legs.   Double doors are easy, a two wing folding and a swing door combo is the challenge for the rear two double feet assemblies.  Lots of parts purchase, sketches, ideas, and research,  but need a few more months of subscriptions to have the model far enough to safely begin frame mods.  Have changed all the Phillips head frame assembly screws to SS M2x4mm and M2x5mm button head and standard head hex socket screws so I could actually tighten them properly.  I lot easier to handle a H1.3 hex driver,  screws stay on until you start them.
