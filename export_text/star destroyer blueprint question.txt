A topic close to my heart!!

I've spent many fruitless hours searching for an accurate bluepront, they simply don't exist.  You can find some half decent graphics on the web but they all seem to have one problem area.

I've been using the elevations from "star wars the essential guide to vehicles and vessels" enlarged to about A2 size. They're not bad but i've cross referenced with lots of other sources because as you enlarge the pic, it can get a little fuzzy and measurements become aproximate.

If you have the budget, "star wars the blueprints" may have something better but my pocket never stretched that far :cry

What scale are you going to build? I may have some useful dimensions you can use.

I have to agree with Omes72, there are no accurate plans available.  You're better off finding some reference images on the net (side profile, top/bottom profile, elevations, etc.) and make your own plans.  Using software like Photoshop, to clean up the images and convert them to templates and saving as an .eps file, and Illustrator import the .eps file and use the templates to draw from.

I used the Star Wars: Essential guide to vehicles and vessels,... and I can tell you, the outline drawing of the Star Destroyer is just plain wrong when compared to the studio model.
