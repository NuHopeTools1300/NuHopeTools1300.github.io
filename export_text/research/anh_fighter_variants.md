# ANH Fighter Miniature Variant Table

## Scope

- Film scope: `Star Wars: Episode IV - A New Hope` only.
- Physical scope: real `ILM` miniatures and immediate pre-production/reference builds tied to `ANH`.
- Workshop scope: the `Van Nuys` era build program, not in-universe ship lore.
- Excluded unless noted: `ESB`/`ROTJ` redresses, later replicas, and `Vader's TIE Advanced` as a separate ship class.

## Working Method

- I treated `X-Wing`, `Y-Wing`, and `TIE Fighter` differently because the evidence quality is not equal.
- `X-Wings` are the most reconstructable as named individual builds or at least named physical lineages.
- `Y-Wings` are moderately reconstructable, but the line between "hero," "high-finish filming miniature," and later fan naming is blurrier.
- `TIE Fighters` are the least reconstructable ship-by-ship for `ANH`; the evidence supports distinct physical states and subtypes better than a clean census of individually named craft.

## Confidence Guide

- `High`: multiple strong sources align, or a source is unusually direct.
- `Medium`: likely correct, but still partly inferential or dependent on specialist reconstruction.
- `Low`: plausible working hypothesis, but not yet pinned down cleanly.

### X-Wing takeaways

- The strongest current reconstruction is `4` finished hero X-wings plus an unfinished fifth hero lineage.
- ILM says there were `9` pyros, but only some of those can currently be assigned stable markings with confidence.
- The most important confusion trap is treating every `Red 3` reference as the same physical miniature. At least one `Red 3` state belongs to the surviving `Red 1 pyro` lineage, not the bespoke `Red 3 hero`.
- `Red 4` is secure as a pyro identity. `Red 4` as a separate hero build is not secure from the evidence I reviewed.

### Y-Wing takeaways

- `Red Jammer` is best treated as a real ANH-era physical build, but not a confirmed screen-used ANH ship.
- `Gold Leader / TIE Killer` is the most securely documented hero Y-wing.
- The exact relationship between `Gold Leader / TIE Killer`, `Tiger Sprockets`, and the `red stripe blue artoo` archive ship is still not fully settled.
- I do think a useful ANH Y-wing table is possible, but not yet a perfectly closed census with the same certainty as the X-wing heroes.

### TIE takeaways

- I do **not** think the current evidence supports a precise ship-by-ship ANH TIE census comparable to the X-wing hero table.
- I **do** think it supports a precise table of:
  - pre-final test state(s)
  - standard plain-hull ANH filming family
  - a few distinct marked or damaged photographed states
  - confirmed pyro subtypes
- Put differently: `X-Wings` are best reconstructed as individual lineages, `TIEs` as identifiable physical states.

## Bottom Line

- `X-Wings`: yes, a strong ANH-only table is possible now.
- `Y-Wings`: yes, but with a visible uncertainty band around exact hero/stunt classification and some screen-role assignments.
- `TIE Fighters`: yes, if we define the goal carefully as a table of `ANH physical miniature states and subtypes`, not a definitive list of individually named ships.

## Suggested Next Pass

If we continue from here, the most useful refinement would be:

1. Split the `X-Wing` rows into `physical lineage` and `paint identity state` so that `Red 1 -> Red 3` and `Red 6 -> Red 10` are even clearer.
2. Refine the dedicated `Y-Wing conflict matrix` for the overlap between `Gold Leader / TIE Killer`, `Tiger Sprockets`, and `red stripe blue artoo`.
3. Create a `TIE photo index` keyed to published book/exhibit shots, because the TIE problem is really an image-correlation problem more than a nomenclature problem.
