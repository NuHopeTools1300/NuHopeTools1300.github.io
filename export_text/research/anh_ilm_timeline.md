# ANH ILM Van Nuys Timeline - First Pass

## Scope

- Film scope: `Star Wars: Episode IV - A New Hope` only.
- Place scope: primarily the original `ILM` facility at `6842 Valjean Avenue, Van Nuys, California`.
- Time scope: from the formation of `ILM` in `1975` through `Star Wars` release and immediate period documentation in `1977`.

## What This Timeline Is

- a chronology of `shop formation`, `staffing`, `design/build milestones`, `reference exports to England`, `miniature-photography anchors`, `principal-photography context`, and `late optical/composite crunch`
- a first-pass research tool, not yet a closed day-by-day production log

## What This Timeline Is Not

- not a list of `when a finished shot appears in the movie`
- not yet a fully closed log of `exact filming dates` for each miniature
- not a claim that every row is equally exact

## Evidence Bands

- `Exact`: tied to a precise printed date or a source with an exact historical date.
- `Range`: bounded to a month, season, or clearly described period.
- `Inference`: plausible placement built from multiple sources, but not yet dated by a direct primary document.

## What Already Looks Strong

- `Late May` through `August 1975`: formation of `ILM`, warehouse acquisition, early hiring, department setup
- `September-December 1975`: early miniature design and the first concrete reference-build exports to `England`
- `March 1976`: by the time principal photography begins in `Tunisia`, miniature / motion-control work is already active in `Van Nuys`
- `Spring 1976 onward`: the Y-wing program and the broader production-line miniature ecosystem are in motion
- `1976-1977`: the shop evolves from build-and-test chaos into a combined build / stage / optical delivery pipeline

## What Is Still Weak Or Open

- exact dates for filming most specific miniatures on the Van Nuys stages
- exact dates for `Falcon`, `Blockade Runner`, `Death Star surface`, `Landspeeder`, `Sandcrawler`, and `Escape Pod` construction milestones beyond the few dated anchors above
- who built which subassemblies on many models after the earliest phases
- a clean distinction between `first completed miniature`, `first miniature photographed`, and `first finished ILM shot`
