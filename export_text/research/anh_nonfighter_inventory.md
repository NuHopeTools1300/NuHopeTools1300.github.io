# ANH Non-Fighter Miniature Inventory

## Scope

- Film scope: `Star Wars: Episode IV - A New Hope` only.
- Workshop scope: primarily `ILM` miniature work tied to the `Van Nuys` model shop program.
- This document covers non-fighter ships, vehicles, Death Star miniatures, and special miniature sections.
- I keep full-size live-action props and set builds separate unless they directly clarify the miniature program.

## Working Rule

- `Count floor` means the lowest number of distinct physical miniature objects or object families that the evidence currently supports.
- This is not yet a full closed census.
- Some rows are `single object` rows and others are `object family` rows.

## Confidence Guide

- `High`: strong direct support from official, auction, or unusually good specialist evidence.
- `Medium`: very plausible, but still partly reconstructive.
- `Low`: good candidate, but not yet firmly closed.

## Read On Your Remarks

- `Landspeeder`: absolutely belongs in scope, but the count is still open. Right now I can only defend `at least one` ILM miniature.
- `Blockade Runner`: yes, and this is already one of the clearer non-fighter cases. I think the floor is `two` real ANH miniatures: one large and one `16-inch` small model.
- `Death Star itself`: yes, clearly.
- `Sandcrawler`: yes, clearly.
- `Death Star tiles`: yes, and this needs to be treated as a **system** of many unique pieces, not one generic base.
- `Death Star laser turret`: yes, secure.
- `trench crane` and `silo`: yes, I think these deserve their own rows.
- `Death Star hangar / gantry`: yes, likely a real special miniature section and worth tracking separately.
- `big radar dish`: possible, but I do not yet have enough evidence to state it cleanly as its own ANH miniature row.
- `Escape pod launch pod / launcher`: plausible, but still needs cleaner evidence to know whether it was its own miniature, a rig, or a set support element.
