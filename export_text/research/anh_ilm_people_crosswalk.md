# ANH ILM People Crosswalk

## Scope

- Film scope: `Star Wars: Episode IV - A New Hope` only.
- Place scope: primarily the original `ILM` site in `Van Nuys`, with a few bridge figures at the edges of that pipeline.
- Purpose: map `who did what`, `when`, and at what level of certainty.

## Working Rule

- This is not a full staff directory.
- I include people when the current evidence ties them to `model design`, `model building`, `miniature photography`, `optical completion`, or an important `handoff` in the ANH pipeline.
- `Attribution type` matters:
  - `signed design` is stronger than a general recollection,
  - `named builder` is stronger than a broad `program link`,
  - `filming / optical` means the person is part of getting miniatures on screen even if not tied to one specific ship.

## Confidence Guide

- `High`: direct naming in official, auction, or unusually strong local evidence.
- `Medium`: good working attribution, but not fully closed.
- `Low`: plausible context role only.

## Read

- The strongest person-to-object attributions so far are:
  - `Joe Johnston` on the `Escape Pod` design anchor
  - `Paul Huston + Stuart Ziff` on the `Death Star cannon`
  - `Lorne Peterson + Jon Erland + Steve Gawley` on the `Blue 1` X-wing
- The strongest program-level attributions are:
  - `John Dykstra` for the Van Nuys facility and filming infrastructure
  - `Paul Huston` for the Y-wing master-pattern phase
  - `Dave Brown` for the final-stage eyewitness chronology
- The weakest area is still exact `who filmed which specific miniature on which date`, especially once the shop is running at full speed in `1976-1977`.
