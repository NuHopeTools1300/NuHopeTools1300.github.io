# ANH ILM Van Nuys Research Starter

## Scope

- Research target: physical miniatures and model-shop-built elements for the first `Star Wars` film (`ANH`).
- Working time window: `1975-1977`.
- Working place: the `ILM` model shop in `Van Nuys`.
- Working corpus: local text transcripts exported from `therpf.com`.

## How To Treat This Corpus

- Most threads here are secondary or tertiary discussion, not production-era documentation.
- Give the most weight to threads that point to:
  - dated production-era articles
  - archival shop footage
  - clearly identified archival photos
  - direct production-lineage claims
- Use replica and build threads mainly as:
  - maps to donor kits
  - indexes to surviving reference photos
  - variant-tracking discussions
- Be careful not to mix `ANH` evidence with later `ESB` or `ROTJ` redresses, repairs, or restorations.

## Evidence Tiers

- `A`: strongest in this corpus. Usually a thread whose main value is a direct link to period footage or a period article.
- `B`: strong secondary research. Usually model-specific threads built around archival images, auction references, or long-running ID work.
- `C`: useful support. Usually builder threads, reference dumps, or meta-indexes that still help track sources.

## Notes From Initial Sampling

- `The first Star Wars miniature_.txt` is useful for the small opening `Blockade Runner` and cites auction-era specifics like the `16-inch` metal model.
- `amazing Chrew video document from 1976 ILM workshop online.txt` is short, but high value because it points straight to `1976` ILM workshop footage.
- `American Cinematographer - July 1977.txt` is also short, but high value because it points to a period article on miniature and mechanical effects.
- `ILM X-Wing Models_ Timeline & Status.txt` is good for tracking surviving `X-Wing` identities and post-production history, but it is not itself build-period evidence.
- `ILM Gold Leader Reference Images....txt` is image-rich and likely useful for `Y-Wing` surface detail, though it reflects later archival condition and restoration questions too.
- `Anh Escape Pod Research Thread.txt` is a strong donor-kit and construction lead because the first post is explicitly framed as a verified kit list and parts-map hub.
- `ANH 5' Falcon with Dykstraflex.txt` is useful as context for the restored `Dykstraflex`, but it is a modern exhibit thread, not evidence of the original `1976-1977` build process.

## Recommended Order Of Work

1. Lock the `A-tier` evidence and extract every concrete production claim from it.
2. Build a model inventory for `ANH` only, without mixing in later trilogy modifications.
3. Use the `B-tier` threads to map each model to:
   - surviving archival photos
   - known variants
   - donor-kit identifications
4. Use the `C-tier` threads only to fill gaps, never as the sole basis for a claim.
