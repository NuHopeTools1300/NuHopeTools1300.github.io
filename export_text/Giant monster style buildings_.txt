I think they're often made using similar techniques as durable architectural models, but then fitted with talc and fuller's earth for dust and debris effect, and often equipped with explosive squibs and small pyro charges.  Older style materials would be balsa and cardboard sheeting. 

We built a cityscape a few years ago, before we got our CNC machine. It was for a fashion shoot, so the buildings weren't made to be break-away, and they were styled to be simple and iconic. We just used a mish-mash of building styles like you'd find in most cities ; art deco, modern, post modern etc.

If I had to do a break-away city right now, I'd use medium density polyurethane foam panels for structure, cut break lines on the reverse sides, and carve the facades with the CNC machine, or add detail bits using strips of foam.
