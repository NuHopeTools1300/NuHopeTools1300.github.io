Hey guys

I was asked a few weeks ago now to build up this Salzo kit by a very cool member here!

Its took a while to get the hull together as she was a bit warped here and there, but nothing us X wing builders arent used to. As always, thanks to Mike for a great kit!

She is to be Red 5, and while im no fan of 5, its been educational working out colors and markings over the past few weeks, truth is, ive hardly ever looked hard at this prop!

The paint work is the usual matt black base with a gloss white top coat.

As you all know, this is then scuffed and rubbed down with fine wire wool to get a good worn patchy effect!

Ive got to placing on the markings today, the grey green took a while to get mixed, and sadly looks more green in the pics, but im going to play with misting it out some to subdue the contrast.

Its been fun looking at how guys like Rob, Matt Meyer and Bob tackled her, and my god im doing my best to keep up with the Jones' and there amazing paint ups!

Ill update next week as weathering and misting will be underway.

As always crits and opinions always welcomed, more so on this bird as, did i mention? Im no Red 5 fan LOL.

bests.....lee

Well i began to weather her today, its been a nightmare as im truly under the damn weather, lots of snot inside the mask and all that LOLOLOL, yummy!

Anyways, its been an eye opener, so many variations in panel hue etc, its really tricky to get 5 looking right, so now i know where Bob and co really had to get there game faces on.

All weathering so far is via the airbrush, i didnt want to go too nuts with pastels just yet as im not too big a fan, but they will be used on the buttplate and here and there on the hull and wings.

All chip marks were shot through punctured holes in the masking tape, this makes for me at least a very scratched sporadic effect!

Latex masking became useful to wear in the nose and some areas on the hull flanks.

So far, i suppose she aint too bad for my first 5 (god i hope you like so far Chris lol) , could do with some opinions though, still a long way to go, but at least if im not on the ball i can go back and tweak!

lee

Guy, the splatter is done with a toothbrush buddy, that and artist acrylics, but no blacks, there to stark!

Just put a blob on the brush, and then apply a bit of water to thin it some, from there play with how thin you want it, and how much spray you want, dial it in by running your thumb across the bristles on some kitchen towel, when your happy, flick it on the model lol.

The thicker the paint, the finer the spatter and less spread, thinner, its more washed out, like the lower end of the buttplate i did?

Just dont do it like Jack Nicholson in Batman, it wont work lol.

Practice on some cheap small parts buddy, its an effect when used sparingly and well, looks very cool to my eye!
