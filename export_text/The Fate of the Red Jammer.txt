I do not know if this has been addressed here earlier and how many times, but I'd like to ask anyway. 

The Star Wars Identities opens in London on friday, go and see it if you can. The official web page offers a glimpse of the main display of filming miniatures assembled with rebels vs. the empire layout. As you can see, the Red Jammer (circled in red) is still in the preview photograph which is dated 2013. 

[IMAGE: 369352-5c379f1190b3053a947b98a2133d6653.jpg]

The Jammer was not with the exhibition in Paris when I saw it in 2014. It was on the catalogue but the display sported only an empty mounting rod. Apparently, the Jammer has been missing since. For the London show, the Slave 1 has been moved to the main display, it now occupies Jammer's spot (flying in landing position, no less!), so no imminent return for the Jammer is expected.

Now, rumor has it that the Jammer was dropped during the preparations for the Paris exhibition and was damaged enough to be pulled out from the tour. I have a vague recollection of someone confirming this but I cannot find this anywhere from my messages or posts. Does anyone know the Jammer's whereabouts? It's probably back in the Lucasfilm archives but how badly damaged is it? Is it being restored, and if it is, are any of the RPF members involved? I want photographs dammit!

If  one of the original filming models is destroyed it is news, just think about how much they're worth. They each are a piece of cinema  history, unique artefacts from a bygone era and I, for one, would like to know if one is destroyed.

Guys, for all we know, the blue 1/red 2 X-wing could be better taken care of than most of the OT miniatures. The owner must certainly be aware of the value of said artefact. I only hope there was a possibility to document it thoroughly with photographs and measurements properly executed.

It is probably safe to say that we, the die hard fans and connoisseurs, value  these wonderful miniatures more than most. Perhaps we see their uniqueness more clearly, or  maybe it's just a fantasy we can't let go once we find a way to connect  with it. Either way, we are talking about cinema history, popular culture heritage that exceeds far beyond Skywalker ranch and even the US borders. I grew up with these things, just like you, and I'm on the other side of the Atlantic!

It is true that as the owner of these models Lucasfilm does what it sees best and we have to respect and trust that. I just wish they would signal something more akin to what I, or dare I say we feel about them. I would love more transparency when it comes to the condition and even location of each individual model. Seeing a chart, a timeline of what happened to them after filming would make me very happy. 

Hearing rumors about the vanished Red Jammer sustaining undisclosed damages, but never hearing a word from the Identities organization or Lucasfilm makes me wonder if everything is as well as it could be. That's why I am asking the question: where is the Red Jammer and what happened to it?
