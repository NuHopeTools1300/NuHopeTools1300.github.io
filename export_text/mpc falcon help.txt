First, you might be in the wrong section here...

To answer your question though, it depends on which on which version you got. If you got the snap kit version that is around 1/65. If you got the normal glue kit, or flight display version, then it is around 1/42. Both have horrible figures. If you got the Pro-shop version, it is around 1/36. 

I'm guessing you've probably got the standard 1/42 one since that is the most common it seems.

I don't believe there are any currently available detail sets for the 1/43 or 1/36 versions

As far as the Falcon (listed in your the threads title), it is about 1/72

the MPC was never on film to my knowledge.

It was used to anamatics and "pre-visulization" for both ESB and ROTJ. It is in the background shots of the archives several places... but never on sceen. However I will take it upon myself to go back and watch ESB again and make absolutely sure :lol.

For the record - I'd love to be wrong 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]

OK I just rewatched it... there are three places that it "might" be used since I could not identify the model that was used in these 3 scenes

1) 0:38:14 VERY bginning of the chase where the Falcon dives and the Star Destroyers dodge each other. I am not sure which model this is - but I don't think tis the MPC sicne the lighting shows a large shadow from the Cockpit tunnel that would be hard to produce from the low flatish tunnel of the MPC.

2) 1:01:21 - Exitting the space slug when the slug jumps out of its hole to bith at them... The ship is too small to tell... Given the size I'd guess it was either the 10 inch one form the bottom of the Medical frigate (shot form far away), or the 2 inch one from the long shot of the back of the Star Destroyer 1:14:48

3) 1:07:06 - The SD is chasing the Falcon out of the asteroid field - long distance shot of SD and Falcon inframe together right after the "my Lord,... we have them." line

______________________________________________________________________________

The rest of the Falcon shots can rule out the MPC for one reason or another based on these simple tells:

1) The shot is clear enough its easy to tell the falocn is either the 5 foot model, the live action set, or the 32 inch model

2) Enough details are visible that its obviously not the MPC - most notably the hull access pits are not undersized as on the MPC, and the sidewall heights

3) Further if we assume that the picture of the Gary Kurtz falcon is the one that was proportedly built by ILM (I have no reason to doubt it) its obviously built straght from the box - i.e. without the addition of the additional front landing suports added for ESB.  By the way that model sits on the table this seems to be true...  Any shot of the underbelly that has these landing gear boxes is obviously not that model.

:cry  My conclusion - the MPC is not in that movie...  I wish it were - I have a TON of them in my stash :thumbsup
