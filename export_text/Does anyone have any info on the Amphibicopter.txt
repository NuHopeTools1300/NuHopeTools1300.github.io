I believe the amphibicopter existed as full size prop and CG model only.  There was lots of model work done for the film but it was all miniature sets and buildings, no vehicles.  Check out the special edition two disc DVD of A.I., the 'making of' disc has several chapters that go into great detail on how the spfx were done, including many images of the amphibicopter. Its well worth it since no other material about the making of ther film has ever been made available. Teddy rules.

The full-size is well....the full-size filming model. That's Tim Flattery standing next to it. Tim was the designer on the film and also designed the pod car. He also did Kilmer's Batmobile in Batman Forever. We have mutual alumni friends and film associates. 

Not sure of the scale model except that it was on Tim's desk and was made more as a model for full-size build study, not for filming. It was supposed to be CG...although they may have used it in the end. You can also see a Mars rover in the background from Mission to Mars. 

I haven't spoken to Tim in many years but maybe someone can contact him directly? I THINK his portfolio is still online. Great guy.

Dave
