Over the the past few months I have been scouring the web, looking for the parts, and information needed to build one of these. I think finally ready to begin construction. This forum has been a HUGE resource for me, so this is my attempt to give a little back, and post a build log. I'll start by giving credit to the Members / Threads that have been the most helpful:

http://www.therpf.com/f10/revisiting-training-remote-42132/

NightEyes gives a great build log here, and did some invaluable color matching from the REAL DEAL when it was on display. He even includes tips from Lorne Peterson, the creator of the original. EXCELLENT thread! :thumbsup 

http://www.therpf.com/f10/anh-jedi-training-remote-150843/

KrokoHunter gives some great advice on cutting out styrene circles, one of the more challenging tasks in this build.

http://www.therpf.com/f9/star-wars-exhibit-props-models-costumes-lots-150372/

Rymo has TONS of detailed pictures of the "real deal" screen used costumes and props from Star Wars. This type of reference material is incredibly useful.

Before I get into any serious construction, here is everything I've collected so far:

Two 6" ABS hemispheres from Plastruct. They also offer then in Acrylic; same price.

1/48 Bandai Panther G

1/72 Hasegawa M4A3 Sherman

00 Scale Airfix Panzer IV

Chrome Rims and axial brackets from a 1/25th AMT/Ertl

.020" Styrene Sheet

Oomoo silicone

Smooth-Cast Resin 

My plan is to make molds and cast the parts I need from the models. The Bandai Panther G is a hard kit to find (and not cheep), if you don't make copies of the parts, you will need 8 identical kits!!! I have seen resin castings for sale in the Junkyard, but I want to try my hand at mold making and casting.

I thought I would start by making the styrene "rings". I should mention that this was my first time working with styrene. I don't have access to a vacuformer (yet), but i needed a way to conform the styrene to the sphere. My first idea was to cut a piece of styrene, lay it on the hemisphere, and hit it with the heat gun. The first picture shows the results.:facepalm

BTW: can anyone tell me how to post pics in the IN the thread, as opposed to adding attachments?

The next idea was inspired by something I saw on KrokoHunter's thread. I made a plaster mold using a dixie cup glued to one of the hemispheres. Now I could press the heated styrene between the hemisphere and the plaster mold to produce perfectly conformed stryrene!
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]

I cut a doughnut shaped piece of plywood and stapled the styrene to it. The styrene was heated with a heat gun until it began to sag. I then laid the doughnut over my plaster mold and pressed the hemisphere down on top of the styrene. worked like a charm!

I haven't thought much about the final display for this thing. A plaque reading "Han Solo's Marksman-H combat remote." sounds VERY cool. 

Cutting the rings from the molded styrene turned out to be a little more tricky than I had imagined. It took a few tries to develop my technique. I found it was best to hold the circle cutter in place, and rotate the styrene. I glued a small chunk of wood onto the plywood the styrene was taped to. This gave the needle on the cutter something to "bite" into. At first I was having trouble with the blade not "tracking" the same line on each pass. The blade on my X-ACTO compass cutter is shaped to cut in a clockwise direction only. I found that if I rotated the styrene backwards after the first pass, there was no tracking issue.

I marked the plaster form to use as a template for cutting the center gap in the circles. I am toying with the idea of leaving little "bridges" of styrene across the gaps with will be removed only after the circles are glued to the sphere. It seems like this would make the final alignment a little easier.

Also started some layout on the hemispheres.
