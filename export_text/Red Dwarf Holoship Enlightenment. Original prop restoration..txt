Hi guys. I have been a member of the site for a while but, rather than post, I have been content to just view the fascinating and exquisite work that is produced and discussed by you talented and imaginative people. However, I have now acquired what remains of the original BBC studio miniature of The Holoship Enlightenment from Red Dwarf which I hope to restore to her former glory (or somthing close). Hopefully, I will have been able to upload some images of the item. Of course, you will immediately see that it is in a bit of a state having led a neglected existance in storage once filming was finished, although I would say that 90% of the ship is present.

I'm sure that I shall be seeking members advice on many aspects of this project though my first dilema is do I just put her back together using original components only, thereby fully maintaining the original integrity but ending with an incomplete, unlit piece, or do I go with my insinct which is to restore her properly using additional components, lighting and all. Naturally, I have no intention of adding any un-original design aspects, or junking any of the original parts. I at least know that for the time being I am safe removing the significant layer of dirt and dust with some glass cleaner and a large box of cotton buds. I would appreciate hearing any thoughts that members might have and, naturally, if there is anyone on the site that might have been involved in her construction or filming then I am very keen to hear from you.

Bill has been based at Shepperton Studios since the 1980's. The Red Dwarf fx work wasn't shot at Shepperton until the seventh season in 1996, and Bill didn't actually work on it until season 8, when he supervised models and props like the 1/12th scale hangar and the Skutters. Of course, if you can contact him or Neill you should!

The Enlightenment was shot at BBC Visual Effects in Acton, unfortunately no longer a 'going concern'! It was built by Paul McGuinness and shot by Peter Tyler under the supervision of the late Peter Wragg. According to Tyler's article in Starburst 167 from 1992, the model was "a magnificent and complex structure, made up from hundreds of pieces of polished perspex, clear hemispheres filled with strange transparent machinery and numerous, tiny krypton bulbs".

Thanks for the pics! It's great to know the model still exists and is being properly cared for!
