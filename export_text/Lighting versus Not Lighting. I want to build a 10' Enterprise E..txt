Re: Lighting versus Not Lighting

How much it would cost depends on several factors. The first being what type of model. An Enterprise would cost a lot more to light than say an AT-AT studio scale kit. The other factor is how you light it. If you buy one of those pre-made lighting kits for models it's going to be expensive. If you do the lighting yourself it's going to be much, much cheaper. You will need an understanding of electronics, wiring, soldering, etc.

What are you looking at doing?

$10,000 is cheap if you factor in that you'd be putting thousands of hours of work into making a 10 footer.

Something to consider about doing a 10 footer is the light that it would actually give off when lit.  With all those windows, and at that size, you'd actually be able to see details inside the windows when not lit. When lit, it would be like a giant lamp for your room.  Plan accordingly, you're not building a model, you're building a piece of furniture for your house.
