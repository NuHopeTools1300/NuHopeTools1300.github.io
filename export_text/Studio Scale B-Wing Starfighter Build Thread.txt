It's been a few months, but I've not been idle!  I'm back now with a new studio scale build thread for the B-Wing Attack Starfighter!  This ship is one of my all-time favorites -- everything about it I find cool and awesome, and so I've decided to have a go at it.  I might be out of my mind -- in fact, I KNOW I'm out of my mind -- for attempting this build when I've only created one other SS build prior. But to quote the Cheshire Cat, "We're all Mad here..."

Leading up to now I've done a TON of research, combing through other threads here on the RPF and other sources, but mostly my own independent research using reference photos and scouring through kits and scans to identify as many kit parts as possible.  I've also had some assistance filling in a few really hard-to-ID kit parts from a few folks who, upon request, have chosen to remain anonymous. The result is that I've got a pretty complete picture of everything needed --at least kit-wise -- and I'm going to be detailing the build here.

So let's jump in and get going!

First, an obligatory picture of kits collected thus far.  I have another 4-5 kits still on the way, and that should put me into pretty good shape.  It's a lot of kits compared to the bomber, with some contributing only 1-2 parts.  I think I'm at around 28 total unique kits thus far, but I'm still researching a handful of unidentified parts, so that could go up.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/kit_list-jpg.1893497/]

And, yes, that F-14A box is REALLY rough (it's what's inside that counts!)!

I'm planning to build this as much like Bill George did at ILM as I possibly can, and this of course means vacuum-forming multiple parts -- the cockpit halves, the main wing halves, the engine housing halves, the upper neck, the main wing gun surround and probably the wing gun housings.  I'm in the process of building my own vacuum-forming machine, and will be working up the bucks needed to cast the parts.

As another preparatory step, I also picked up the Bandai 1/72 B-Wing kit.  This kit was created using scans of the original filming model, and I can tell you it's super accurate!!  I'm amazed at how they were able to reproduce, at scale, a number of obscure parts on the kit.  Just amazing, and an excellent resource for making measurements -- this will be especially useful when I go to make the bucks.  My only real complaint with the model is that the scale isn't actually 1/72 -- it's much closer to being 1/80th scale.  I determined this through numerous measurements of the actual kits parts vs. the representation of the parts on the Bandai kit.  This means that for any of the measurements I might need I have to end of multiplying it by around 2.54x on average.

And with that, let's begin!

The Cockpit Neck Mid-Section

I wanted to get going on this build, so I picked a section I thought would be easy enough to build -- so I went with what I'm calling the "cockpit neck".  This section is made up of two Airfix Saturn V rocket parts, some Leopold parts and styrene strips.  I know there's been some debate as to whether it's an Airfix, Revell or Entex kit that contributes to this, but as you'll see, all of the reference markings definitively show it's the Airfix kit.

First thing was to trim up the halves -- you can see the trimmed up section on the left and untrimmed on the right.  This primarily consisted of removing the thin locking section at the front and sanding down all of the raised sections around the perimeter (with the exception of the large bump still on the left-hand half).

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/middle_cockpit_01-jpg.1893486/]

Then I glued up the halves together.  There is a collar that goes around where the cockpit connects to this section and so I also cut out a disk of styrene and glued that to the end to act as the main support for the collar.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/middle_cockpit_02-jpg.1893487/]

This was followed by the collar itself.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/middle_cockpit_03-jpg.1893488/]

Finally, I added a few strips of styrene around the perimeter to match the reference.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/middle_cockpit_04-jpg.1893489/]

Once I got to this point, something about it kept bothering me.  Looking closer, I really didn't like the raised sections around the perimeter of the Saturn V parts.  This was creating an uneven shape, and looking again at the reference pics, I wasn't finding any indications that these sections were on the filming model.  They needed to go!  Unfortunately, while attempting to fix this I ended up screwing it up.  Luckily I had another Saturn V kit ready to donate!

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/middle_cockpit_04a-jpg.1893527/]

Here you can see the two halves with the raised sections cut off -- not really a circle anymore, is it?

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/middle_cockpit_07-jpg.1893492/]

With a little friendly coercion I was able to glue it up and into a circular shape.  The disk inside the assembly is part of the coercion, keeping the shape from pulling back into an oval.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/middle_cockpit_08-jpg.1893493/]

I added all of the strip styrene back -- this looks a LOT better!  You can see now where the cut marks and protrusion I left in place match up to the reference.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/middle_cockpit_10-jpg.1893495/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/middle_cockpit_05-jpg.1893490/]

Throw on a few Leopold parts and the last of the needed styrene and this section is looking pretty good.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/middle_cockpit_11-jpg.1893496/]

The reference also shows a few of these little triangular shapes, which are the result of sanding off the raised sections around the Saturn V parts.  With this current version, these markings also now line up.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/middle_cockpit_11a-jpg.1893533/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/middle_cockpit_06-jpg.1893491/]

I still need to add a bit of wiring, but I think it's a pretty good start for this build.  Once I've worked out the armature, this will have to get mounted to that, likely with a hole cut through the bottom and a bearing to allow for cockpit rotation.  I'll also have to run electrical wiring down through the neck so I can light up the rice lights in the cockpit.

Next up I will be tackling the cockpit itself.  Here's a teaser with the kit parts I've collected so far:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/cockpit_prep_01-jpg.1893534/]

And with that we're off!  I'm expecting this to be a fairly slow-burn of a build.  I have lots more research still to do -- need to work out the structure of the armature, finish building the vacuum former, and create all the bucks needed -- not to mention finish gathering kits, parts, and working out the rest of the assemblies.  But I've got a lot figured out already, and look forward to sharing my progress with everyone.  Til next time, keep on scratchin!

What?! Another update?  Thought this was a slow-burn thread?  Welp, I’m sitting idle at the airport watching my flight get delayed and figured I’d post something, as I’ve gotten a bit further ahead than posts may indicate.

Cockpit Interior

First step was to figure out what the base pattern is for the interior of the cockpit that all kit parts attach to.  I did a number of measurements and ended up with a shape 1mm on each side smaller than the cockpit shell size.  I cut that initial base out of .040 thick stock.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1721-jpeg.1893678/]

Next I started adding some kit parts to make sure my spacing looked right.  I also noted that on the reference there appears to be another thinner layer underneath this one, so I added that layer.  I also padded up the back sides, as the Bismarck/Tirpitz parts that go there should be elevated a little.  Parts are looking pretty good so far.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1727-jpeg.1893683/]

Once I was satisfied with the general shape/size, I began work on the rear of the cockpit.  For this I cut a circle out of .040” thick stock and figured out using the ref images about how far down from the top the kit parts begin.  I also cut up the F-14 parts that flank the rear seat.  I also worked up a more structured cockpit shape and attached the rear to the rest.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1740-jpeg.1893686/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1742-jpeg.1893688/]

I wasn’t happy with how the top surface looked — I didn’t think it was accurate enough so I redid it a bit.  I’m much happier with v2:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1745-jpeg.1893689/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1748-jpeg.1893690/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1747-jpeg.1893691/]

So now what’s a cockpit without a pilot?  The head for the pilot is off ground crew in the F-14, but the body of the pilot.  So I went to town hacking the head off and reattaching it to the other body.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1728-jpeg.1893684/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1731-jpeg.1893685/]

I also hacked off his feet, as they are missing in the studio model.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1741-jpeg.1893687/]

In addition, I also chose to work a bit on the under-cockpit gun assembly.  The base is from the Mörser Karl, cut down as shown, with a gun from the same kit on the left side and an Sd.Ah.52 gun in the right.  3mm tubing with brass tube inserts completed the base look.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1724-jpeg.1893680/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1726-jpeg.1893682/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1725-jpeg.1893681/]

More parts are needed, but I’m waiting until I have the cockpit shells before I do those.

With that I went about attaching all the kit parts I had to the cockpit base.  Add a pilot, and it’s looking pretty snazzy!

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1805-jpeg.1893692/]

I’m still missing 3-4 parts that need to go into the cockpit, and those should be in my hands by the time I get back from traveling — I’ll update once the cockpit is truly done.

I also attached the chin gun.  Why not?  I’m sure I’ll have to move it so it’s barely attached, but I couldn’t resist!

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1756-jpeg.1893696/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1755-jpeg.1893695/]

And that’s where we are!  And just in time, my flight is now ready to board.  I have a few other in-progress pics for smaller sections I’ve been  working up, and I might post those while traveling.  Else, I’ll post once I’m back and can get more work done.  Til next time!

I’m back!  With a pretty small, but for me, important update.  A few more kits have arrived at Casa de Scott, not least of which was the 1/350 Tamiya Tirpitz, which is a must-have for this build.  The Tirpitz and the Bismarck share a lot of parts, and a lot of the cockpit can be built with either one. However, the “dash” section for the B-Wing comes exclusively from the Tirpitz, so if you’re following along, you’ll need one of those, too.  It’s also needed for a few more parts, and this update is mostly me fitting those parts in.

So let’s continue with the cockpit!

First, I added these two parts from the Tirpitz along the inside sides of the cockpit.  These walls were layered styrene, so I had to cut away the outer most layer to properly fit in these parts.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1869-jpeg.1896068/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1868-jpeg.1896069/]

Looks a ton better, a much more finished look!

Here are the two parts needed for the main dash section I alluded to earlier.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1870-jpeg.1896019/]

The bottom one get glued behind the top one.  Sorry, I didn’t take a lot of in-progress pics at this point as I was super excited to get this installed.  And here it is, all fit in.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1872-jpeg.1896022/]

Lastly, I installed the control handles from the F-14 to pretty much finish off the kit portion of the main cockpit area.  I gotta source some rice lights and get the wiring installed for this, but until the shells are made, this is about where I’m gonna leave this.

Some more pics, cause why not?!

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1874-jpeg.1896023/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1875-jpeg.1896024/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1876-jpeg.1896025/]

This feels like a bit of a let-down, so I’ll throw one more thing I’ve been working on in here — the engine vent assemblies.

There are two of these vents on the B-Wing, meaning you’ll need two 1/12 Tamiya 312T4 kits to make these, plus a pair of parts from the PT-15, a pair from the 1/20 Ligier, and a pair from the 1/24 Airfix Harrier.

First, take those lovely 312T4 engine blocks — and hack them apart!  Gotta trim the sides and a bit off the end.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1765-jpeg.1896026/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1766-jpeg.1896033/]

Next, glue the Harrier missile rack part to the bottom and the PT-15 part to the end to cover up the hole.  You’ll also need a piece of styrene to go under the PT-15 part, as it’s not tall enough to fill the gap.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1767-jpeg.1896028/]

This is the Ligier part that then get glued underneath this engine vent assembly.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1822-jpeg.1896029/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1820-jpeg.1896030/]

A few strips of styrene down the one side and voila! Final result is an engine vent sub-assembly!

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1821-jpeg.1896031/]

Now use your duplicator and create another, so you have two.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1826-jpeg.1896032/]

And that will do it for now!  I’m continuing to build my Vac-former, but it got cold out and so it’s miserable working in my garage.  I’ll be focusing more on getting that up and running over the next few weeks, with minor updates on sub-assemblies until then.  Gotta also start figuring out an armature — I’ve got an idea, but if anyone knows what the original looked like or have any advice, I’m def open to it!

Until next time, keep on scratching!

Bbbbrrrrr…!  It’s -2 deg F in my garage, so work on my vac-former has ground to a halt.  Good news is the heater unit is in and working — just need it to warm up some so I can finish making the platens and holding frames.  So what’s a guy to do?

Build the main wing gun assembly, of course!

This assembly has a LOT going on.  I’ll try and detail as much as I can.  Let’s go!

Main Wing Gun Assembly

I chose to start off with the base of the assembly that everything attaches to — the engine/transmission part from the 312T and the backside of a gun assembly from the Gepard.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1893-jpeg.1898411/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1894-jpeg.1898412/]

Next up was the Leopold gun, which I cut down.  I added a small piece of styrene tubing to act as a spacer to keep the gun better aligned when attached and give it more surface area for gluing.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1899-jpeg.1898416/]

I also attached some Mörser Karl parts, another Long Tom part, and a box from I think the Sd.Ah.52 (I’ll have to verify that!):

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1900-jpeg.1898415/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1901-jpeg.1898417/]

Attached the ubiquitously used SD.AH.52 gun and this filter unit from the P34 Tyrrell:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1903-jpeg.1898418/]

And the Long Tom gun:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1904-jpeg.1898419/]

You’ll notice perhaps that the barrel of the Long Tom has been cut off — on the blue variant of the B-Wing it remains attached but I’m guessing it broke off on the meatball version and (as you’ll see later) was reattached in a different spot.

Next I took on the other large cannon, which is pretty much just the 1/25 SU-100 cannon with a PT-15 part on the back side of the tip and a P34 part on the end.  This end has 1/8” dia stock pushed through the end, so I drilled out the end to size before assembling.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1912-jpeg.1898422/]

Remember that Long Tom gun I mentioned earlier — handling that here with some tube stock and the tip from an Airfix Harrier antenna.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1918-jpeg.1898423/]

And attached.  It’s such a weird place to put this gun back on…

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1919-jpeg.1898424/]

You can’t see these in the reference pics, but they’re present on the gun assembly from the Bandai kit (attached to an Entex Porsche Transmission part — yeah, not going after that one!), so I added them.  I’ll probably have to cut them up when I get to installing this assembly, but it works for now.

This past weekend I spent some time working on the B-Wing “pod” section that’s aft of the cockpit.  I’m sure ILM used some off-the-shelf components for some of the internals here, but I’m just gonna scratch most of it together.

Started with constructing the v-belt-looking disc that sits right up inside the primary ring.  This one right here:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1964-jpeg.1899468/]

Ok, so I really have no idea what I’m doing, but started working on an armature for the B-Wing.  I got a block of aluminum, 1-1/2” x 1-1/2” x 6” and cut it down to 2-1/2” long.  I measured that the distance from the rear mount point between the engines and the central mounting points on the body was about 2”, so 2-1/2” gives me a little meat in the front for the threaded holes.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1994-jpeg.1901090/]

Next I drilled holes through all four sides to thread.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1995-jpeg.1901091/]

I also drilled one through the back door that engine mount point.  Once that was done I cut down my 3/4” OD rod to about 4.45” long, and then taped that end with a 1/2”-13 tap.  Did the same with the 5 holes on the block.

I used some 1/2”-13 fully threaded 1” long rods to connect the threaded rod end and the block together.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_1997-jpeg.1901092/]

These are looking pretty good to me.

In the original filming model the bulbs used were halogen bulbs that required ceramic bases to mount them.  I'm going to use LEDs that don't require those bases, but I wanted the engines to have the same raised base for the bulbs.  I created a small ring by wrapping a piece of thing styrene around some tubing to thicken it up and inserted that into the bottom of the surrounds.  This is mounted on a small round base I made out of .040" styrene underneath the surrounds.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/engine_bells_08-jpg.1910246/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/engine_bells_09-jpg.1910247/]

I think once I get to that stage I’ll start work on the bucks needed to form the upper neck section and start getting things together on the upper half of the ship.  

Til next time!.

The items I am vac-forming, from my understanding and talking to others who are in the know, were originally vac-formed and not from lot parts.  So no undiscovered parts there. Some attribute the wing gun shrouds as being from a Monogram A-4E Skyhawk, but the shape is wrong — it doesn’t work.

I’m making up the entire armature myself, since it’s not clear what was actually used inside, but basing it on some evidence and photos.  For example, the side mounting out of engine housing is accurate, as is the mounting between the engine. There’s also a mounting pole down through the middle to the end of main wing gun.  The hinges are based on me taking measurements from the Bandai B-Wing, then comparing to the reference pics using kit parts to confirm the size.  The size I landed on is pretty accurate, though truthfully not as round as I’d like, but it’s close enough.

The only thing I have to revisit now is that mounting thread size. Literally today in a Facebook thread (no pun intended) asking about armatures ILM used, Bill George chimed in and effectively said that the smaller models used 3/4” mounting size as the standard, and I’ve used 1/2”-13. Back on the bench, have a few updates, mainly to the cockpit and the starboard wing.  Let’s go!

The cockpit.  One thing I learned at Wonderfest from Bill George is that the cockpit for the original B-Wing model wasn’t actually wired for lighting.  He said it was too complicated to get the cockpit to rotate fully and light it, and so even though there are grain of rice light bulbs mounted in the cockpit, they were just colored with clear paint and not wired up to light.  I’d been putting off adding lights to the cockpit because of the wiring, but learning this freed me to just add the lights and not have to worry about how it’s lit.  So I started adding lights!

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3006-jpeg.1942656/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3008-jpeg.1942658/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3009-jpeg.1942659/]

I had started adding these lights using CA glue, but that really wasn't working well.  While at Wonderfest I was clued into using IC Gel, which is a CA variant, to attach parts to metal or odd materials to plastic.  It's mainly used with coral in fish tanks, apparently.  I gave it a try, and the bulbs stuck in place almost immediately, making installation a LOT easier.  I still have 1-2 bulbs to install, but I ran out of them (they're 1.7mm dia, harder to find) and am waiting on more.

Next, I added some paneling to the lower collar section -- the one with the missing panel -- to try and get it finished off.  It still needs armor plating across the front, but I'll get to that later, too.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3010-jpeg.1942660/]

Now -- the starboard wing.  This was a fun one!  Since I had already done quite a bit of work on the port side wing, I mostly knew what I was doing and what I had to correct to make this wing look right.

Here are me adding parts for the front wing wells.  Mostly Morser Karl parts, but also an 1/20 Tamiya Ford engine part.  The hinges are glued in with 2-part epoxy.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3002-jpeg.1942665/]

Contrary to what I'd done thus far with the port wing, I took some time to create the buck I needed to form the shape for the wing gun shroud.  This shape here is my second attempt -- the first was WAY too wide, so I had to do a lot of modifications to the buck to make it pretty thin to get this shape.  It looks and measures correctly now.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3004-jpeg.1942666/]

I need to create the armor plating that attaches to the shroud, and so this time I followed some advice I was given at Wonderfest and used tracing paper to create the proper shape.  First I drew out the shape I needed on the shroud, then used tracing paper to copy it from the curved surface to a flat shape.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3018-jpeg.1942672/]

I've been putting this off a bit, but decided to get going a bit on the wing gun barrels and connection point to see what I could come up with.

For reference, we're starting by making this:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/1755986975580-png.1963422/]

To create the base, I grabbed this part from the Leopold and cut off the end and side pegs.  The diameter is pretty spot on for what I've measured for the barrel diameter.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3409-jpg.1963395/]

Once cut down, I pushed in a .2" OD tube into the end to create that next section of the gun barrel.  The ID on this is perfect to fit around a 5/32" brass tube.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3442-jpg.1963396/]

I then needed to figure out what parts would work for the rounded sections at the end of the barrel.  For this, I used these:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3446-jpg.1963400/]

The tan parts are from the Mobelwagen, the silver from the P34 and the dark green from the SU-85.  I glued them all together and sanded down the Mobelwagen parts by around 1/3 to get what looked like the right diameters and thicknesses.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3447-jpg.1963401/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3448-jpg.1963402/]

I then drilled a 5/32" diameter hole through the middle, and assembled.  There's another one of those .2" diameter tubes in that middle section between the first and second "round" sections, with a .25" OD tube around that to beef it up.  Throw a Morser Karl part on the end and you get a barrel!

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3449-jpg.1963403/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3450-jpg.1963404/]

From there I drilled a hole through the end of the wing gun shroud to fit the 5/32" brass tube through.  You can see it here poking out the back, which matches to the reference.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3463-jpg.1963406/]

Here it is from the front.  I've also added 2 of the Long Tom parts to the top to fit in that gap int he shroud.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3464-jpg.1963407/]

Next I attached the Gepard gun.  I cut the extended tips off, and shoved it in there on an angle that looked right.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3465-jpg.1963408/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3466-jpg.1963409/]

You may have noticed the black rounded part above -- this is from the 1/12 312T and was something that looked to me like a potential part for that location.  I like the roundness of the top, the extended section in front that happens to be very close to the barrel base's diameter, and I think looks nice.  So I started working with it to see what I could do.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3467-jpg.1963410/]

There's a curved section that should also be beneath the barrel, so I gave a go at scratching one that would line up with the 312T part.  I also added a few pieces to outside of the 312T part to make it look more like I think it should.

Back again with another small update!  We left off test-fitting the gun shroud to the main wing.  I was happy with how that went, so now time to add details!

I was having issues getting the .01” thick styrene to bend into the needed compound curve for armor plating so I just vac-formed some thinner .04” stock over the shape and cut that out.  It thinned down to between .01” and .02” and worked great!

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3642-jpeg.1975844/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3643-jpeg.1975845/]

I next cut down the threaded rod so it was much shorter and threaded a 1-1/2” long hex coupling onto the end.  I then cut the hex coupling down to 3/4” long and rounded over (mostly) the outside edges to give me the needed attachment point for the bottom.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3661-jpeg.1975846/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3660-jpeg.1975847/]

I then filled the gap of the ring with some apoxie sculpt to give it some rigidity when being handled.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3663-jpeg.1975848/]

Cut a hole through the shroud and attached it to the end, tweaking until it fit the way I wanted.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3659-jpeg.1975849/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3657-jpeg.1975850/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3656-jpeg.1975851/]

I now felt good about permanently attaching the bottom shroud section to the top.  I also added the 1/700 Enterprise/Hornet parts to the sides of the shroud.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3666-jpeg.1975852/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3667-jpeg.1975853/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3668-jpeg.1975854/]

And on the wing:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3674-jpeg.1975858/]

I wanted the bottom cover to be easily removed, so now that I had all my positioning set I added standoffs and magnets to the bottom of the cover and used the threaded bottom mount (which is steel and not aluminum) as the attraction point.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3669-jpeg.1975855/]

Fits well!

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3670-jpeg.1975856/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3671-jpeg.1975857/]

Looking at the reference, there’s a formed shape with what looks like a crossbeam near the bottom mounting point, so I added something similar.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3686-jpeg.1975864/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3682-jpeg.1975859/]

Now I had to sort out the electrical connection.  I was using these barrel shaped connectors here so I just wired one up, pushed the wires back through the apoxie sculpt (with a little X-Acto persuasion), and taped it together with the mount.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3683-jpeg.1975860/]

After cutting another hole I had room to slide the shroud over the connectors.  Great fit and right where I want it!

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3684-jpeg.1975861/]

I don’t have a pic, but the bottom cover fits over that all perfectly.

And that’s where we are.  I wanted to test the bottom connection point, so I remounted the B-Wing upright from the bottom.  This also let me make tiny adjustments to the positioning before I permanently mounted the shroud to the wing.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_3685-jpeg.1975862/]

And that’s the end of this update.  Hoping this weekend I can get the main gun mounted.  Stay tuned!

So I didn’t like how dark that ILM Stormy Seas blue was looking, even after misting it with the base coat, so I mixed up some Light Blue 65 with a drop of Engine Black and a few drops of Stormy Seas and got a much lighter, more subdued blue.  I’m much happier with this color!

Here’s a nice compare of the two colors together:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_4406-jpeg.2002172/]

I didn’t bother with the liquid mask this time — I’ll just paint on the chipping here, which is how it was done on the original filming model anyway.
