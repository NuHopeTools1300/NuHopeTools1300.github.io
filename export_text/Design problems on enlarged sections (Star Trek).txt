Your first pic, where Spock's shuttle flips before docking, is actually a matte painting. The details don't match, and the saucer dome is too pronounced. The side docking port was an enlarged model that again doesn't quite match. ILM's enlarged torpedo launcher/neck also has differences. The differences aren't so much mistakes, they were just made to serve their purpose and they didn't take the time to match every detail. 

The docking port on the space station wasn't even there originally, it was added as CGI in the director's cut (in an effort to correct a continuity error).

The change in the red decal for the docking ports was made when they went to the A. After the accident with the paint, and the need to make the A stand out from the shakedown version of the refit, the decals were changed in almost all places where there were registry and docking ports. There is one spot, though my memory fails at the moment to remember precisely where, that it still says 1701 and does not have the A even though it is on the A version of the ship, however. The original decal was indeed almost right on the edge of the docking port. And yes, there were no details on the 8' model with the little lock handles around it, that you see in the closeup of the docking with Scotty and Kirk using the larger partial model. I believe that TimeSlip put those on their version of the refit, but I am not 100% certain; I'd have to ask.
