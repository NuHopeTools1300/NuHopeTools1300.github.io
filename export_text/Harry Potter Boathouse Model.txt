For anyone interested in miniature building....I recently found myself presenting my folio to the man who was in charge of building the 'real' Hogwarts miniature used in the Harry Potter films. Luckily, he loved my model (phew!!!), but gave me a tip for scale windows like this. He said that he uses acetate and etches the lattice pattern onto it on both sides of the material. This makes the acetate buckle slightly and so when the model is lit, the light "kicks" off the windows, like a real church window. 

Top tip from one of the best miniature builders in the industry there, and as some of you guys were asking how I'd made my model, I just thought I'd share that!
