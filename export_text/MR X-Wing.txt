The "prototype" shown at Toy Fair is an assemblage of original cast parts to give a representation to the industry of what's to come. It is in no way indicative of the final piece, as the actual master model is still being worked on.

The X-wing MR are making is indeed a new version. Built to the dimensions of the original body parts we were supplied with, it is an all new beast, created from original kit parts assembled onto a new body and wings. It's being built by the team who created the AT-AT and the Millennium Falcon. It will have opening wings and lighting effects for the engines and cockpit as previously announced, and will be released in the livery of Luke Skywalker's Red Five.

Barry

That concept isn't a violation of business 101. In fact it what is called a "missed opportunity". 

In manufacturing  we constantly pull items out of the production line. Mainly for quality control purposes. Sometimes they are saved/documented for quality control purposes. 

MR could do this, they have a small enough market that it could done with very little loss. There is an American company that offers custom paint jobs with mass produced items, and MR is a giant compared to it.  The product is made overseas then shipped to the US, and painted in their Las Vegas shop. Of course they charge for it, that is business 101. It's also business 101 to follow the Nike model, who is only in the business of marketing, not making shoes. They subcontract everything, and collect the money. The X-wing looks to be another outstanding offering from MR. Yet, when I think about it, it seems a pity the X-wing should be the last Studio Scale Star Wars offering from MR. Think of what could have been, what we were anticipating...T.I.E. Fighters, Slave 1, a Star Destroyer...the list could go on.

Sure, another company could reach a licensing agreement with Lucasfilm, but whom and when? And if such a company does pick up where MR left off, will their quality be comparable to MR? Quality control issues aside--as I imagine most issues are resolved sooner or later--you must admit MR has made available items beyond our wildest dreams. And is it not for dreams that we are so willing to give in to enormous price tags to have in our possessions the closest replicas of our favorite on-screen icons.

I suppose we should expect that the next company to come along will learn from MR. I think especially of the MR USS Enterprise. What an immeasurable improvement upon a failed and disastrous attempt at such an offering only a few years earlier with Unobtainium. That scandal left many wondering if it were indeed not possible for studio scale replicas to be mass produced in any manner of acceptable quality. How wary we were when MR announced their intentions to release there own version of the Enterprise. What a pleasant surprise it turned out to be! 

At least we who are Trek fans can, for now, expect more from MR. But it was a sad day when we learned there will be no more from the Star Wars end. After this year I expect any Star Wars item with the MR tag on it will only increase in value.

It is as though the MR X-wing is released as an encore to a magnificent performance. When the encore is finished we know the show is over. Once the X-wing is released that particular curtain closes.

As a Star Wars fan I would like to thank Master Replicas for this incredible product run, which began with Lucasfilm in 2002. It was short, but oh so sweet!
