I'm back. I wasn't goint to show a mid paint on this beauty from Steve & the team, but I had to take shots under various light Temps to get the color I wanted. This was one tricky task indeed. Too blue, not enough blue so many color variants on ref pics etc etc etc I decided to try something new & worked out average (incorrect) light settings that people would've used taking the shots of the prop & mixed my color according to the new info. A lot of photoshop work to get were I wanted & many hours trying different paint mixes & different color temps!!! My conclusion was......Bollox to it! I've seen the prop in the flesh in Paris but only had a cheap camera but I clearly remember what colors it wasn't. All the photoshop stuff helped but when it came to the final color choice it was a lot of " a bit more of this & a bit more of that" type mixing. Finally I came up with a mix of Medium Blue/Grey, White, RLM green & another lighter blue. I really balls'd it up as I have only done 1 wing while I wait on some T track, I spilled the remainding mix only leaving me with very little paint to finnish the other wing & touch up work. I've thinned it down to get more out of it but it really is a case of fingers crossed. It wouldn't be me if I didn't balls something up:lol. 

For the look of the Tie I really dont like the brand new imaculate style some people have chosen, I decided to try & make it look like the actual prop does now with Dust, Scratches, blemishes & all. Its quite a scruffy prop up close so I didn't bother with an immense amount of clean up on the body & wings. I have added weathering to the wings & the Black area's need redoing due to the touch up work I've done. This has got many layers of color to get to where I am now but in the flesh it does have a great deal of depth to it. I want it to fit into my collection & not stand out as a clean beast so some may find it a bit weathered but HEY its my Tie & like it:love. These are some of the shots I just did quickly to show where I was at, different color temps where used to show the difference it makes to the color. Hope you like it

Thanks guy's. Its really hard looking at it with only 1 wing done but I'll get there in the end. A board member here is sending me the extra T track so as soon as I get back from my holiday next week I should be able to finnish it before I go back to work in May, I had to do the Archive look as it would do my head in staring at the clean version. I don't mind the canon situation as its for my own collection, & to honest as soon I started with the weathering it came to life. I just noticed that the guns are quite lighter as the new mix was used so it looks like it will be quite a tough job to match the wing colors. This is were the RLM Green comes in to play, I gave it a light coat over the blue/grey & it gave it a great aged look. Fingers crossed for Wing 2. A new trick I've picked up is using a putty rubber on the weathering, works a treat for removing/controlling  pastels, also with area's i would normally pre-shade I did it arse about tit, base color, shading, more base coat wash, then more shading then more base coat & so on, it gives a great shadow quality that you cant get with just pre-shading but the camera doesn't pick it up that well. I'm in  the middle of desinging a new type of display stand for the Tie as well.

[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]

Here is lots of pics of the Tie as it is now, all out doors in good sunlight with auto color temp settings. It still looks every color under the sun depending on the angle but I'm realy happy with it. Somthing had always bpthered me was the black panels on the wings, when I first did the flat black it looked wrong very matt, I always liked it when the wings picked up the lights or flash so I added a coat of satin clear over the black panels & it reflects just the right amount of light in any room or condition without looking too satin.

Any way hope you enjoy & remember it is not ment to look like the prop on day one but something that has been moved around for years from show to show & is dusty & unkept? I still may add the paint chips to reveal the mustard colored resin on the wings, gonna sleep on this?
