Well its about time that I got around to this. I have always hated the paint on the SS snowspeeder & was always disappointed that it wasn't the red/orange version. Not for long. After the earthquake the other day my speeder came off its stand & fractured the left wing. I was actually pleased as this gave me the go ahead to re do the weathering & all. Its only just been sanded & undercoated but already I've hit a few problems. I ended up sanding back to the resin in some places & could I get the undercoat to stick, could I fu**.

After cleaning thrice with detergant then white spirit, it just kept bubbling in a particular place!!!!!!! It was resolved by an undercoat for the undercoat of Dull coat. This seems to have done the trick, so far?

I'm hoping to get a base coat on today but have still not decided my paint scheme. I have enough ref of the standard SS red version, but after looking at Sculpting a galaxy I love the look of the version it was originally (Luke's speeder). I'm thinking of doing what I've done before & go for a Guy's version, a hybrid of all the things I like but staying as close to as original weathering as possible. I'll update some pics tonight & I will do a step by step on this one, just to show how flukes are a big part of my work :lol

Its now a black snowspeeder & looking rather mean. I've basically light sanded

the speeder to remove some of the raised weathering from the Factory, then applied 2 coats of Halfords grey undercoat & then 2 light coats of Halfords matt black. I've done the black so that I can break through the top coat when its on to show dark weathering patches underneath & to add a little pre shading in places. I'm about to work on my top color & an overall plan of attack.

Finally a mist of the white/buff mix to take the edge off the black before the top coat. Mixing that next

[IMAGE: IMG_3501.jpg]

Re: MR Snowspeeder has a new Jacket (repaint)

Update time. Had my usual disaster's today........ Mixed my paint wrong masking lifted.........Run out of red............ Forgot to all my chore's!! bollox to the last one. 

I started off by adding the liquid latex to the black/white areas where I wanted the heavy (down to resin) weathering.

[IMAGE: snowlatex.jpg]

Next I spent ages mixing my top coat, I ended up using a basic Medium Grey with some Buff & German grey added. Over this I did a light spray of Deck tan to warm it up a bit. There seems to be a greenish tinge to the prop but I avoided this as there are so many shots of the prop under different light. Over the Tan mist I did some patch misting with a medium brown & then a white.

[IMAGE: snowsection.jpg]

[IMAGE: snoaairbrush.jpg]

[IMAGE: Snowmistcoat2.jpg]

Next was the light panels that are dotted around the hull. This was tricky as  masking in tight area's can be a nightmare. As to the color, I spent ages mixing various white /grey /blue variations but could never decide. My first attempt didn't come out to nice so I reverted to white with a touch of medium grey & just a few drops of blue. It went on quite glossy but this will be dealt with when the weathering fun start. The right side of the hull has much less paint on the light panels, Learn as you go again?

The red/orange was my biggest problem, I only had a small amount of red & mixed it with yellow & a touch of brown. On paper it looked OK ish but on the grey of the hull it came out horrible. Like a muddy peach color. UUUHHHHH!. A quick dash into manchester got more color's & bombed it home. The final color is a mix of Red & a touch of Orange. over this is a mist of yellow. IT looks a lot redder in the pic's but to the eye is pretty close to my ref shots, even the girlfriend said so when she got in from work. All the latex was removed but some was so buried that I had to snoop around to find it all, even now putting these pic's up here I've spotted some area's I've missed! Cool.

Re: MR Snowspeeder to have a new Jacket (repaint) update 1

How much do I hate masking, I have spent the past few days trying to add all the small details like the little triangles, ZZ, etc etc. Never again! This was one of the most frustrating things ever. I must have cut 100 or so tiny triangles out of a pieces of masking tape trying to get the perfect one... no chance. I ended up going with one that was slightly oversized. In fact I think all the details are slightly oversized but hey hey. I think I'm at the point where I cant take anymore masking. Those red/white striped area's at the back must have taken my hours & there still a bit dodgy. If anyone is thinking of doing this then GET DECALS "Please" . I've done most of the panels that needed highlight's or darkening. For these I used a basic light brown or a white/deck tan mix. Its all still looking a bit blocky but no weathering has started yet. I've done some simple tone weathering with the airbrush but nothing substantial.
