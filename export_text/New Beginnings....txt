I'd like to take this opportunity to publicly say that the long-time fued between myself and Steve is

overwith and water under the bridge. I've spoken with Steve at length today on the phone  and I feel we had a healthy discussion on many a subject. Most importantly a friendship lost for a time. A friedship I can now say has been fixed and a renewed camaraderie  between two guys who have a passion for Studio Scale modeling is now back on track, the way it should be.  

 Steve has graciously offered to replace the old Tie fighter kit I purchaced from him years ago with one of his new kits as a show of good faith. I will be sure to post a full review once the model arrives, I can't wait. I've also got to thank Ryan for your email, it opened my eyes and opened a door I thought was closed. 

 I've had an unheathy attitude towards the hobby for several years now, people have rubbed me the wrong way and I them, I've also had a lot of personal problems over the past few years wearing me down. There was a post a long time ago when one of us, Dan K I believe was really close to finding a part. He had posted a photograph of what he had found trying to generate a discussion .  I spewed out a reply to his post like "not even close", rudely and purposefully trying to throw the guy off when he was really close. I  apologize for that, it was wrong of me. You deserved to be pointed in the right direction with the kind of determination you showed when you were so close to finding the correct kit. 

 My faith in the hobby has been rejuvenated and I've got Steve to thank for that. I look forward to future projects,discussions etc. I look forward to helping my friend when he needs it. May a stronger more determined erra in studio scale modeling commence. 

Thanks again Steve for everything, 

Jamie
