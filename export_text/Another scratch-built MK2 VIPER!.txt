Well, I was too impatient to wait for Moe & Frank's splendid model, so just after New Years I started on my own scratch-build of a Mk2 Viper. I've done a few props from scratch before and a good deal of regular modelling, but this is my first proper scratch-built model. 

Since patterning board seems hard to come by where I live (Sweden) I've used balsa wood, Evergreen sheets and (lots) of filler. I did some kit bashing for the rear detailing and cockpit. I am currently doing final cleanup and last week I did the decals by using Moe and Frank's pictures as a reference base. I am doing Adama's restored Viper which Apollo flew in the pilot.

I've been documenting the whole build and will be putting up a progress report/tutorial when it's done if anyone is interested. Though I will say- if you're not 100% sure that you want to do it all by yourself, get the kit instead. Because as you can see, the kit is shipping and I'm not done yet.

Thanks guys. I really wanted to give it a torn up look, like it had just been in battle with a hundred Raiders or something.

The paint chipping was done by basically using rough (100-180) sandpaper as a brush. I dipped the paper in silver/chainmail paint and "dotted" it on. At the same time I did short sweeping motions backwards to give the impression of space dust hitting the hull at high speed and scraping off the paint.
