Though in my experience, searching the RPF is a shot in the dark. Sometimes you score big with the info you want, sometimes you turn up nothing, and most of the time the real knowledge to be gained ends up in a thread that was never meant to be a tutorial, but rather the poster was just anal-retentive about showing off every little step.

What I would love to see is an entirely new section to the RPF that is just detailed tutorials, not instructions for specific items, broken into plastics, metals, prosthetics/rubber, textiles, painting/detailing, and so forth. 

Having a sticky in general modeling would short-change the community and limit the knowledge base, simply because good tutorials would cross the boundaries between multiple facets of the RPF's collective disciplines. That's not to say that others would not come in, it's to say that what is more useful? A thread on all aspects of vacuum-forming everything or a thread on vacuum-forming focused on a model making mindset?

I been a lurker on these boards for quite some time and have picked up a TON of knowledge. Unfortunately most of it comes from having read thousands of threads and picking up tidbits here and there (mostly in very unlikely places). 

Steve Neils blog is great. In fact - Steve has some very very good video documentation on mold making (the trick is finding which episode though...most of it I think was his work on the space helmet)

TrekWorks channel on youtube is another great resource.

Something I always have to remember to keep in mind is that a lot of the techniques that one may come across can be adapted to fit a whole host of projects with some simple tweaking. 

So while a thread on creating a pepakura iron man helmet may not be something you ever want to do - many of the techniques, tools and products used are something that may have direct applications for another project.
