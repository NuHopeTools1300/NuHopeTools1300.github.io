Hi guys,

I figure that I would bring some closure to this thread and post my findings that I posted over at Starship Modeler.  I recently received a number of flat dishes from Plastruct and the VHD-300 looks like the dish to be used for the 32" Radar.

Here's a comparison pic of the flat dish, the VHD-325 was used for these comparrison images, but it shares the same contour of the smaller "300".  The VHD-300 is in the bottom pic with some details taped to it.

[IMAGE: radarplast.jpg]

It looks like the vintage Saturn shroud has to trimmed at the base.  I've drawn a line around the base of the shroud where I believe it should be trimmed.  The new base should have an outer diameter of 21.83mm.  I've determined this number by the space between the Scharnhorst parts.  

To get an accurate cut, I've taken a piece of .060" styrene and cut a 24mm hole and placed the shroud inside.  

Using the styrene as a gauge for depth, I can then use my razor saw to cut the trim piece.

I cut out a piece of styrene to a 21.83mm O.D. piece and placed it in the spot where the shroud is destined to go on the dish, to demonstrate the spacial relationship between the dish, the detail parts and the shroud.

[IMAGE: VHD-300.jpg]

The VHD-300 dimensions are 76mm OD, 71.4mm ID and 13.5mm high, I think that this is close to if not right on for the Falcon radar dish.  

I haven't proven my theory on how to construct the dish, this is just what I came up with by measuring and comparing the detail pieces with the dish against the reference pics.
