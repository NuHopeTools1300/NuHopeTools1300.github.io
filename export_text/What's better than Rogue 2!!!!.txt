Cheers Jason, yeh and we both used very similar colours as our base coat, I went off the known beaten track slightly and tried things I've not done before to see how it ended up...mixed results but well happy with it and I think its stopped darkening now. I wouldn't want to paint another for a while but looking forward to getting ready to painting a 20 inch X-wing wing tonight 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]

I have to say photography and the eye are like chalk and cheese, to the eye Lee's looks slightly darker and mine Less warm, but quite warm, in daylight its a lot closer to the colour lee got but lighter and under house lighting the just go in all directions. This is the first paint job I've done were I intensionally mixed the base coat a lot lighter than I wanted to see how it looked a few weeks later, def a good technique when using Tamiya acrylics over a black base coat....I love learning.

I also learnt to stop pissing around with misting till I've done the hole ship, I had a nightmare matching the canopy and barrels to my some what tampered with base coat on the hull!!!!

I used a Royal Navy grey with flat white at about 65-70% white in the mix, then spritzed it about 4 times with very thinned Brown artist acrylics, leaving it on for about 30 secs each time then dabbing it dry..great results but would do it less if I did it again. Its misted with the 3 panel colours also but that ended up being pretty pointless to a degree (stop it Guy)
