These are a few of the reference photos that I notated paint colors on.

I am not saying this way is the best way. Just how I did it and to maybe help others along the way.

I used Model Masters flat black for the base coat (let dry at least one day). All colors from here are from Floquil. I am sure Archive-X will be just as good.

I then airbrushed Reefer White. It took 80% of one bottle. Save the rest as it will be used later in the painting process. Do not be heavy handed, you still want a slight trace of black to come through. Again, let this dry for at least a day.

Trust me when I say that once you get to the last step in painting, the white will no longer be white.

Steel wool the areas that you can do trying to not harm detail/greeblies. Making sure to only go from front to back and back to front! Make sure the steel wool residue is cleaned from every place. It likes to hide in all sorts of places. You do not want these particles in your paint work!

I did the following all in one day...

Start adding the panel colors. Yes, they will not look correct right now. They will in later steps.

I hand painted 99% of the damage trying to be as accurate as I can.

Do some weathering such as the streak marks. I mostly used Engine Black.

Reefer white misted over the entire ship to blend in colors, blend in the hand painted damage and the streak marks. Example, that Dark Blue will now be more grey blue. It's like magic!

Again... steel wool the areas that you can do trying to not harm detail/greeblies. Making sure to only go from front to back and back to front! Make sure the steel wool residue is cleaned from every place. It likes to hide in all sorts of places. You do not want these particles in your paint work!

The next day I added the blaster marks on the top (port) wing (note: I did these by hand as well).

Still to do for my build...

Add additional weathering (streak marks, damage, etc) if too light.

I don't think I forgot anything. If I did and I recall it, This post will be updated.
