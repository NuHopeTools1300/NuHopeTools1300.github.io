Thanks!  I understand what you mean.  The harsh studio lighting makes it look white,but this is in primer gray!  I use Plastikote,or duplicolor sandable primer. Duplicolor is easier to use,because it has a FAR SUPERIOR spray head,and you don't have to worry so much about orange peel texture. This way you don't have to spray in into a clean soup can,and then pour it into your airbrush jar to achieve a smooth finish.  You can use Plastikote,this is what I used to paint my most recent red 5 with.  This just takes a little more finesse when spraying it,and a lot more patience waiting for it dry between coats. I usually spray it in four passes,and it's done!  Of course,I've been doing this for twenty plus years!  Duplicolor I recommend for the beginner,or for convience.

The blue tower would be in 1/48 scale, for use with the 12" inch X-wing model.  The red one would be 1/72 scale used for the 8" inch X-wing model.   This diorama was never used in the movie,but it is modeled after some of the matte paintings,and the the tower used for live action sequences.  WWW.STARWARSARCHIVES.COM has pictures that were taken behind the scenes of the actual set that was used during the filming of the movie. This will give you a sense of what was used,and why.
