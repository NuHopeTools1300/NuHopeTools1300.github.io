Hey guys,

Glad to hear they're arriving and that you like them. 

Regarding the fit of Chewie into the seat - I think this is mainly due to his larger body which naturally makes him sit more forward (compared to Han's thinner body). I've also noticed that the control column / steering wheel seems to be in slightly different locations on some Falcons - this is obviously a variation in the way they were assembled originally. My own Chewie sits pretty far forward but I don't think it's too distracting.

If you really don't like it, the only thing I could suggest is "shaving" some of the resin off his back so that it reduces the width of his body and pushes him further back into the seat.

Chris

Ozzy,

They look great but I fear they will be too big for the MR Falcon. (There's always the possibilty that they could be cast in resin which shrinks though - I saw someone "shrink" a 1/4 scale head down to 1/6 scale and the results were pretty good).

The starting point for our figures was a 1/35 tank driver figure but he was definitely oversized for the cockpit scale, so we had to come up with our own scale for them.

We worked out that the size of figures needed to be approx 1/40 scale to fit into the cockpit seats accurately. Also, it's not possible for them to have lower legs because they can't be fitted into the seats. Or figures didn't have anything below their knees!

Here's a little size comparison that I did which shows the difference between the scale you're sculpting and the scale we ended up with. (I just picked a size of 6ft for Han as I believe Harrison was around the 5'11 - 6' height when he made Star Wars).

[IMAGE: Han-Scales2.jpg]
