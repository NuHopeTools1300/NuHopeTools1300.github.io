I'm just figuring the best way to support it and stop it from moving out of position as I turn so to keep the dome shape good.

I do have some pics of the destroyer already, but please feel free to post anything you have. The more pics and reference I have, the easier it will be and the more accurate I can make it. 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]

Sometime later, I will complete a Mothership using these mini Destroyers.

UPDATE!

Ok, I have made some progress on the dome shape for the 2" Destroyer. I cut a small circle from a piece of sheet styrene and packed the rear side with clay, so that the clay was exposed from the front face. I then took hold of a large circular shaped stencil and rotated this around on its edge on top of the clay to scrape out the dome shape using the rim of the pre-cut 2" circle as a guide.

Next, I filled the crater left in the clay with plaster and, after pulling it from the clay mold, I then attached a thin circle of sheet styrene to the underside to reinforce the fragile plaster cast (edges were very thin) before creating a silicone mold. I was then able to do a pull from the silicone mold in resin for final clean up. This will gve me a neat little dome for the main section of the City Destroyer. I will then proceed to cut out the smaller crater section at the front of the ship and add the vertical wall part.
