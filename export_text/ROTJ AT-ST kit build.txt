This is the Neisen kit as cast by Mike Salzo. 

I'm waiting on some brass to arrive in the mail. It will be used to hold up the eye lids and for the rack that goes on the top. I'm hoping to bend some brass rod into shape for the rack and hold it all together without soldering. The kit part for this piece was too rough to try and save.

I've got the interior kit but, since it didn't contain seats and would be too hard to appreciate when closed up, I left it out. It'll cut down on weight on the joints too.

There's a kit part that was missing or I lost - not sure which. It's the bolt-like part that goes where shown in the attached photo. Does anyone know what kit it's from? 

That assembly looks a little rough in the extreme closeup shot, and I'm hoping that a little primer will smooth out the construction a bit.

I also had to scratch some of the hinges on the top hatch. As far as I can tell, they seem plain as I made them, but in some closeup shots of the prop there seems to be additional detail on the sides.

I'm hoping to make the base look like Endor - at least that's the plan for now. I've got a deAgostini biker scout and a 1/24 scale speeder bike I might put at the bottom for scale reference, and to bring it to life a little more.

Thanks for looking. Comments and criticism are welcome.

Here are some areas that might be helpful if you're building one. 

First is the missing detail on the upper thigh. The part that was a trailing arm from a nebelwerfer (on the ESB AT-ST) should have the borders lined with strip styrene. I used white styrene strips and a .060 piece carved for the thicker piece seen in the pic.

The other thing I did was I found some 1/2 inch round mirror pieces on eBay. The mirror pieces represent the slight protrusion of the center of the thigh outer hub. It's the most precise way I could get a thin 1/2 inch axle part without having to cut a perfect circle in styrene. These I then surrounded with 1/2 inch snap rings also found on eBay. I got a snap ring assortment because there are larger snap rings that I used in the forward of the thigh. So I needed a slightly larger one to go around the kit part in that area.

I also replaced some of the screws with hex head socket screws I found at mcmaster carr. I just drilled the areas where they go and glued them in place.

Hi Scott, some really good work there, The thigh snap rings tip is one that I must use on mine (busy on a another project )!!!!

 The part you are missing on the guns was  missing on mine { if ever it was part of the kit ) Its easy to scratch with hollow brass tubes. The hexagonal nut you can cut from plastic sheeting. Drill the hole first (or several in my case to practise with) , cut a square around the hole then cut the corners off the square. You may have to do a few to get it dead right, its a tiny part!).

  You made the right decision with the interior, it adds a lot to the weight and I've drilled a bigger hole under it to take a bigger steel rod as support under mine. What length 3mm hex nut did you order out of interest?They look better than the cast ones  (which I might just knock off now).

PS You may want to consider adding a sliver of plastic to the front top of the "axle" plate to level  it off, and do the same behind  the "A" on its side shaped piece so the top of it falls exactly in the middle of the circle ( go look at my build) for photos.

PPS 

    The additional detail on the top hatch hinges is a thin brass wire drilled  through to hold it all together then the tips are cut and pushed down into the hull top. At least thats what I did with mine and it looks right.

Got the brass delivered. 

I used 6mm wide x .5mm thick strips for the leg tendons.

Used 1/32 rod for the eyelid supports. It took a few tries to get all of the dimensions right, but it came out pretty well. I just see some superglue that needs to be address by the look in the pics.

And the top rack - first attempt with 3/32 tube. Which of course tended to distort wherever it was bent. It came out reasonably close, but still doesn't have the crisp corners it needs to have. And it has the crimps in the corners no matter how much I try to finesse it to get rid of them.

I might get some 3/32 rod to try next. I really wish I could either fabricate it from brass rod that's soldered together, but that's a bit much I think, since it takes a torch to solder brass - from what I've known. I had a jewelry making class in college where I soldered some brass, and remember it's quite tricky. 

Either that or get a perfect resin cast part, which is nearly impossible. And will still have parting lines to deal with. So I'm kind of at a loss trying to figure out how to make the rack on top.
