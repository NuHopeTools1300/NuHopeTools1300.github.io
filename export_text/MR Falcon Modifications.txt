WOW, thanks for all the compliments, I'm glad you guys like what I've done with it. As for the base color, this was a bit tricky. The base they used was a "grime-white" mix of Floquil, but it wasn't even close, the ships much darker, even straight grime wasn't the right shade. Knowing that they no doubt went over the ship with light mists of various colors during the weathering process and considering that I had spent hours trying to get the shade right and used up all my Floquil paints doing this, I just used some Tamiya acrylics that I had and mixed a shade that was close to right out the gate and weathered it down a bit to match. The base I used was about 70% white and 30% buff, the ship overall(at least mine) has a very tannish look. I then did a wash with pollyscale grimy black(water based) and lightly misted a little yellow-brown-black until everything matched fairly well. It's really just trial and error, at least for me. The bottom doesn't have a lot of the heavy handed airbrushing on it and very few of the painted on streaks so I won't be repainting any panels, I'll just mask over the few painted streaks with some post its and re streak over them with the airbrush and then I'll be completely finished. I need help with the bottom though as I haven't thought of a good way to support the ship from the top, it shouldn't take long, maybe thirty minutes tops to go over those streaks so I'll probably get my wife to hold it up with the side rested on a pillow. Hope any of that is useful.
