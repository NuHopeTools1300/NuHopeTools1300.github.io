This past August I attended the Star Wars: Where Science Meets Imagination Exhibit in Indianapolis.  The staff was very strict about using any kind of illumination on the models. Fortunately the museum’s overhead lights illuminated the top surfaces of the models and produced excellent results. The underside of the models were poorly light, but my camera worked well in these situations. As in Ottawa, I used a Nikon D7100 camera with a Nikon 18-300mm f/3.5-5.6G AF-S DX Nikkor lens. The camera automatically adjusted for lens distortion. I used RAW mode which gave an image that is 6000x4000 pixels and is approximately 28 Mb. I used Nikon’s Capture NX 2 to do a basic conversion of the RAW file to a JPEG and applied the Auto level setting to each file, which produced a manageable file size under 4 Mb.

  I uploaded the files to my Microsoft Onedrive account (formally skydrive). It’s free to access the pictures. However, if you don’t have an account I believe you’re limited to downloading a maximum of 300 Mb at one time. It’s free to set up an account with them.

Use the following link to access the files: https://onedrive.live.com/#cid=B9FE0AEBDC25FDBC&id=B9FE0AEBDC25FDBC!19998

  You can find the following pictures:

  Millennium Falcon 5 foot ANH (2482 pictures)

  Tantive IV (2844 pictures)

  ISD Devastator ANH (2160 pictures)

  Sandcrawler (378 pictures)

  Landspeeder Full size and model (~200 pictures)

  Probe Droid (~150 pictures)

  Y-Wing (826 pictures)

  Hoth Macrobinoculars (108 pictures)

  Luke tauntaun stop-motion puppet (69 pictures)

AT-AT (92 pictures)

  Feel free to share these pictures, but no selling for profit and please don't post them to other websites without my permission.

Steven
