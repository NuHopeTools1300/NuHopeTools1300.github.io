I realize an Iron Man suite replica is technically a studio scale model ( if a suite was made for the movie) but you may have better luck in the replica costume forum.  That's where all the costume experts hang out.  I am a pep builder, myself, though.  I am no computer wizard, so I simply use point to point measurements to scale my pieces.  It is easier than trying to figure out percentages.  jackie'srockin was kind enough to post a scale chart, but most builders don't.  And you really have to read the entire build thread of the modeler, because there is a lot of information that comes out as the build progresses.  Some of the armor sharks are even willing to calculate the correct scale FOR you, because they are nice people.  I would, but I don't know how.  Like I said, I just use point to point.  Also! :  armor that connects to other armor HAS to be the same scale as its other components.  Like an arm- bicept elbow and forearm should be the same scale... chest and abs... thighs and shins and knee-plates, ect.  Good luck with your build!
