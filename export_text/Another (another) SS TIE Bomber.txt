It truly is the season for TIE Bombers!  This is my first studio scale build that I've ever done from kit parts.  I got turned onto this build by the folks over in the Nerf Herder's Workshop on YouTube/FB, and then also by Boxcar Bret's fantastic build.   I actually started this build a month or two ago and have just been slowly making progress -- and with everyone else out there sharing their builds, I thought I'd do the same!

And with that, here we go!

First, obligatory pics of kits collected thus far for the build:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/kits1-jpg.1835011/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/kits2-jpg.1835012/]

I also have the old tooling Shinano and few additional copies of some kits.  Still working on getting the Tamiya Yamato, but have a ways to go until I need it, so no rush there.  Anything I need many multiple copies of I plan to make castings of.

Easiest place to start on this is the wings!  I made sure to use the same wing from two different MPC Vader kits because as many others have noted, the wings are not mirror images and not from the same kit.  I began by adding 15 mm of .03" styrene stock around the outside edges -- this seems to be in line with what others have noted, and what I've rough-measured out of the Chronicles books.  I cut out the notches around the inside perimeter, then scribed the line around the outer perimeter on the inside surface of the wing.  I am pretty happy with the result.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wing_scribed-jpg.1835015/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wings_scribed2-jpg.1835016/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wings-jpg.1835020/]

These Vader MPC kits aren't the best and getting them off the sprues intact can be a challenge.  I had some damage from that process that I had to repair.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wing-repair-jpg.1835021/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wing-repair-1-2-jpg.1835022/]

It's all better now!

Feeling pretty good about this build so far.  It's not been too bad yet, but I know the difficult parts are yet to come.  Loving the challenge of creating this the way ILM did it back in the day.

Next post, my ups and downs of tackling the bomber-side "cockpit".

The port-side fuselage's front section, which I will likely refer to as the bomb-side "cockpit", was what I decided to take on next.  It looked like one of the more difficult parts to create, and since I didn't yet have tooling to start working on the fuselages themselves, nor have an armature to mount everything to, I figured why not?

I had read in a few places that the part used is a Plastruct VHE-200VG elliptical dish, so I picked up a few of those (plus a few VHH-200VGs and 2" dia ABS tubing).  I printed out a rough "template" using Moska's TIE Bomber parts reference, and got to cutting!

My first cutout seemed pretty good for the slot, though maybe a bit thick.  Figured it was just the angle and pressed on!

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/bomber_dome_1stattempt-jpg.1835208/]

During this phase, Boxcar Bret was kind enough to give me a hint or two about a faster way to do this, and so I took his advice and ended up with all of the notches cut out along the perimeter, and a pretty good octagon out the middle.  Test fit in the Prince of Wales part stuck to some stock material, and I thought it looked pretty good -- from above.  But something was off, and a few folks let me know...

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/bomber_dome_1st_att_2-jpg.1835209/]

...this was not the correct Plastruct part.  This dish sits too high, and the curve is off from the SS model -- it should be a little less "domed", flatter.  The correct part is VHD-200VG, so I nabbed a couple of those and began again.

This time I decided to build a little "jig" to hold my dome in place and allow me to draw the lines for the cutouts/octagon at the correct locations.  It worked out really well, I think!  Using measurements from the SS model references, and a little corroboration from Boxcar Bret, I applied a circle around the dome with a compass at the proper depth of cut.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/dome_2nd_attempt-jpg.1835210/]

Cut out the notches on my band saw, snuck up on them with my files and some sandpaper, and I was pretty darn happy with the result.  The PoW part fits right on it, and this now looks significantly better than the previous dish did.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/dome_2nd_attempt2-jpg.1835211/]

Now I just need to cast some copies of the PoW part and I can wrap this dome up and start on the actual pilot-side cockpit.  I've never cast any parts before, but also don't want to shell out $70-$90 for 3 more PoW kits just to get 6 more of those small parts so I'm going to give it a shot.

More to come!

This is the update that catches up to my current progress on this build.  While gearing up to start doing casting, I decided to tackle what I expect is going to be one of the hardest parts of the build overall -- constructing the wing connectors (pylons?)!

In most other builds I've seen (where images still exist -- older RPF posts are missing quite a few of them), folks are using some machined or cast parts for those wing connectors.  My understanding is that while almost all of the parts on the ILM TIE Bomber have been identified, that no one really knows how the wing connectors were built (which parts, components, etc).  With that in mind, I figured there had to be something out there that would make my life much easier, if only for part of the construction, and took to digging.

I figured a good place to start would be the Plastruct catalog, since I figured those parts would have been available back in the late 70s.  After a bit of searching, I came across Item # VHF-36, which is flanged head, used for vertical and horizontal tank ends for storage tanks.  It has a 1-7/16" diameter, which I measured to be very close to the dimensions needed to fit in-between the Vader wings.  Let's give it a shot, at least as a starting point.

Cap in hand, I placed it onto the wing -- and it's a perfect fit!  I couldn't believe it.  But, to my eyes, I thought that maybe the height of the flange was too high and I'd need to sand it down some.  So, I grabbed one of the Mörser Karl kit parts that attaches to the base of the wing connector to see how off it was.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wing_pylon1-jpg.1835270/]

It's not off at all!  The height on this is exact!  At this point I feel like it is too much of a coincidence that the diameter of this part, plus the height, are an exact fit here.  I'm very much leaning toward this being what ILM used for the base of the wing connectors.  Maybe a found part?  ^_^

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/barrel_fit-jpg.1835269/]

With that sorted, I poured over the Plastruct and Evergreen catalogs to see if there were tubes or other similar parts available that would fit onto this base part to help build up the connector, but didn't come up with anything.  At this point I figured I'd just scratch-build the rest of it from styrene stock.

I know the diameter of the tube that goes wing-to-wing is 3/4" OD.  Starting from there, I warmed up some .030" (.75 mm) and wrapped it around the tube to shape it into a circle.  Inserted that into the base, making sure it was tight to the ID and perpendicular to the base. 

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wing_pylon2-jpg.1835273/]

At this point it was still a bit loose fitting around the tube, so I added some .010" thick stock material around the inside to tighten it up.  I used the 3/4" tube to ensure it stayed in proper position and fit correctly.  In hindsight, I could have probably done it all with just .040" stock, but the thicker the material, the harder it gets to bend, so I do think this layered approach was the way to go.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wing_pylon3-jpg.1835274/]

Next I took quite a few measurements off a few different references, mostly Moska's parts guide and images from the Chronicles book, to ensure I got the size and shapes correct for the connectors.  These images all vary a bit, so of course the Chronicles book images of the actual model itself is the source of truth here.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/screen-shot-2024-06-29-at-10-39-25-am-png.1835275/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/screen-shot-2024-06-29-at-10-39-41-am-png.1835276/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/screen-shot-2024-06-29-at-10-40-19-am-png.1835277/]

As can be seen, the next two sections after the shaft connection point are actually conical in shape, not parallel to the shaft, and so I needed to measure the ODs of both the top and bottom of each section, as well as determine the proper thickness of each section.  I ran those dimensions (apologies, I don't have those numbers on hand) through a sheet-metal conical shape calculator, which told me the radii I needed for top and bottoms to get the proper shape, plus the angle in degrees I needed to cut the shape so when the ends meet, it's a proper cone.  I also had to account for the thickness of the material, so I ended up using the ID measurements for those radii.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/screen-shot-2024-06-29-at-10-51-17-am-png.1835279/]

To assist in supporting the conical shape, I also glued some thin strips of either .01" or .02" styrene stock to ensure the base of the cone had a mount point.  Then I began wrapping the base.  Did this twice for each conical section, gluing the styrene end-to-end.  For the bottom-most cone, I also had to ensure the proper spacing between the edge of the base and the base of that cone to allow for the Hanomag parts to fit in correctly.  Neither of these conical parts are the same thickness, either -- the upper one has a thinner lip than the bottom.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/pylon_cones-jpg.1835280/]

This was really looking correct to me, and I was very excited with my progress here.  I felt like I had unlocked some secret of the masters, and it pushed me onward!

The wing connectors have two strut sections that protrude off of it to the wings, and that was my next target.  I made a template out of cardstock, cutting it carefully and adjusting it until it fit snugly to the connector part I had constructed thus far.  Then cut it to the fit the wing and angle off the wing to the top conical ring.  Used that to create two side section, and cut a top piece to fit.  I also shaped where it made contact with the wing to sit overtop of the Vader wing protrusions.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wing_pylon4-jpg.1835281/]

Rinse and repeat for the other side.  Filled in the gaps with some Tamiya putty and sanded flush(ish).  Also drilled a hole through the base with a step-drill bit to 3/4" to let the tube pass all way through.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wing_pylon5-jpg.1835282/]

I was thrilled with how this turned out, but I won't lie, I didn't really want to make this all again.  So, I decided I'd add some of the needed kit parts and then down the line I'd cast it to make the other side.  Needs a bit more cleanup, but overall, looking good!

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wing_pylon6-jpg.1835283/]

A test fit with the kit parts attached.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wing_pylon_added-jpg.1835284/]

And who can resist dry-fitting some kit parts?

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wing_test_fitting-jpg.1835285/]

Whew!  That was a lot!  Challenging section of the build, but oh, so rewarding at the end there when it's all together!   I think it's starting to look really good, and once I get some castings made, I'll try and wrap up these wings for good.

That's all caught up now to where I am in the build.  I have plans this weekend to start working on the fuselage sections, though maybe I'll start working on casting some of these parts first.

For anyone who's done casting, how important is it really to get a vacuum chamber to pull out the bubbles?  I'm looking at using some MoldStar 30, which says it out-gasses itself and so long as you slow-pour it, you don't end up with bubbles -- but I imagine there will still be some bubbles, right?  Any advice here is welcome!

Til next time, keep on building!

Time for another update.  Lots of small incremental steps as I wait to get my hands on an armature as I’m not well-equipped to machine one myself.  Lets’s jump in!

What better place to start than the fuselage bodies?

I don’t have a lathe.  I considered sending the bodies out to have them precision machined, but decided I’d have a go at cutting the 12 grooves to make the 11 “ribs” that go around the fuselages.  Worst case is a botch them up and just have a set made for me later.

I started by measuring .375” from the front end of the 2” OD Plastruct tube and marking that with a parallel scriber.  I then placed dymo tape right up against that line to create a straight edge for scribing.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0653-jpeg.1837101/]

I then used my old Tamiya scriber and carefully went around the tube.  Once I’d made 3-4 passes, I removed the Dymo tape and finished widening the line until it was close to the proper measurement (around .6 mm).  I then added that line width and the rib width to the parallel scriber and scribed the next guide line.  More dymo tape, more scribing.  Repeat.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0667-jpeg.1837102/]

Eventually got them all done.  Cut the tube to length, then repeated it all again for the other tube.  Measurements all came out super close to correct, and while it’s not 100% accurate, I think it’s certainly good enough to pass!

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0674-jpeg.1837103/]

Next I measured out the center lines and figured out where the holes for the main support tubes had to go.  Drilled those out using a step drill bit.  Keeping all of that centered properly was a chore, but I consoled myself that once I have a center block of something is slight off I’ll have room to adjust.

Threw the bodies on the tube — I’m pretty happy with how they turned out!

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0675-jpeg.1837104/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0676-jpeg.1837105/]

Next up, the cockpit dome.   I had a pair of VHH-200VG domes, so first thing was to cut them down to size.  As others have done, one of the easiest ways to get the height to be consistent is to cut it off on a drill press with a cutting wheel.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0694-jpeg.1837077/]

Now that the height is close, I snuck up on it using some sandpaper until it looked just right.  I wanted the inner lip of the Vader cockpit window to sit inside of the dome, so I also cut/sanded that until it fit right in, nice and flush.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0695-jpeg.1837078/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0696-jpeg.1837080/]

With that done, I needed to scribe the vertical lines coming off of the vertices of the cockpit window.  This was a bit challenging, as the spacing is NOT equidistant between the spokes around the edge of the window.  So, I made indicator marks to align the window to keep it straight.

Taking a page out of Boxcar Bret ’s build, I made a jig to sit the dome into so that I could get some straight, vertical lines.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0697-jpeg.1837086/]

Once the lines were drawn, I applied dymo tape to the line and started scribing.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0698-jpeg.1837087/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0699-jpeg.1837088/]

Welp, that didn’t actually work out how I wanted.  Because of the curvature of the dome, as soon as you stuck down the dymo tape, it started to curve to one side, resulting in a line that veered one way or the other off-center.  This wasn’t good.

At this point I also noticed that the height was also off.  I had a small lip around the outside edge of the cockpit window, and per the reference, that should come up right under the cockpit.  Always check, double-check, and triple-check your references!!!   Good thing I had a spare dome!!

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0707-jpeg.1837089/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0728-jpeg.1837090/]

Since I’d already messed this one up, I also had a go at doing the horizontal scribing.  This was done by using a parallel scriber to mark the locations where the panel lines cross the verticals, then placing dymo tape from one intersection to the other.  For these lines you WANT there to be a curve, and the dump tape provides that beautifully.  So, a little practice before starting again.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0701-jpeg.1837093/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0720-jpeg.1837092/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0702-jpeg.1837094/]

OK, so second go at the cockpit, but proper height this time.  Plus I scribed the vertical lines using the jig to keep them straight.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0706-jpeg.1837095/]

Add the horizontal panels lines as described above, and finally using a pin vise and micro drill bit, add on the rivets.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0721-jpeg.1837096/]

With the cockpit window attached.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0709-jpeg.1837097/]

Test fit to the fuselage.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0710-jpeg.1837098/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0711-jpeg.1837099/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0712-jpeg.1837100/]

She’s starting to look like a ship!

thibkaji said:

			Time for another update.  Lots of small incremental steps as I wait to get my hands on an armature as I’m not well-equipped to machine one myself.  Lets’s jump in!

What better place to start than the fuselage bodies?

I don’t have a lathe.  I considered sending the bodies out to have them precision machined, but decided I’d have a go at cutting the 12 grooves to make the 11 “ribs” that go around the fuselages.  Worst case is a botch them up and just have a set made for me later.

I started by measuring .375” from the front end of the 2” OD Plastruct tube and marking that with a parallel scriber.  I then placed dymo tape right up against that line to create a straight edge for scribing.

View attachment 1837101

I then used my old Tamiya scriber and carefully went around the tube.  Once I’d made 3-4 passes, I removed the Dymo tape and finished widening the line until it was close to the proper measurement (around .6 mm).  I then added that line width and the rib width to the parallel scriber and scribed the next guide line.  More dymo tape, more scribing.  Repeat.

View attachment 1837102

Eventually got them all done.  Cut the tube to length, then repeated it all again for the other tube.  Measurements all came out super close to correct, and while it’s not 100% accurate, I think it’s certainly good enough to pass!

View attachment 1837103

Next I measured out the center lines and figured out where the holes for the main support tubes had to go.  Drilled those out using a step drill bit.  Keeping all of that centered properly was a chore, but I consoled myself that once I have a center block of something is slight off I’ll have room to adjust.

Threw the bodies on the tube — I’m pretty happy with how they turned out!

View attachment 1837104

View attachment 1837105

Next up, the cockpit dome.   I had a pair of VHH-200VG domes, so first thing was to cut them down to size.  As others have done, one of the easiest ways to get the height to be consistent is to cut it off on a drill press with a cutting wheel.

View attachment 1837077

Now that the height is close, I snuck up on it using some sandpaper until it looked just right.  I wanted the inner lip of the Vader cockpit window to sit inside of the dome, so I also cut/sanded that until it fit right in, nice and flush.

View attachment 1837078

View attachment 1837080

With that done, I needed to scribe the vertical lines coming off of the vertices of the cockpit window.  This was a bit challenging, as the spacing is NOT equidistant between the spokes around the edge of the window.  So, I made indicator marks to align the window to keep it straight.

Taking a page out of Boxcar Bret ’s build, I made a jig to sit the dome into so that I could get some straight, vertical lines.

View attachment 1837086

Once the lines were drawn, I applied dymo tape to the line and started scribing.

View attachment 1837087

View attachment 1837088

Welp, that didn’t actually work out how I wanted.  Because of the curvature of the dome, as soon as you stuck down the dymo tape, it started to curve to one side, resulting in a line that veered one way or the other off-center.  This wasn’t good.

At this point I also noticed that the height was also off.  I had a small lip around the outside edge of the cockpit window, and per the reference, that should come up right under the cockpit.  Always check, double-check, and triple-check your references!!!   Good thing I had a spare dome!!

View attachment 1837089

View attachment 1837090

Since I’d already messed this one up, I also had a go at doing the horizontal scribing.  This was done by using a parallel scriber to mark the locations where the panel lines cross the verticals, then placing dymo tape from one intersection to the other.  For these lines you WANT there to be a curve, and the dump tape provides that beautifully.  So, a little practice before starting again.

View attachment 1837093

View attachment 1837092

View attachment 1837094

OK, so second go at the cockpit, but proper height this time.  Plus I scribed the vertical lines using the jig to keep them straight.

View attachment 1837095

OK, been a week and some change, but I'm coming down the home stretch!  Let's see where we're at!

Continuing on from last time, I finished up the Leopold parts that get mounted to the tops/bottoms of the aluminum tubing.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wing_connectors_trim_1-jpg.1855554/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wing_connectors_trim_2-jpg.1855555/]

Next, the first of a few corrections.  In an earlier post I showed that I put that one Prince of Wales piece that mounts to the front of the bomber directly attached to the front panel, as I didn't have a lot of good reference showing its position otherwise.  Boy was I blind!  The Chronicles Vehicles book actually shows its position pretty clearly.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/front-panel-bottom-2-png.1855541/]

So, I decided to make the adjustment to place this component properly.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/front_lower_component_1-jpg.1855559/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/front_lower_component_2-jpg.1855560/]

And now since I'd pulled this piece out of the original front panel assembly, I decided I needed to fix that panel.  I revisited the reference once again and decided that what I thought was two pieces of sheet stock is actually a single sheet and the back "panel" is the aluminum armature block itself.  So, went back to the drawing board and reconstructed the panel.  I also noted that there should be a pin that is visible through the center of the panel, so I rebuilt it with that in mind.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/front-panel-redo-1-jpg.1855542/]

Note the hole drilled through the middle of this part now.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/front-panel-redo-2-jpg.1855543/]

Side view, showing those protruding parts should be flush to the top of the backing sheet stock.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/front-panel-redo-3-jpg.1855544/]

Mounting the pin.  I used small 1/16" dia brass pins I got from work.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/front-panel-redo-6-jpg.1855548/]

I mounted the pin to a thin piece of sheetstock (.020" thick) and then mounted that through the hole on the back of the panel. This was trimmed to fit within the threaded hole on the front of the armature block so that the panel would sit flush.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/front-panel-redo-7-jpg.1855549/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/front-panel-redo-4-jpg.1855546/]

You may have noticed above that I did, also, glue the bomber front to the fuselage. A better overall view:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/bomb-fuselage-front-1-jpg.1855523/]

With that attached, time to connect the 8 "clips" from the Sd.Ah.52.  For the perimeter, these all need to be trimmed fairly close to make the parts site relatively level to the larger front section.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/sd-ah-51_1-jpg.1855550/]

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/sd-ah-51_2-jpg.1855551/]

And get them attached:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/bomb-fuselage-front-2-jpg.1855524/]

And 6 more makes 7:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/bomb-fuselage-front-3-jpg.1855525/]

And this is where we hit up against correction number 2.  As you can see on the reference, this one clip part should be attached to the long Leopold part that runs the length of the fuselage.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/bomb-fuselage-front-5-jpg.1855528/]

And... I'm off.  Looks like my scribing on the top here was off by about 1.5 mm or so.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/bomb-fuselage-front-4-jpg.1855526/]

I considered leaving it and having just this one part be off...but, I can't.  I just can't.  *Sigh*  Popped off all the parts and used some Tamiya putty to fill in the grooves.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/bomb-fuselage-corrected-panels-2-jpg.1855521/]

...it's better.  I'll adjust it more after primer, but good enough for now.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/bomb-fuselage-corrected-panels-3-jpg.1855522/]

And the clip now fits in the proper location.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/bomb-fuselage-front-6-jpg.1855529/]

While I'm messing with the front of the bomb fuselage, might as well go all in!  Mounted the other Sd.Ah.52 parts that go on the front.  A word of advice!!  Place a piece of tape across the opening.  Don't ask how I found this out...

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/bomb-fuselage-front-7-jpg.1855530/]

And the clip piece.  Used a toothpick to position it the proper distance up from the center.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/bomb-fuselage-front-8-jpg.1855531/]

And that's the pair.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/bomb-fuselage-front-9-jpg.1855532/]

Oh, and here's the bottom center panel all cleaned up.  It needs some additional stock in the center to make it accurate, but I'll do that later.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/center-bottom-1-jpg.1855538/]

Last Morser Karl part added to the bottom:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/cockpit-fuselage-bottom-2-jpg.1855539/]

I also mounted the cockpit dome to the fuselage, and added the last small part that was missing there:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/cockpit-top-6-jpg.1855540/]

The wing end caps have what have been identified as pin connectors, likely for providing power to the lights, so I added some small 3mm dia brass tubing to the round sections on those endcaps.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/wing-caps-pins-1-jpg.1855552/]

I think I just need to permanently attach the cockpit front to the dome, add another pin to the front panel, figure out how I'm going to attach the wing endcaps/front panel/top panel so they are removable but firmly attached (probably magnets in some way), and finish up the bottom panel.  Then I think I'll be ready for primer!

A few pics showing where we are.  It's been a long road to get here, and I can see the end of the road coming up fast.  Til next time, keep on scratchin'!

OK, I promised some info on the hash marks.  I based all of my hash marks on reference photos I found online, mostly from nice closeups hosted on Modeler Magic (Modeler's Miniatures & Magic).  I used a mechanical pencil, .7mm lead dia, as that’s what I had on hand.  I did give an initial try with a #2 pencil, but had to keep sharpening it and so I abandoned that pretty quickly.  These are not exact to the reference, but fairly close for being freehand with no measurements made.

thibkaji said:

			Time for another update.  Lots of small incremental steps as I wait to get my hands on an armature as I’m not well-equipped to machine one myself.  Lets’s jump in!

What better place to start than the fuselage bodies?

I don’t have a lathe.  I considered sending the bodies out to have them precision machined, but decided I’d have a go at cutting the 12 grooves to make the 11 “ribs” that go around the fuselages.  Worst case is a botch them up and just have a set made for me later.

I started by measuring .375” from the front end of the 2” OD Plastruct tube and marking that with a parallel scriber.  I then placed dymo tape right up against that line to create a straight edge for scribing.

View attachment 1837101

I then used my old Tamiya scriber and carefully went around the tube.  Once I’d made 3-4 passes, I removed the Dymo tape and finished widening the line until it was close to the proper measurement (around .6 mm).  I then added that line width and the rib width to the parallel scriber and scribed the next guide line.  More dymo tape, more scribing.  Repeat.

View attachment 1837102

Eventually got them all done.  Cut the tube to length, then repeated it all again for the other tube.  Measurements all came out super close to correct, and while it’s not 100% accurate, I think it’s certainly good enough to pass!

View attachment 1837103

Next I measured out the center lines and figured out where the holes for the main support tubes had to go.  Drilled those out using a step drill bit.  Keeping all of that centered properly was a chore, but I consoled myself that once I have a center block of something is slight off I’ll have room to adjust.

Threw the bodies on the tube — I’m pretty happy with how they turned out!

View attachment 1837104

View attachment 1837105

Next up, the cockpit dome.   I had a pair of VHH-200VG domes, so first thing was to cut them down to size.  As others have done, one of the easiest ways to get the height to be consistent is to cut it off on a drill press with a cutting wheel.

View attachment 1837077

Now that the height is close, I snuck up on it using some sandpaper until it looked just right.  I wanted the inner lip of the Vader cockpit window to sit inside of the dome, so I also cut/sanded that until it fit right in, nice and flush.

View attachment 1837078

View attachment 1837080

With that done, I needed to scribe the vertical lines coming off of the vertices of the cockpit window.  This was a bit challenging, as the spacing is NOT equidistant between the spokes around the edge of the window.  So, I made indicator marks to align the window to keep it straight.

Taking a page out of Boxcar Bret ’s build, I made a jig to sit the dome into so that I could get some straight, vertical lines.

View attachment 1837086

Once the lines were drawn, I applied dymo tape to the line and started scribing.

View attachment 1837087

View attachment 1837088

Welp, that didn’t actually work out how I wanted.  Because of the curvature of the dome, as soon as you stuck down the dymo tape, it started to curve to one side, resulting in a line that veered one way or the other off-center.  This wasn’t good.

At this point I also noticed that the height was also off.  I had a small lip around the outside edge of the cockpit window, and per the reference, that should come up right under the cockpit.  Always check, double-check, and triple-check your references!!!   Good thing I had a spare dome!!

View attachment 1837089

View attachment 1837090

Since I’d already messed this one up, I also had a go at doing the horizontal scribing.  This was done by using a parallel scriber to mark the locations where the panel lines cross the verticals, then placing dymo tape from one intersection to the other.  For these lines you WANT there to be a curve, and the dump tape provides that beautifully.  So, a little practice before starting again.

View attachment 1837093

View attachment 1837092

View attachment 1837094

OK, so second go at the cockpit, but proper height this time.  Plus I scribed the vertical lines using the jig to keep them straight.

View attachment 1837095

Add the horizontal panels lines as described above, and finally using a pin vise and micro drill bit, add on the rivets.

View attachment 1837096

With the cockpit window attached.

View attachment 1837097

Test fit to the fuselage.

View attachment 1837098

View attachment 1837099

View attachment 1837100

She’s starting to look like a ship!

Next up — bomb chute, wing details, and creating molds.
