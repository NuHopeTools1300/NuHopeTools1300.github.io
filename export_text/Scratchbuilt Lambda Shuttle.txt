Guy, yeah lighting is always a nice touch !

So :

Why ?

Because that's close to the original (even if the neon had a "U-shape").

How ? 

Cant be more easy, took me 2 min to put them into the fuselage with 2 sides tape (included with the neons !).

Those are cold neons, this mean no heat ! They are run by 12 volts.  A switch is also included 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
 No wiring to do... The life of those neons are about 15000 hours !

Where ?

I bought them on ebay (I cant give the link here) from Italy. It costed me 15 euros (shipping included). They come in different colors and size. They are deisgned to light your computer ! But I had the idea to put them in the butt of my Shuttle. And it works well... The light kit come with the 2 neons, which are 10 cm long (20 cm is exactly the width I was needing for my shuttle ! Actually this is more than 20 cm because you have the tip of the neons ! It adds about 4cm).

Look for that on ebay : "REVOLTEC MINI NEON BLUE 2 X 10 CM - COLD CATHODE CCFL".

Yeah it will fly !

Haha just kidding but the wings will be motorized !

I hope it helps and gives some ideas...

[IMAGE: 4919-min3-2v-max3-4v-20ma-min1-715mcd-typ1-970mcd-max2-230mcd-if-20ma-min7-000k-typ8-000k-max9-000k-120-tsod-260-5-seconds.jpg]

(I rewrote this diagram again. 5/3/2011  You can use fluorescent light for injection nozzle.)

These LED blinks at intervals approximately 2 seconds. If you want to change the blink time, you have to calculate in this formula.

Ｔ(Second) = 1.1 * R2(MΩ) * C1(μf)

  2.7MΩ = R2  ---> ?

  0.68μf = C1  ---> ?

                 1.1 * 2.7 * 0.68 = 2.0196(sec)

  Approximately 1.5(sec)

      R2 = 2.0MΩ

      C1 = 0.68μf

                 1.1 * 2.0 * 0.68 = 1.496(sec)

  Approximately 1.0(sec)

      R2 = 1.4MΩ

      C1 = 0.68μf

                 1.1 * 1.4 * 0.68 = 1.0472(sec)

The white on the Shuttle is a warm white for shure, any blue is due to the back base coat showing througha thin layer of paint.
