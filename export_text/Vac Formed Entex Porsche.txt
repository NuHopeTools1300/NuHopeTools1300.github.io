Thanks all for the kind words.

As to Lee's question about shrinkage...I don't think shrinkage is  a problem.  The curved shapes make it hard to measure.  :facepalm  I measured the door part using my digital calipers.  From top to bottom of my door I am .008" (.2mm) too short.  Over the length of the door I am .02" (.54mm) too short.  I think those numbers can easily be explained by a couple too many passes of the sanding block.

A bigger nit to pick is the  thickness of the parts due to stretching.  After taking several measurements of the original Porsche parts, generally the body and doors are .08 thick.  The bumper is .06.  My rear fender that I pulled with .08 sheet measures .06 around the edges.  The bumper that I pulled in .06 sheet measures around .046.  I'm working in my kitchen with a home made vac table, so I don't have any fine controls.  Once I smooth out some of the rough spots in the bucks I will experiment with heat/droop time and maybe starting with a thicker sheet.  Any vac forming advice would be welcomed.

Starting with a thicker sheet will obviously help

right off the bat, if that's the way u need to go

for some of the parts. I never play around with

temps to much past 350 deg, that's plenty of temp

for forming up to 3mm. look at the droop of the plastic, you 

want it to have about as much droop as the pattern

is high. If you get to much droop it can web, if not enough

well then it won't form well at all. You have to play around 

with it and get some experience.....you will pay some dues

but that's always part of it.

Are U  using HIPS High Impact Poly Styrene?

Looking good Michael!  I can't wait so see how you finish them up.  

I made the thin pulls using .02" sheet.  It is quick and easy to form.  My intention is two fold.  Like Michael said the definition is much crisper.  Any problem areas that may need attention on the .1" pulls should be more noticeable on the .02".  Also, like with everything Slave-I, trimming mistakes can be costly.  I thought the thin pulls would be a good way to test out a trim before you go cutting a real Entex, my thicker vac pulls, or even resin copies if you have them.  

I rigged my digital calipers and measured the thickness of the kit and my pulls.  The doors and fenders of the kit are generally .08" thick, the bumper is .06".  I used .1" sheet for the doors/fenders and .08" sheet for the bumper.  The completed vac pulls measure within 1/100 of an inch of the real thing!   About 1/4mm for you metric folks.

They will require some cleanup and detail work, but they should make pretty nice styrene substitute for the Porsche kit.
