Its DONE - finally! The TIE Bomber is completely removable and reversable via a custom-designed "hinge" I created (note the wings are up "into" the gantry). That was the trickiest part, besides drilling through the plexi case to make it "float"...

I finally got it to a makeshift photo studio without breaking anything... I am very proud of everything, since I hadn't scratched/built anything besides house models in 15 years. (That, and working in engineering 60-100 hours/week for almost two decades). I'm back to the hobby, completely stoked, and got my next five projects lined up already. Just need to clone myself and... :cool Me so happy now!

I still am learning model photography, but here are the final pics. Pardon a few shots [still] with reflection. Most are pretty good. I tried to light it with flourescent overheads to give that "Imperial cold" feel. You can also get a feel for the size of the case (17 in x 18 in x 30 in). I'm just happy that with all of the design I put in to this "relearning" project, that I remembered to make it fit through a standard door... :thumbsup

I also have the full buildup pics of the Bomber and the gantry, if anyone wants to see them... I had done the buildup elsewhere... but would be glad to repost them and more. I'm a newbie, so go easy on me... hehe it's been 15 years... well, 16 now that it's done... I have learned much [young one], but I'm not a master, yet...

Aww shucks... :$

How the hinge works: there's a set of five thin, but stiff, diameter wire rods that feed through a bolt and through the two aluminum rods supporting it. Originally there was one thick rod, but it was hard to level/adjust the ship. The slight "bend" in the thin rod bundle allows it to help settle the weight over center, as well as make slight adjustments.

The nut is epoxied in, so you could turn the bolt to turn it backwards, after first popping off the end cap and pulling the rod bundle out (which is handy for replacment, too), then putting it all back together.  At least I think it all still fits backwards, since I moved the hanger back slightly late in the project.

Why use the extra Leo and MK parts in the hinge/hanger?  That was part of my tribute to main contributor kits.  It took several designs to make that work and still expose the ship details from all sides...

[IMAGE: TR005.jpg]

[IMAGE: TR027.jpg]

Flip the hinge back, carefully slide the ship in, and the wings just clear the gantry edge, making the ship pull up into the middle of the rack. That was a key feature I wanted to pull off. Of course, this is all while the heavy case is balanced over the edge of the table and you are reaching backwards up into it... trying to not have it fall and slice through your neck... :wacko  It was either that or cut a working hole through the table...

Thanks all.  I was really shooting for originality after being inspired by several of the SS TIE Bombers (ok, ALL SS) built up from time to time on these forums.  This would never have worked out without an enormous amount of design efforts to make everything fit together, sometimes months down the road.

Even with my crazy schedule, hopefully I can get one of these dios done per year, depending how big they are... and um, how much space the wife lets me continue to consume... :love

For me, to be original or unique, my dios have to:  interpret (not necessarily copy) an action scene, tell a story by looking at it, and have some structural elements to it (my specialty).  I have my next two projects finally picked... now just need to get to designing again so I can start building in the fall when I generally have more time.
