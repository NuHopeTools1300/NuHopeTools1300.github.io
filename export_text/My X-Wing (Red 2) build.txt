Here's some actual Red 2 stuff. I did the panelling on the Saturn V engine cans. Not perfect, but I'm happy with how these turned out.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0591-jpeg.2006091/]

Without first-hand reference, I'm not sure I can do much better than this. Figuring out panel dimensions on curved surfaces from photographic reference can be tricky. And if you don't want to get into differential calculus (I don't) then you can counts of the 'ribs' on the perimeter of the cans to align the panel edges. I applied tape to a can and used a combination square to draw some lines.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0574-jpeg.2006092/]

In case you're wondering: I bought a couple of partially assembled kits as 'salvage', thinking I could do a disassembly. I was wrong, but they ended up being useful for practice, which I think ultimately did save me money even though I ended up buying more kits than I had planned! In any case, that's why I'm doing this on an assembled stage-3 above.

Once the lines were drawn, I scanned in the image and made some paper templates.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0577-jpeg.2006093/]

I taped these on to check fit. The circumferences are probably pretty close, but I had to more or less decide the length by eye. I could have gone to greater lengths with software, but decided to eyeball it.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0580-jpeg.2006095/]

After some adjustments in the vector art, the next step was to get into styrene on my practice cans so I could practice applying and do some final adjustments:

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0581-jpeg.2006096/]

I found it helpful to use PVA glue stick (rather than tape) to apply the templates to styrene. This helped keep the scribe lines true, but was also useful when applying the panels to the engine can as it prevented me from gumming up the surface with solvent-welded fingerprints.

[IMAGE: https://www.therpf.com/forums/attachments/img_0584-jpeg.2006097/]

I'm attaching a PDF of my panel template if you would like to use it or improve it or to laugh at how wrong they are. It's up to you. I won't deny you whatever gives you joy.
