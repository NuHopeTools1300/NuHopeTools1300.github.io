So its been pretty obvious for years that the A-wing was based around a Tomcat, it goes without saying but seeing was another thing, thanks to some very good friends sharing some very rare pics Ive been able to recreate the A-wing Marquette to a degree!!! Sadly not much good ref exists but enough to confirm some of the kits used, the shape and a bit more!!

Its been really interesting to me having built its bigger brother, theres so much that you can work out why and how ILM did the big one as they did, and in actual terms its really clever what they did on the mini

Sadly theres not a lot of Ref on it so I decided to make up what I couldn't see or ID due to picture quality and the fact that Its never reared its head infront of my eye's before I kinda thought its un likely to again in the near future...so went ahead with build with what info I had

Still need to Id the cockpit and bulkhead but have a few kits on standby that will almost be spot on, but not 100% to the mini

This is a hugely fun build and cant wait to Finnish it when I return ...muwahahhhaah

------------------update:

Well, cannot find any  clear images online but what I have found shows the trunking includes  the main gear bay roof (just like the real thing) and there are indeed  the bracing 'rings' molded on the part.  There is also two good sized  tabs along the long axis that depicts the horizontal wheel well  bulkheads that would be ideal for placing the A-Wing's fins - just twist  the trunking parts to apply the fin's toe-in configuration.  However,  this would also make the 'split line' of the trunking be canted also, so  the engine pod contour is not regular in the vertical plane, but  aligned with the plane of the fins instead.

For what its worth...

Found this PDF of the original Hasegawa kit issue instructions which shows the part in question:

http://designer.home.xs4all.nl/models/F16-32/Hasegawa-F16A-1-32.pdf
