Started out on this last week, the NiceN/Salzo studio Snowspeeder.

First off, i had the first gen of this kit, and although, Nick really did do great job on the pattern (lets face it, its a very tough one to nail) the kit quality just wasnt up to my par, but then, iam VERY critical of resin kits in this day and age.

Now onto this kit, nothing you guys havent seen before from Richard, but i have to second, and sing its praises.

Mike has done a FANTASTIC job in the casting of this kit the hull being resin does have a tendency to warp, and there are a few minor misallignments, but its all easily over looked (and easily fixed in the case of the hull) when the overall quality is this good.

Im yet to find any major areas that need putty, though, i will find them as im over anal on bubbles lol, this is, the first dust of primer here, and i was very pleasantly surprised, on how little clean up/putty work i have to do.

Still much to do, and im just waiting on the hardware to get her mounted to a Panavise, but if you were on the fence about this, get one, seriously, i gave up for the sake of my own anal sanity on scratchbuilding this prop, this kit, is a very close second.

More to come soon, after a long break from ANY studio modeling, im just having a bit of fun for now, it all got a bit too serious for my liking lol.

Hey Lee: this is what I did with mine. you can see where I added strips to it. I just wanted to add some weight to the lower horizontal pieces.

Currently Richard is working this up into a finished piece. He's going to give it the paint job with the red markings, and I noticed a little late that the canopy on that one has thinner pieces in this area...oh well. Never noticed until then the difference in the canopies. In fact I don't understand if the two models were different in other ways.

By the way, I did this by laminating the thickest styrene sheet I had to achieve the same depth as the model canopy. Then I cut strips. Then I filled and sanded until the seams disappeared. Hope it helps.

Re: Another Snowspeeder.

Hi Lee,

Its great to see you getting stuck into this. I can't help noticing that your middle window sections on the sides of the canopy are looking a bit wide?

The frontmost vertical support should always sit behind the trailing edge of the roof window. Your one has the spar sitting well forward of it. 

When I did the canopy patterns I didn't follow the MR one too closely as ( to my eyes) it looks a bit 'chunkier' than the ILM version, possibly to aid in the manufacture of the parts out of polystone resin which is quite brittle. I'm sure you can make loads of improvements on what I've done but I would just re-check those side windows.

Cheers

Nick

Started some of the weather, chipping process. Feeling like a duck out of water, its been a LONG time since i painted something in this league.

All red markings are paint, and most markings will stay that way, decals will ruin this, and a few on the supplied sheet are not quite right, so, so it the old way, paint them lol......ive done my best so far, there sooooo fiddly.

Lee,

i guess it is easier than you think.

It is not necessary to vac-form those clear visor parts. Build a pattern/form in shape of the visor (maybe wood is not the best material, i think milliput is better)

and glue it on a rod/pole, fix it in a wooden block or a vise. Take a much bigger piece of clear plastic sheet (maybe 15 x15 cm ?), heat the middle of the piece with a heat gun and pull it over the visor form with both hands. Now you can cut it in the shape you need.

After that you can airbrush it with a transparent orange colour.

I use this technique on my Red 2 X-Wing Pilot. Yes, i know your visors are much bigger but you should try it.

Thanks buddy. The base colour is Tamiya Sky grey, mixed to flat white, with a tiny drop of deck tan, it just warms the grey a bit and works very well.

All other markings and weathering, were made from the base coat mix, by adding dark (German grey) hues for the stripes and scoring. The rest, was a case of washes, misting and scuffing, really not a science one lol, all done on the fly by eye, Seven. No stark blacks were used at all, and all the red used is Tamiya flat red, i added a touch of sky grey to it to take the edge off, then used it for all airbrushed and hand painted markings.
