I can only speak for myself, but when ever the Slave 1 is invoked (like summoning a demon), I make it a point to credit the backs of whom everyone stands on to get it where it is today (in all it's various iterations - I think there are three simultaneously now?)

It all started with Allan Hansen, who's probably $5-7K in the hole with nothing to show. He's the godfather of the project and suffering the most.

IDers were Ed Minto, Dan Loes, Isel Caro, John Currila, Lee Ralph, Julien, Guy, for sure. I assume Neisen had more guys IDing too, but off the top of my head it was those fellas at the time, and the first version was born on Kurt Kuhn's board.

REL made the first go round from what turned out to be mostly Max Mill's initial work, and made a handful of fiberglass hulls. Since these were ported off the Fine Molds model and scaled up, they were no bueno.

I spend a couple thousand a year or two later, trying to make parts of a bad fiberglass casting work - enlarging the skirts mostly, per Allan's guidance. George Takacs had a guy do a run in vacformed plastic, and the results were incorrect and displeasing to a lot of people. Attempt aborted.

Guy partnered with Rob from the ground up, then it fell apart.

Guy has a new project with his crew, Rob continued what he started with Guy, and there's the Disney one that is clearly ready for primetime, though probably not up to the exacting standards of the hardcore small group of guys that have fought long and hard (with each other and to get a Slave 1 made).

I'm SURE I left stuff out, but that's an overview?

I have, since the failed vac run, been waiting for the first viable Slave 1 from some one else to come to market. I still have enthusiasm for it, but I'm not dying on that hill again.
