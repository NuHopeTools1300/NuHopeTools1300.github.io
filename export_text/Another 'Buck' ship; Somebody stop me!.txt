More updates in the Flicker album...

Also, a little background on the model:

It was first seen in 'Plot to Kill a City' where Buck impersonates mercenary Rafael Argus- the sled was Argus' ship (with a sexy talking on-board computer)

In the very next episode, 'Return of the Fighting 69th', it is featured as the 'Seymore Class Cargo Sled'- the ship used by the retired squadron to attack the bad guys' asteroid base. 

The same interior cockpit set was used for both episodes and it matches what you see on the model pretty well, but the latter episode portrayed the ship as having a 'tail gunner' dome somewhere behind the cockpit between the engines, although no such detail existed on the miniature- this makes me think that the model was built for the 'Plot to Kill a City' episode.

The sled also appeared in several other episodes of the series, many times as a stock shot from one of the previously mentioned episodes.

Although 'reuse and recycle' was commonplace on shows like "Buck", the use of the sled in these episodes is consistant with it's portrayal as an older model 'cargo sled and bomber as mentioned in 'Return of the Fighting 69th'.

The only thing that doesn't make sense is the 'cargo sled' part- not sure where the cargo would go, unless it pulls some sort of cargo container (an interesting thought...)

Ahhhh, my favorite Buck Rogers design behind the Ranger 3 and the four seat Starfighter! I've always loved the look of the Sled belly bomber.

BTW, the turret gun set used in "Fighting 69th" was a reuse of the celestrial dome set from the Battlestar Galactica episode "The Hand of God". Regardless of whether or not the miniature was designed to have a turret back there, the set at least was a hand me down as opposed to something new built for Buck.

Somebody mentioned the astro sled from "Olympiad". Yes, it was a smaller craft than the sled, but it did appear to be a re-use of a mold for the Seymour/Sled in much the same way that Ranger 3 became Lunar Shuttle Avion in Galactica and the Searcher shuttle in Buck season 2.

The Sled from Olympiad was initially built for one of Doug Trumbull's early ridefilm efforts - both a full size prop and a miniature.  I'm not 100% positive of this, but I was told that it pre-dates his work on CE3K.  It was around the shop when BR went into production, and at some point was co-opted by BR.  A copy was made (not sure if the original was molded or if it was just remade using the existing miniature as a guide) and used as the "69th" sled.  (I know the "Fighting 69th" aired first.  I'm not sure of the production order.  People's memories of this period are fuzzy and I'm still trying to sort it out.)

The kicker to all this is the fact that the Olympiad sled is a Ralph McQuarrie design. Check out the "Art of Ralph McQuarrie" book........

69th aired before Olympiad, but "Plot to Kill a City" aired before both episodes. I am pretty sure "Plot" was produced first as the original intention with Buck Rogers was to do it as movie of the week style two hour long episodes (Galactica had similar plans which we saw with the Kobol and Gun on Ice Planet Zero episodes) until the decision was made to go with a full season of hour long episodes. Given that both "Planet of the Slave Girls" and "Plot to Kill a City" were two hour long stories, their scripts were likely finished first and may have been in pre-production when the hour long episode format was settled on.

Interesting tidbit about Trumbull being the originator of the set piece and possible basis model and McQuarrie apparently designing it. Weird how things like that work themselves out.
