the problem with a new edition is always the justification for it. As a rule, there are identical models with higher details, which will put a reissue of older kits for the masses into question.

I just had a conversation with someone yesterday about the reissue of the sealab. It is possible that the interest in this kit will not be of interest to the masses and, as suspected, is only aimed at model builders who need parts for other models. The consequence would be that not much is produced because sales are lacking. Accordingly, this kit would not be available on the market for long because the economic aspect is missing.

It is similar with the Bandai 1/48 Panther G model, there are much better models with more details, Bandai will not re-release it because they probably take exactly this aspect into account.
