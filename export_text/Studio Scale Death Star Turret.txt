Click to expand...

At the beginning the gun position will be hold with a screw. Then I'll motorize it, I have 2 options : servo or geared motor (like the original, the rotation of the turret was achieved with a belt).

Servo :

pros : easy to deal with, limited travel dont need to use limit switches, "plug and play", can be hooked directly to an arduino (what I plan to use), cheap

cons : weak (if someone is trying to move the guns by hand), use power to hold any other position than neutral, makes noise when holding any other position than neutral

Geared motor :

pros : can hold any position without using power, very strong, same system as the original, speed can be adjusted via motor controller and arduino

cons : need limit switches, need a motor controller to be pluged to the arduino

Lee, it's not as pristine, everything was cut using a small modelling handsaw 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
 Holes were eyeballed (it shows haha).

Well I've done more work, it is incredibly strong !

I cut several disks (10) with a center hole with my CNC. Some go inside the PVC barrel and are attached to the aluminium tube, some inside the aluminium tube to hold a smaller aluminium tube 
[IMAGE: yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7]
 and the last ones go into the MIG barrels and hold a brass thick tube that slide inside the previous small aluminium tube.

The MIG barrels can move forward and backward to simulate recoil (I will probably use the cheap heng Long recoil system even if they're crap, will see if I find something better !).

Nothing is aligned but you get the idea (the whole barrels arent permanently attached to allow the inner installation of the armature.)
