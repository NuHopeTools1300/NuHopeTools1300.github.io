Thank you very much! I'm so glad you enjoyed it! 

To answer your questions about what is holding it up...I am a drummer. There is one of my strongest cymbal stands under there. The model is entirely scratch built, and it is pretty heavy. 

My goal was to build it the same way the original filming model was, so I did it entirely by hand with no laser cutting. Even the large Plexiglas hull panels were cut using a glass cutter against a large metal ruler. I went through 2 boxes of X-acto blades to cut all the styrene. The size and proportions closely match the filming model, at least as close as one can get without actually measuring the real thing. However, I had some help in that area from the man who was in charge of building the real thing.

The display doubles as a traveling crate. I built it with a cradle inside to hold the model securely. There's enough foam padding the cradle to absorb the shock of the bumpiest roads.

To get back on topic, what I am planning is to build a display booth much like the one the Chicago Museum of Science and Industry built to display both the original Star Destroyer and my other replica. It too doubled as a traveling crate, as it was a touring exhibit.

If you want to see the original thread that discusses how I built it: http://www.therpf.com/f10/star-destroyer-3-a-116225/
