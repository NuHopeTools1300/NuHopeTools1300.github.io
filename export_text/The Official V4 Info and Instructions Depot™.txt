Ok so after a couple of weeks of recovery (knock on wood it stays that way)  ready to start an info thread on this.  This is the first casting that will be headed off to Jason who will take over this thread soon enough for a formal set of instructions.   For now just a few pics and some info on what has changed from the V3.1

So biggest change other than the reshaping of the upper fuselage is the addition of the "cheater"  open/close mechanism.  If your going to go all out and get a Mike Reader armature you will not need this.  Basically it's the 2 L bracket parts with a short aluminum armature pipe,  keeping the pipe hollow will allow for some wires if needed.  The L bracket assembly is cast in High Density Resin and should be more than strong enough.

Armature cover detail,  basically those funky detail parts that cover the ungly inner armature stuff.  Basically will be a "plug"  for when the wings are open.

For the first time that i know of,  and a huge thanks to Isel,  the correct phantom engine for Red 2 and Blue leader will be available,  also the short turkey feathers on that option for the first time from me.

As with the V3  you will be able to order the correct engine combo by indicating to me if you want Red 1-Red 5,  for those who don't know each one had a unique combo of the Sat V and phantom parts.  Ie  Red 2 has the 4 inner engines seen in grey in the pics,  Red 5 has 4 of the one in tan,  Red 2 has 4 Sat V cans with no bar,  Red 1 is 4x Sat V with the bar etc etc.

Not pictured yet but i will also offer up the gear down version of the v4,  fixed wing position closed with landing gear,  gear has metal cast into it.  I did a V3 that is esentially the same,  will post some pics later.

Sure i am forgetting something but will update as i go along.  Will start a JY thread soon.

thanks

mike

First ensure the supplied hinge assembly fits and operates within the body halves. You will need to sand the hinge halves themselves, and possibly trim the BOTTOM hull recess:

Looking awesome Jason,  thanks.  

First just want to address the panel line thing,  for some reason a few of the panel lines "grabbed"  when i pulled the pattern out of the molds.  It has been happening on a number of parts across a couple of different kits that i have poured molds for.   I have not changed the way i do panel line scribing,  so i don't know if it's a new formulation of the mold max 30 (it has happened thru at least 2 or 3 recent batches),  or some other weird thing.   I thought about making a new set of molds for the fuselage,  but since i have no way of telling if it would just happen again decided against it (these molds are big).   

If you go slow and use a reversed broken tip #11 exacto,  it should only take 10 minutes of clean up to fix.   I kind of hate sending out kits that need something like that,  but figured it would be ok.

I have lots and lots of stuff cast,  and will be preparing to send out kits starting next week.   

Had kind of a weird week again,  my oil tank started to leak a bit in my basement shop,  which is my scratchbuilding and mold making part of my shops.  Luckily i had tank insurance and they came by today and replaced the tank with no mishaps.   The bad  part was that i had to dismantle almost everything in my shop to make room for them to swap out the tanks,  so lots of chaos.   The good news is i can now think about a better lay out while everything is all apart anyway.

Hey Guys,   the wings have no real top/bottom up down orientation.  They were referenced from the pyro as far as the chip details go,  so like a lot of the "little"  details they are generic.

If you going to get seriously detailed about little stuff like that you will want to shave those off and match it to your Red # as closely as you want to.

It's the same with the fuselage, it has the pyro chip type detail.

It's the practical way to do it,  i can't have 4 sets of fuselage molds or 4 sets of wing molds to match the smaller details and keep the kit economically sound.

My suggestion for the little things like that is to just have fun with the kit,   a standard build of this kit nicely painted and weathered may not be a super accurate chip for chip replica but will build to a very respectable looking x wing,  and IMO i keep the price point low enough for a studio model that's something you should build and enjoy.  I know that is a controversial statement for the studio scale section in some ways but i am not getting any younger and i am trying to adopt that philosophy a bit for myself.

OK, so here are my results.  Since this is my first resin model, I'm asking questions and taking advice.

The resin "pad" under the part is about the thickness of a nickle.

[IMAGE: 2D78314A-15F1-4EC1-9D22-14E14CD56716_zpsxbressmn.jpg]

I rotated the part, face up on the belt sander feed table to remove most of the edge.

Next, I GENTLY held the back against the belt to remove the majority of the remaining pad.

There was a TINY gap under one edge of the part - I used this to gauge a little more light sanding on the belt sander.

[IMAGE: 37C44637-A02E-4A46-8DEB-ABE5DAB8AAF2_zpsj9gd8gug.jpg]

Finally, I cleaned up the whole part with 1000 grit, jeweler's files inside the "teeth" and a 5/16" drill bit through the center.

All together, about 10 minutes.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Ok,  for anyone who missed it,  the resin armature that i used did not live up to the promises of the material.  Meaning that the material was supposed to be warp resistant to like 112 degrees or something,  however some people who got the kit and built it have had problems with the armature drooping.  Not the wings but the L brackets on the armature.

The solution is this brace that i developed.  When the wings are open this jams into the armature supporting the wings and l brackets in the open position.  This should prevent the sag.

I am casting up a bunch of these now and will start sending them out as i can.

I am casting the armatures in a new resin that is supposed to be warp resistant to 150 degrees,  so that along with the brace should address the issue.

However i will say that there is really no substitute for an alluminum armature,  and i highly suggest getting one if you have a kit.  I do not charge much for the V4 by comparison to other x wings on the market or other resin kits for that matter.  The cost of the kit + an alluminum armature is still less than other typical resin kits.

If any one also wants the "old"  style V3 open wing brackets,  let me know.  You wings will be open all the time but those were bullet proof.
